Section 1:
Amendment No. 1 to HB6003
Mitchell
Signature of Sponsor
by adding the following as a new Section 9-4-217 in the amendatory language of SECTION 1:
9-4-217. Volunteer neighbor assistance fund.
(a) There is created a fund within the state treasury to be known as the volunteer
neighbor assistance fund. The fund consists of grants, appropriations by the general
assembly, federal funds, to the extent permitted by federal law and regulation, and any
other moneys made available to the Tennessee emergency management agency for the
purposes of such fund from any other source or sources. It is the legislative intent that
the fund also consists of fifty million dollars ($50,000,000) to be transferred from the
balance of the revenue fluctuation reserve.
(b) Moneys deposited in the fund must be invested for the benefit of the fund
pursuant to ยง 9-4-603.
(c) Except as provided in subsection (a), moneys in the fund must not revert to
the general fund, but must remain available to be used exclusively for the purpose
specified in this section.
(d) Subject to appropriations and the availability of funds, the Tennessee
emergency management agency shall use the volunteer neighbor assistance fund to
provide grants in the amount of ten thousand dollars ($10,000) to homeowners who
have lost or sustained damage to their homes as a result of a natural disaster.
(e) The Tennessee emergency management agency shall develop and publish
an application form for households to apply for grant funds. The application must require
a demonstration of need.

(f) The volunteer neighbor assistance fund is subject to examination and audit by
the comptroller of the treasury.
(g) If a person filing a claim or invoice for a grant or grant funds, including for
reimbursement, knowingly makes a false, fictitious, or fraudulent statement or
representation, or knowingly submits false, fictitious, or fraudulent documentation or
information to a county, the Tennessee emergency management agency, or the state
treasurer, then such person is liable under the False Claims Act, compiled in title 4,
chapter 18.
(h) Rules may be promulgated in accordance with the Uniform Administrative
Procedures Act, compiled in title 4, chapter 5, in order to ensure the funds are received
and expended for the purposes consistent with this section.
