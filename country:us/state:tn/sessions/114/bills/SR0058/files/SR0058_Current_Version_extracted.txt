SENATE RESOLUTION 58
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to congratulate Byron Conaway upon his
induction into the Tennessee Aviation Hall of
Fame.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
remarkable efforts, have distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all be
proud; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Byron Conaway of Savannah, who has
been inducted into the Tennessee Aviation Hall of Fame, a testament to the important role he
has played in the history and advancement of aviation in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, born and raised in Adamsville, Byron Conaway is a lifelong aviation
enthusiast and pilot, and he is the proud owner and operator of the Sibley Aviation Museum at
the Robert Sibley Airport in Selmer; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Conaway uses the museum to reach many youth in the area and
introduce them to aviation; in addition to educating them on this important and most interesting
topic, he has no doubt influenced the life paths of countless young Tennesseans; and
WHEREAS, he has also served his community as a philanthropist, supporting veterans
and underprivileged citizens in West Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, the success of this State, the strength of our communities, and the overall
vitality of American society depend, in great measure, upon the dedication of people such as
Byron Conaway, who use their considerable talents and resources to serve others and who
should be specially recognized for their public and community service; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we recognize and congratulate

Byron Conaway upon his induction into the Tennessee Aviation Hall of Fame, extending our
best wishes for much continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
