SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 190
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Sheriff Wayne M. Potter.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the untimely passing of Sheriff Wayne M. Potter; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Potter was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Oak Ridge on January 3, 1975, Wayne Potter grew up in Wartburg,
where he graduated from Wartburg Central High School, and he went on to attend Cleveland
State University, graduating from the Cleveland State University Law Enforcement Training
Academy; and
WHEREAS, he began his law enforcement career at the Brushy Mountain State
Penitentiary as a correctional officer in 1995, and in 1998, he joined the Morgan County Sheriff's
Office, serving as a correctional officer at the county jail; and
WHEREAS, ever eager for greater challenges, Sheriff Potter rose through the ranks of
the office, always securing the respect and admiration of his community and colleagues, and in
addition to serving with the Wartburg Police Department and the Oliver Springs Police
Department, he worked as a school resource officer, patrolman, and narcotics investigator,
displaying his wide range of capabilities and excellence; and
WHEREAS, in 2018, Sheriff Potter began his first term as sheriff of Morgan County after
his successful election, and after four years of decreasing crime, improving the office's
capacities for ensuring public safety, and further cementing the trust of his constituency, he was
reelected in 2022, in an uncontested election—a historic first in Morgan County; and

WHEREAS, his strength in the midst of the many trials of law enforcement always
originated in his unshakeable faith, which granted him the peace of a promised rest in eternity;
and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Potter was also deeply devoted to his family, spending time traveling
with them as often as he could, and he always endeavored to remain true to family values of the
highest order; and
WHEREAS, apart from his law enforcement work, Sheriff Potter provided for his family
by owning local businesses, including the Wartburg-based Blue Line Security, and through
property investment management; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his loving wife, Lea Potter; his children, Jesse Garcia,
Mary (Brandon) McCoy, Wendy Marie Potter (Hayden) Freels, Wayna Malea Potter, Wayla
Makaela Potter, and Wayne Potter, Jr.; his granddaughters, Adrianna Jessalynn Garcia and
Amaia Skye McCoy; and his siblings, Wendy "Missy" Potter (Douglas) Vespie and Walter (Lorie)
Potter; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Claude Luther "C.L." Potter and Nancy Justes
Potter, Sheriff Potter was preceded in death by his brother, William Michael "Porky" Potter; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Potter leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Sheriff Wayne M. Potter,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Sheriff Potter.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
