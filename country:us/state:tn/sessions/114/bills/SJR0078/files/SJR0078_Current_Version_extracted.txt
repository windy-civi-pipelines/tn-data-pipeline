SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 78
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to congratulate the Greene County Fair and its
board, staff, and volunteers upon being named
the 2024 Champion of Champions Fair by the
Tennessee Association of Fairs.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
those events that promote local business, entertain and educate our citizens, and celebrate the
history and culture of the great State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, the Greene County Fair is one such estimable event, which recently
received the most prestigious honor of being named the 2024 Champion of Champions Fair by
the Tennessee Association of Fairs; and
WHEREAS, among the many county, regional, and state awards that the Tennessee
Association of Fairs annually presents, the Champion of Champions accolade is the highest
honor possible, and the Greene County Fair previously received this distinction in 1963, 1988,
and 2005; and
WHEREAS, with the help of the community, volunteers devoted more than 8,000 work
hours to plan and execute the fair, which, in addition to hosting 32,000 attendees in 2024,
displayed more than 3,000 exhibits and featured woodcarving, quilting, and canning, in addition
to beef, dairy, chicken, and sheep shows; and
WHEREAS, the Greene County Fair Board President Rick Clark, who has served the fair
in numerous capacities since 1999, is to be greatly commended for his hard work and
dedication to making the fair the best in its classification; and
WHEREAS, the success of the Greene County Fair and its well-earned recognition bring
great pride to the people of Greene County, who every year unite with tireless dedication to

celebrate and promote local business, agriculture, and history with this time-honored event;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we congratulate the Greene County Fair and its
board, staff, and volunteers upon being named the 2024 Champion of Champions Fair by the
Tennessee Association of Fairs and extend to them our best wishes for much continued
success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
