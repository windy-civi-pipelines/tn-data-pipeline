SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 304
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Richard Clark on twenty
years of public service in law enforcement.
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is especially proud to be the home of so many men and
women who have performed above and beyond the call of duty in service of public safety; and
WHEREAS, thousands of Tennesseans continue the time-honored tradition of law
enforcement service, dedicating themselves to protecting and assisting their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Richard Clark is one such law enforcement professional, who contributed
greatly to public safety during his twenty-year career with three agencies; and
WHEREAS, from 2017 to 2024, Mr. Clark served as an officer with the Lebanon Police
Department (LPD), working at various times in field training, patrol, K-9, and public information;
and
WHEREAS, prior to his exemplary tenure with LPD, Richard Clark was a valuable officer
with the Metro Nashville Airport Department of Public Safety from 2004 to 2015, and then
served with the Greenbrier Police Department from 2015 to 2017; and
WHEREAS, he received numerous awards for saving lives during his career and was
named Employee of the Month at LPD in 2018, in addition to receiving a letter of commendation
from the department; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of McGavock High School, Mr. Clark earned his Emergency
Medical Technician-IV degree from Columbia State Community College; and
WHEREAS, his many accomplishments aside, Richard Clark is most appreciative of the
love and companionship he shares with his wife of fourteen years, Mikasha Clark, and their
children, Mila and Noelle; and

WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Richard Clark for his
dedication to law enforcement and the citizens of this State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Richard Clark on twenty
years in law enforcement, thank him for his service to his community, and extend to him our
best wishes for every future success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
