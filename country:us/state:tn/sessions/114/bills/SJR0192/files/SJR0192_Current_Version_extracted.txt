SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 192
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Priscilla Presley.
WHEREAS, it is the privilege of the members of this General Assembly to recognize
those Tennesseans who have made a lasting impact on this State through their extraordinary
achievements, dedication, and leadership; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Priscilla Presley, whose unwavering commitment to
preserving and promoting the legacy of Elvis Presley, Memphis music, and the State of
Tennessee and her various charitable endeavors can only be categorized as outstanding; and
WHEREAS, born in New York, Priscilla Presley spent her early years traveling with her
family, courtesy of her father's career as an Air Force officer; soon after settling in Del Valle,
Texas, the family was transferred to Wiesbaden, West Germany, where Priscilla Beaulieu met
her future husband, Elvis Presley, in 1960; and
WHEREAS, the couple eventually married in 1967, and their only child, Lisa Marie
Presley, was born in 1968; despite their divorce in 1973, Priscilla and Elvis Presley maintained a
close relationship until his untimely death in 1977; and
WHEREAS, following the passing of Elvis's father, Vernon Presley, Ms. Presley took on
the role of co-executor of Elvis Presley's estate, demonstrating remarkable tenacity and
leadership; and
WHEREAS, Priscilla Presley faced immense pressure from legal advisors to sell
Graceland, yet she stood firm in her conviction, declaring, "That will never happen. We're not
selling Graceland," and set forth on a mission to preserve and enhance the legacy of the
Presley family; and

WHEREAS, her diligent efforts, research, and determination led to a successful
partnership with Jack Soden and the subsequent transformation of Graceland into a world-
renowned historic landmark and tourist destination; and
WHEREAS, aside from the quest to save Graceland, Priscilla Presley continued her own
career and entrepreneurship; in 1980, her love of animals and commitment to their protection
led her to host Those Amazing Animals, a unique and educational television show that served
as a precursor to today's Animal Planet; and
WHEREAS, in 1983, Ms. Presley took on the role of Jenna Wade in the popular CBS
evening television drama Dallas, becoming an instant audience favorite and one of the show's
most popular leading ladies, seen by over 300 million viewers worldwide each week; and
WHEREAS, Priscilla Presley displayed her comedic talents in the 1988 box office smash
The Naked Gun: From the Files of Police Squad and reprised her role in The Naked Gun 2Â½
and The Naked Gun 33 1/3: The Final Insult; she also starred in the films Breakfast with
Einstein and Haley Wagner Star and guest-starred on television's Melrose Place, Touched by
an Angel, and Spin City; and
WHEREAS, for ten years, Ms. Presley starred on British stages with acting legend
Warwick Davis in pantomimes, including Aladdin and Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, with
her favorite role being the Wicked Witch in Snow White; and
WHEREAS, her business acumen has been proven through successful entrepreneurial
ventures in linens, fragrances, film and music production, and public speaking, in addition to her
ongoing dedication to Graceland; and
WHEREAS, Graceland was prepared for public opening with a $560,000 investment,
which was recouped only thirty-eight days after its opening on June 7, 1982; in the years since,
Graceland has attracted over 22 million guests to Memphis, becoming the second-most visited
- 2 - 004152
residence in the United States (after the White House) and creating an economic impact of over
$6 billion since its opening; and
WHEREAS, through the efforts of Priscilla Presley, Graceland was listed in the National
Register of Historic Places in 1991 and was designated a National Historic Landmark in 2006;
she has worked tirelessly to preserve Graceland Mansion, its grounds, and countless artifacts
belonging to Elvis Presley, ensuring its enduring popularity and significance; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Presley's dedication to preserving Graceland and promoting it globally
for the past forty years has firmly established Memphis as the world's preeminent rock and roll
mecca, with Graceland serving as a gateway to the rich musical heritage of Memphis; and
WHEREAS, the music that emanated out of Memphis, including that of Elvis Presley,
changed the planet, with curators of the Smithsonian Institution noting that the world learned the
English language by singing the lyrics of songs by Elvis and other musicians from Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Presley has served as an international ambassador for Elvis, Memphis,
and Memphis music, promoting projects, opening exhibits, and enhancing global recognition of
Memphis's musical heritage; and
WHEREAS, in 2018, she produced the critically acclaimed HBO documentary Elvis
Presley: The Searcher, which artistically narrated Elvis's life through his music; and
WHEREAS, with the release of Baz Luhrmann's movie Elvis in 2022, Priscilla Presley
traveled to several premieres and international events, further promoting the legacy of Elvis and
Memphis music and contributing to exhibitions and performances globally; and
WHEREAS, in 2023, the biographical drama film Priscilla, co-produced by Priscilla
Presley and based on her memoir Elvis and Me, continued to highlight her impactful life and
career; and
WHEREAS, Priscilla Presley continues to advocate for animal protection charities and
serves as spokesperson for the Dream Foundation; she has given her time and support at the
- 3 - 004152
Tennessee Capitol for the expansion of exhibits and the educational mission of the
Smithsonian-curated Memphis Rock 'n' Soul Museum to relocate to a prominent centerstage in
the historic Lansky building on the iconic Beale Street, further contributing to the preservation
and future of Memphis's rich musical heritage; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that, more than six decades after Elvis Presley was
honored with the Governor's Colonel Aide-de-Camp award, the highest civilian honor in the
State of Tennessee, in March 1961, we are pleased to honor Ms. Priscilla Beaulieu Presley,
another Tennessee treasure, upon her receipt of the Colonel Aide-de-Camp award for her
outstanding contributions and unwavering dedication to preserving and promoting the legacy of
Elvis Presley, Memphis music, and the State of Tennessee and applaud her distinguished
career in the entertainment industry as we recognize March as Women's History Month.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
