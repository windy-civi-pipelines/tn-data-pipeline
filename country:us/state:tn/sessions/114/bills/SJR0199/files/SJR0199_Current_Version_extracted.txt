SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 199
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Cecil Renner on the
occasion of her 103rd birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to those citizens who are celebrating
special occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Cecil Renner celebrated her 103rd birthday on January 23, 2025, a
milestone commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Renner has lived an amazing life and has experienced firsthand the
breadth and span of American history; and
WHEREAS, born in the Cedar Creek community of Greeneville on January 23, 1922,
Cecil Renner is the daughter of Sherman and Lillie Mae Bowers Hensley; she had two sisters,
Lavice King and June Waddell, and two brothers, C.C. Hensley and J.U. Hensley; and
WHEREAS, in the 1940s, Mrs. Renner and her husband, Robert, moved away from
Greene County, but shortly after her husband's passing, she returned home to be near her
sisters; and
WHEREAS, Cecil Renner has been blessed with two children, Earl Renner and Vickie
(Mark) Schmitmeyer; two grandchildren, Jenny and Matthew; and one great-grandson, Billy; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Renner loves animals and is known for her heart of gold and infectious
smile; and
WHEREAS, she remains a beloved member of her community who is admired for her
faith, integrity, and enthusiasm for life; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
SJR0199
004198
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Cecil Renner on the
celebration of her 103rd birthday and extend to her our best wishes for many happy returns of
the day.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
