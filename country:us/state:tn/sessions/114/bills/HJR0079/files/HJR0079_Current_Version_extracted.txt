HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 79
By Gillespie
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of William Reid Sanders,
Sr., of Memphis.
WHEREAS, it was with great sadness that the members of this General Assembly
learned of the passing of William Reid Sanders, Sr., a lifelong Memphian; and
WHEREAS, a loyal friend on the personal level, Mr. Sanders was also an active
philanthropist and a pillar of his community; and
WHEREAS, he evidenced his zest for life as "camp counselor" for the many excursions
he organized for friends and family and through his deep love of animals, particularly his pets,
Max and Gus; and
WHEREAS, possessing a deep generosity of spirit and a contagious optimism, Reid
Sanders was ever ready to lend a helping hand in matters both big and small, and he always
saw the best in others; and
WHEREAS, a gentleman of keen business acumen, Mr. Sanders remained humble
despite his many successes in life; and
WHEREAS, because of his reputation as a founder and Executive Vice President of
Southeastern Asset Management and President of Longleaf Partners Funds, he was frequently
asked to share his advice and counsel as a board member for public and private companies;
and
WHEREAS, after his retirement from Southeastern, Reid Sanders engaged in numerous
ventures, including the founding of Sanders Properties; and
WHEREAS, because of his talent, integrity, and generosity, he was highly sought after
as a director/trustee and benefactor of numerous nonprofit institutions, including the Jefferson

Scholars Foundation at the University of Virginia, Rhodes College, the Hugo Dixon Foundation,
The Dixon Gallery and Gardens, and the Campbell Clinic Foundation; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Sanders was especially proud of the title "Honorary Alumna" conferred
upon him for his extraordinary leadership as Chairman of the Board of Trustees for Hutchison
School, a Memphis girls' school that is the alma mater of his wife and daughter; and
WHEREAS, he was a member of the Memphis Hunt and Polo Club; The Memphis
Country Club, which he served as President; and Yeamans Hall Club; he enjoyed attending
Memphis Tigers basketball games and visiting his children in Charleston, South Carolina; and
WHEREAS, Reid Sanders attended Presbyterian Day School, Memphis University
School, Central High School, and the University of Virginia, where he was a member of Sigma
Alpha Epsilon fraternity; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Sanders is survived by his best friend and wife of forty years, Christeve
Robinson Sanders; daughter, Christeve Aubrey Sanders (Jack Davis); son, William Reid (Lydia)
Sanders, Jr.; half-sister, Jean Sanders (Bob) Kirkpatrick; and many nieces, nephews, and
godchildren; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his father, Edward Harvey Sanders, Sr.;
mother, Mimi Reid Sanders Smith; stepfather, Dr. Hugh Smith; brother, George Clarke Sanders;
half-brother, Edward Harvey Sanders, Jr.; half-sister, Normi Sanders (Tim) Geske; and step-
sisters, Mimi Smith (Albert) Mallory and Nancy Smith (David) Johnston; and
WHEREAS, a man who relished the opportunity to bring happiness to others, Mr.
Sanders had a profound impact in Memphis, and he will be sorely missed by his family, friends,
and the community at large; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
- 2 - 002446
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of William Reid Sanders, Sr., of Memphis, reflecting
fondly upon his life of purpose and commitment to the greater good.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our condolences to Mr. Sanders's family
and many friends.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
