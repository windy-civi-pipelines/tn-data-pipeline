SENATE RESOLUTION 15
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION to recognize Dr. Nakeisha Owens-Griffin for her
honorable and astute service to the people of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this legislative body should recognize those
estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to improving the education and well-
being of our youth; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Nakeisha Owens-Griffin is one such noteworthy person, who brings
more than twenty-seven years of combined education and advocacy experience to her role as
an education consultant for the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Owens-Griffin has served as a middle school teacher for eight years, an
adjunct professor at Arkansas State University for over ten years, and an adjunct professor for
the HBCU Shorter College and Christian Brothers University; and
WHEREAS, she leverages her vast experience to train school districts, teachers, and
administrators in more than 212 schools and community agencies and serves as a special
education supervisor and compliance monitor for Department of Education nonpublic schools;
and
WHEREAS, an esteemed professional, Dr. Nakeisha Owens-Griffin completed her
doctoral degree in higher and adult education and has received recognition for her outstanding
service to the 800 to 1,100 youth in Department of Children's Services custody in Shelby
County; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Owens-Griffin is wholly dedicated to ensuring that children in foster care
receive resources, equity, and advocacy; and

WHEREAS, through her Operation Sleep and Snacks service project, Dr. Owens-Griffin
and three other leaders of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority chapters raised over $10,000 worth of
sleep items and snacks in one day for foster youth sleeping in office buildings in 2023; and
WHEREAS, a respected and trusted leader, Dr. Nakeisha Owens-Griffin is a proud
charter member of the Alpha Alpha Gamma Omega Chapter of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority,
Inc., which she currently serves as president; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Owens-Griffin is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public
service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and she should be
specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we recognize and commend Dr.
Nakeisha Owens-Griffin for her honorable and astute service to the people of this State and
wish her all the best in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
