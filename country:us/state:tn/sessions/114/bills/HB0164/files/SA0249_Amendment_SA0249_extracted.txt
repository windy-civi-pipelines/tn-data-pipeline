Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-4-101(a)(1), is amended by
adding the following language as a new subdivision:
( ) "Animal chiropractic":
(i) Means the evaluation and treatment of a non-human's vertebral or
extremity joint dysfunction through spinal, joint, or musculoskeletal manipulative
therapy and soft tissue therapy; and
(ii) Does not include:
(a) Performing surgery;
(b) Dispensing or administering of medications; or
(c) Performing veterinary medicine, as defined in ยง 63-12-103,
other than the evaluation and treatment of a non-human's vertebral or
extremity joint dysfunction through spinal, joint, or musculoskeletal
manipulative therapy and soft tissue therapy;

Section 3:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 63, Chapter 4, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) The board shall adopt rules, pursuant to the Uniform Administrative
Procedures Act, compiled in title 4, chapter 5, for:
(1) Regulating the performance of animal chiropractic by a chiropractic
physician;

(2) Establishing educational standards for certification of a chiropractic
physician to perform animal chiropractic. The educational standards must
include obtaining a certificate in animal chiropractic from the American Veterinary
Chiropractic Association; and
(3) Establishing health and sanitation standards for a facility where a
chiropractic physician performs animal chiropractic and human patients are
treated.
(b) A chiropractic physician may apply to the board for certification to perform
animal chiropractic. An applicant for certification must:
(1) Provide the board with proof, satisfactory to the board, that the
applicant meets the educational standards established pursuant to subdivision
(a)(2); and
(2) Meet any other requirements for certification established by the board
pursuant to subdivision (a)(1).
(c) The board shall maintain on its website a list of chiropractic physicians who
are certified in good standing to perform animal chiropractic.

Section 4:
SECTION 3. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-4-112(c)(3), is amended by
designating the existing language as subdivision (c)(3)(A) and adding the following as a new
subdivision:
(B) In addition to the chiropractic education required pursuant to subdivisions
(c)(2) and (c)(3)(A), a chiropractic physician who is certified to perform animal
chiropractic shall annually complete a minimum of six (6) additional hours of continuing
education in animal chiropractic that are approved by the board.

Section 5:
SECTION 4. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-4-122, is amended by adding the
following as a new subsection:

(1) Except as provided in subdivision (c)(2), it is a violation of this chapter
for a person or business entity to advertise that the person or business entity
provides chiropractic care, chiropractic services, or chiropractic therapy unless
that care, service, or therapy is provided by or under the direction of a licensed
chiropractic physician.
(2) It is not a violation of this chapter for a licensed veterinarian, as
defined in ยง 63-12-103, who is certified pursuant to SECTION 6, to advertise that
the licensed veterinarian is certified to provide animal chiropractic.
(3) This subsection (c) does not restrict a person licensed or certified
under this title, or other law, from engaging in the profession for which that
person is licensed or certified; provided, the person does not advertise that the
person is a chiropractic physician or a provider of chiropractic care, services, or
therapy.

the following language as a new subdivision:

vertebral or extremity joint dysfunction through spinal, joint, or musculoskeletal
manipulative therapy and soft tissue therapy;
