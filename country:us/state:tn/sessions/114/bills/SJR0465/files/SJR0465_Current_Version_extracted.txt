SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 465
By Briggs
A RESOLUTION to honor and recognize Jack Magee, 2025
Valedictorian of Christian Academy of Knoxville.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to pay
tribute to those students who, through their natural talents and unflagging commitment to
academic excellence, have distinguished themselves in the classroom; and
WHEREAS, Jack Magee is one such exceptional student, who was recently named
Valedictorian of the 2025 graduating class at Christian Academy of Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, through diligent study, steady composure during examinations, and
steadfast commitment of time and talent, Mr. Magee has achieved outstanding academic
success and joined the ranks of the best and brightest students in Tennessee and our country;
and
WHEREAS, in his remarkable high school career, Mr. Magee has earned an exemplary
grade point average of 4.46 while participating in numerous extracurricular activities, including
acting as a teen advisor and being a member of the marching band, the National Honor Society,
the National English Honor Society, the Scholars' Bowl, and the student Senate; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Magee is no stranger to accolades, and his impressive performance in
all his chosen endeavors has accrued him such notable honors as serving as the National
Honor Society chapter president, earning the Leonard Bernstein Award, and being named a
National Merit Finalist, a National Honor Society Scholarship semifinalist, an AP Scholar with
Honor, and an All-State Band member; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Magee has also generously given of his time to teach drum lessons to
younger students, acting as an exemplary mentor among his peers; and

WHEREAS, his display of intellectual aptitude and ambition surely bodes well for his
future success, as he goes on to study neuroscience and radiology and continues to follow his
passion for music; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Magee's noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for
his parents, Brandi Magee and Matt Magee, his school, and his community; and
WHEREAS, Jack Magee's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of the respect,
admiration, and commendation of the members of this General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend and honor the 2025 Christian
Academy of Knoxville Valedictorian, Jack Magee, on his impressive academic achievement and
extend to him our best wishes for a happy, successful future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
