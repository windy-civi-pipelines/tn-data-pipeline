SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 225
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor William "Bill" Clabough,
Sr., for his contributions to the Foothills Land
Conservancy.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all
be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is William "Bill" Clabough, Sr., whose leadership and
tireless efforts in support of the Foothills Land Conservancy can only be categorized as
outstanding; and
WHEREAS, through the tremendous investment and leadership of Bill Clabough over
the last twenty years, the Foothills Land Conservancy (FLC) has assisted in the land
preservation of more than 200,000 acres that include 500 conservation projects in Tennessee
and six surrounding states; and
WHEREAS, FLC endeavors to protect, preserve, and enhance the lands and
environment of the Southern Appalachian region and also to promote the character of the land
for the benefit of the general public, now and in the future; in 2025, FLC will be celebrating its
fortieth year of service as a regional land trust; and
WHEREAS, a Blount Countian through and through, Mr. Clabough was born in Maryville
and graduated from the Blount County School system and the University of Tennessee,
Knoxville, where he received a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration; he spent
more than thirty years in the retail grocery business before serving as an elected representative
in both the Tennessee House of Representatives and Senate; and
SJR0225
005108
- 1 -
WHEREAS, Bill Clabough became FLC's executive director in March 2006; his passion
lies in moving FLC forward on land protection partnerships, land stewardship, and community
outreach, and he is a hands-on leader for all the projects and initiatives the organization
manages; and
WHEREAS, within the State of Tennessee, and almost exclusively during Mr.
Clabough's tenure, FLC's land conservation projects grew to span fifty-two counties and over
250 landowner partnerships, with more than 100,000 acres preserved in total; and
WHEREAS, he has also assisted in the permanent conservation of Blount County's
beloved Camp Montvale, seven Tennessee Century Farms, Jarvis Park in Blount County, an
additional 1,000 acres of land transferred to the Mount Roosevelt State Wildlife Management
Area in Roane County, and 649 acres of land in Polk County, which was transferred to the
Tennessee Wildlife Resources Agency; and
WHEREAS, in recent years, Bill Clabough has worked with FLC's board and staff to
create a permanent foundation for planned gifts that includes land acquisition and stewardship
programs, the Land Preservation Fund, and other conservation-related projects; and
WHEREAS, in 2010, FLC completed the 25 in 25 Initiative, a campaign created and
launched by Mr. Clabough in October 2007; the public awareness campaign set an
organizational goal to preserve a total of 25,000 East Tennessee acres by 2010 to mark the
twenty-fifth anniversary of FLC; and
WHEREAS, at the time, FLC had 17,000 acres protected through land donations and
conservation easements but wanted to engage the community in the coming years and bring
awareness of its mission of land conservation to East Tennessee residents and families;
through the efforts of FLC's staff and board, local media coverage, and partnerships with the
Little River Watershed Association and Legacy Parks Foundation, FLC was able to accomplish
its preservation goal in December 2009; and
- 2 - 005108
WHEREAS, Bill Clabough was a dynamic participant in the legislative process during his
time in the General Assembly, earning the respect and admiration of his colleagues for his
outstanding contributions as vice chairman of the Commerce, Labor and Agriculture Committee
and as secretary of the Finance, Ways and Means Committee; and
WHEREAS, he also served with distinction as treasurer of the Senate Republican
Caucus during the 101st General Assembly and was elected Chairman of the Senate
Republican Caucus during the 102nd General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clabough was duly honored as Legislator of the Year by the Tennessee
Firemen's Association, and he has ably served the citizens of Blount County as a board
chairman of CBBC Bank, former board member of the Great Smoky Mountains Park
Commission, and member of the Blount County Commission, Blount Memorial Hospital, and the
Boys and Girls Club of Blount County; he is also a former member of the Blount County
Families First Council and a participant in Leadership Blount and Leadership Sevier County;
and
WHEREAS, Bill Clabough also served his community as a member of Kiwanis, Farm
Bureau, Trout Unlimited, and the Blount County, Sevierville, and Gatlinburg chambers of
commerce; he has also served as a volunteer with the Great Smoky Mountains National Park
and Appalachian Trails Conservancy; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clabough, along with the leadership, board, and staff of FLC, works
diligently to protect, preserve, and enhance the lands and environment of the Southern
Appalachian region, and he is most worthy of our approbation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor William "Bill" Clabough, Sr.,
- 3 - 005108
for his many contributions to the Foothills Land Conservancy and extend to him our best wishes
for much continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 4 - 005108
