SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 155
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor Bob Kesling on the occasion of his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
careers; and
WHEREAS, Bob Kesling is one such estimable individual, who, after more than twenty-
five years of service as the Voice of the Vols on the Vol Radio Network, will take his much-
deserved retirement this year; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Kesling is a renowned voice in the broadcasting industry, and his
outstanding tenure and unwavering commitment to Tennessee sports have made him the
longest-serving member of the Vol Network's seventy-five-year history; and
WHEREAS, Bob Kesling began his association with the University of Tennessee,
Knoxville, as a walk-on fullback on the 1972 freshman football team; he began his career in
broadcasting two years later when he was hired by John Ward as a film editor for the Bill Battle
Show on the Vol Television Network; and
WHEREAS, in 1976, Mr. Kesling was hired to serve as Mr. Ward's spotter for Vol
Network radio broadcasts, and he sat beside the legendary Voice of the Vols in the booth for
fifteen seasons, spotting and handling pre-game, halftime, and post-game interviews; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Kesling graduated from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, in 1977
and served as the radio play-by-play voice of the Lady Vol basketball team from 1978 until 1999
and called Pat Summitt's first six NCAA National Championships; and

003513

WHEREAS, on July 1, 1999, Bob Kesling was officially named the Voice of the Vols on
the Vol Radio Network, one of the largest networks in collegiate sports with more than sixty
stations, in which position he proudly served as the play-by-play voice for Tennessee football
and basketball; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Kesling represented Tennessee athletics in various roles, including the
Vol Network Director of Broadcasting; he hosted Vol Calls, the Big Orange Hotline, the Josh
Heupel Show, and the Rick Barnes Show; and
WHEREAS, Bob Kesling previously served as sports director of WIVK Radio and as
sports director of WBIR-TV, where he also worked for Jefferson-Pilot Sports on its SEC
Television package; he served as both pre-game host and play-by-play announcer for the
basketball network and sideline reporter and play-by-play announcer for the football broadcasts;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Kesling broadcasted various sporting events for CBS/Westwood One
Radio, Fox Sports, Fox SportSouth, and the SEC Network, in addition to calling games for the
Knoxville Cherokees hockey team, the Knoxville Blue Jays baseball team, and the Nashville
Sounds baseball team; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Bob Kesling is a four-time recipient of
the Sportscaster of the Year award; he was inducted into the Tennessee Radio Hall of Fame in
2024, a most prestigious honor that recognizes his enduring legacy in the broadcasting industry,
and the Greater Knoxville Sports Hall of Fame in 2015; and
WHEREAS, a highly respected member of his community, Bob Kesling served on the
board of directors for several organizations, including Goodwill, the Leukemia and Lymphoma
Society, and the Knoxville Sports Hall of Fame; he also served as chairperson of the 2013
United Way campaign; and
- 2 - 003513
WHEREAS, his many career accomplishments aside, Mr. Kesling is fortunate to share
love and companionship with his wife, Tami, and their two daughters, Allison and Melissa; and
WHEREAS, for five decades, Bob Kesling has served as a cornerstone of the Vol
Network, demonstrating the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, and winning the
unbridled respect and admiration of his colleagues and community alike; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Kesling exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean, and he should be specially recognized; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Bob Kesling upon his
storied broadcasting career and service as the Voice of the Vols, extending to him our heartfelt
wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
