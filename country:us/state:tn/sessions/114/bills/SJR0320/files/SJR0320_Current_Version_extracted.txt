SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 320
By Campbell
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Dr. Heather Lynn
Webber for her dedicated service as a teacher.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those outstanding teachers who have devoted their careers to providing the
highest-quality education for Tennessee's students; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Heather Lynn Webber is one such estimable educator, who has
distinguished herself as a professional of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Webber has demonstrated an unwavering commitment to education
throughout her decade-long service as a seventh-grade science teacher at the University
School of Nashville, where she has touched the lives of countless students and instilled in them
a deep love for science and learning; and
WHEREAS, the daughter of Dr. Bart Campbell, a highly respected figure in the
community, and Audrey Campbell, a renowned poet, playwright, and actress, Dr. Heather Lynn
Webber is the sister of Brent Campbell and our esteemed colleague Senator Heidi Campbell;
and
WHEREAS, Dr. Heather Lynn Webber is a graduate of both the University of Tennessee
Medical School and Brown University and brings a wealth of expertise to the classroom,
particularly in science and sex education, with her background as an obstetrician-gynecologist;
and
WHEREAS, her many accomplishments aside, Dr. Webber is most grateful for the love
and support she shares with her husband, Dr. Angus Webber, and their two sons, Connor

(Schyler) Webber and Blair Webber; she is also a proud grandmother, affectionately known as
Di Di to her granddaughter, Margot Ochiltree Webber; and
WHEREAS, a dedicated naturalist, Dr. Heather Lynn Webber shares her love of nature
and animals with all who know her, and she is a devoted mother to numerous poodles; and
WHEREAS, she is also a beloved member of the Nashville theater community, an
accomplished actress, and an active and generous friend and community member known for
her beautiful voice, contagious wit, and colorful costumes; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Webber's brilliant and creative approach to teaching has been an
inspiration to her colleagues and students alike, and her friendship and counsel are highly
valued; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Heather Lynn Webber's contributions as a teacher, mentor, and
community leader have left an indelible mark on the University School of Nashville and beyond,
and as she embarks on the next chapter of her life, we wish her continued success, happiness,
and fulfillment; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Dr. Heather Lynn Webber
for her outstanding service as a seventh-grade science teacher at the University School of
Nashville, commend her exceptional service to the children of Tennessee, salute her
professional expertise and personal excellence, and wish her all the best in her future
endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
