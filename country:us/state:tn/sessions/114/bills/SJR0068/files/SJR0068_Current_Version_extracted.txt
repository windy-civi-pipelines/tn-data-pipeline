SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 68
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to recognize Chris Cutshaw on the occasion of
his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm
throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Hamblen County Mayor Chris Cutshaw is one such estimable person, who
served for over forty years at Morristown Utilities, from which he recently retired; and
WHEREAS, throughout his exemplary tenure of more than four decades, Mayor
Cutshaw has distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the highest order and as an
exceptional asset to his community; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Cutshaw began his career with Morristown Utilities on June 4, 1984,
and he served the Lakeway area first as a meter reader and then as lead electrician and
lineman apprentice before his final role as journeyman lead lineman; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Cutshaw's knowledge, experience, and leadership roles have
prepared him well for his service as Hamblen County mayor; and
WHEREAS, a strong advocate for the citizens of Hamblen County, Mayor Chris Cutshaw
is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that have earned Tennessee
recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend Chris Cutshaw upon the occasion of
his retirement from Morristown Utilities and extend to him our best wishes for continuing
success in his role as mayor.
SJR0068
002353
- 1 -
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
