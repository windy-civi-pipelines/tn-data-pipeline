SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 509
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Tracy Solomon of
Greeneville upon his receipt of the Veteran
Owned Small Business of the Year award.
WHEREAS, small businesses are the backbone of Tennessee's economy, and the
goods and services provided by their diligent proprietors contribute significantly to the overall
well-being of the citizens of this State; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable small business owner is Tracy Solomon of Greeneville,
owner of TEVET, who was awarded the Veteran Owned Small Business of the Year award by
the United States Small Business Administration's Tennessee District Office; and
WHEREAS, a proud military veteran, Mr. Solomon followed his family's tradition and
joined the United States Navy, where he worked to support F-14 Tomcat squadrons, gaining
hands-on experience with complex systems and electronics; and
WHEREAS, after completing his military service, he transitioned to the private sector
and ultimately used a second mortgage to launch TEVET, which is the leading federal reseller
of engineering equipment and supplies and a premier provider of cutting-edge solutions in
technology and defense, specializing in providing Test and Measurement equipment and
software for government agencies and their prime contractors; and
WHEREAS, the company's success has allowed Mr. Solomon and his team to give back
to their community through support for a variety of organizations, including the Holston Home for
Children, Disabled American Veterans, Capitol Theatre of Greeneville, local JROTC programs,
and veterans' groups; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we join in acknowledging this exceptional Tennessee
businessman; now, therefore,
SJR0509
007974
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we congratulate Tracy Solomon upon his receipt of
the Veteran Owned Small Business of the Year award from the U.S. Small Business
Administration's Tennessee District Office, extending our best wishes for much continued
success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
