SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 218
By Haile
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Sumner County
Museum on the occasion of its fiftieth
anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize and
commend those institutions that have contributed significantly to the cultural heritage and
historical preservation of our great State; and
WHEREAS, the Sumner County Museum was founded in 1975 through the vision and
leadership of John Garrott, Robert Ramsey, and 115 incorporators, who sought to preserve and
celebrate the rich history of Sumner County; and
WHEREAS, the museum has remained steadfast in its mission to make the history of
Sumner County meaningful and accessible to a broad audience by collecting, studying,
exhibiting, and interpreting through educational programming, special exhibits, publications, and
research; and
WHEREAS, the Sumner County Museum's extensive collections—including exhibits on
Randy's Record Shop, Native American history, agriculture, wartime contributions, nineteenth-
century home life, entertainment and music, transportation, textiles, blacksmithing, tinsmithing,
and gunsmithing—serve as invaluable resources in preserving the county's heritage; and
WHEREAS, the museum's commitment to education is exemplified by the Hidden
History Passport and Traveling Trunk programs, which bring history to life for students and the
wider community; and
WHEREAS, the museum has further engaged the community by hosting significant
annual events such as the Candlelight Cemetery Tour, Night at the Museum, Historic Haunts

Bus Tour, and Songs and Stories, each of which provides unique opportunities for historical
learning and cultural enrichment; and
WHEREAS, the Sumner County Museum has forged strong partnerships with Sumner
County Schools; Sumner County Government; the City of Gallatin; the City of Hendersonville;
the Gallatin, Portland, and White House chambers of commerce; Volunteer State Community
College; the Sumner County Historical Society; Sumner County Tourism; and the Sumner
County Historic Sites Collaborative, thereby strengthening historical awareness and community
bonds; and
WHEREAS, the museum has contributed significantly to the local economy by drawing
visitors, supporting community engagement, and generating occupancy taxes that bolster local
tourism initiatives; and
WHEREAS, under the exemplary leadership of Executive Director Ryan Baker, along
with the dedication of the board of directors, staff, and volunteers, the Sumner County Museum
has continued to thrive and uphold its mission of historical preservation and education; and
WHEREAS, we are proud to commend the museum upon its fiftieth-anniversary
celebration at the Barn Dance, an event that reflects the deep musical and cultural heritage of
Sumner County and serves as a fitting tribute to the museum's enduring impact; and
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Sumner County
Museum on the occasion of its fiftieth anniversary, celebrate its many contributions to the
heritage of our State, and extend our best wishes for continued success in the years to come.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
