HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 490
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Terran Jerrell "T-RAN"
Gilbert.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we acknowledge and applaud those business and
cultural leaders who are wholly devoted to enhancing the quality of life for their fellow citizens
and improving and enriching the place they all call home; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy individual is Terran Jerrell Gilbert, known
professionally as T-RAN, who has distinguished himself as a citizen of the greatest dedication,
ability, and integrity and has served as a history-maker who epitomizes the spirit and
commitment that are characteristic of a true Tennessee Ambassador; and
WHEREAS, T-RAN is a singer, songwriter, record producer, musician, music video
director, actor, motivational speaker, entrepreneur, and philanthropist, as well as the founder of
the entertainment company 22Visionz; T-Ran was born on December 7, 1984, in Chattanooga
to former Chattanooga City Councilman Russell J. Gilbert, Sr., and community leader and
educator Terri A. Gilbert; and
WHEREAS, as a musical artist, T-RAN has toured in ten countries and three continents;
he has shared the stage with the likes of Kirk Franklin, Lyfe Jennings, Shania Twain, Monica,
and Kool & the Gang, and he was featured on TBN with rapper/host T-Bone as his song charted
in the TX10 Countdown. His albums, Live and Not Die and The Imprint, have received airplay
worldwide; and
WHEREAS, as an actor, he has appeared in the films Straight into Darkness and
Unconditional, as well as in episodes of Nashville, and in 2012, he took his music and
motivational speaking on the Season of Non-Violence Tour, with Dr. Arun Gandhi, grandson of
Mahatma Gandhi; and

WHEREAS, T-RAN's philanthropic endeavors have included many benefit concerts over
the years, such as his 2012 concert with gospel artist Gordon Mote and the celebrity benefit
concert in Los Angeles for Homeboys Industries, which benefited programs for gang-involved
and previously incarcerated men and women; and
WHEREAS, in 2014, T-RAN sponsored a benefit concert for the Nashville-based
nonprofit Nashville Peacemakers, an organization fighting against human trafficking, youth
violence, and illiteracy; he also performed at the IndieCares Concert Series sponsored by
Nashville Inner City Ministries, benefiting needy families, and in 2016, he teamed up with
Unchained Movement and Nashville Peacemakers in promoting human trafficking awareness
with the use of the video for his song "Nowhere"; and
WHEREAS, the founder of the entertainment company 22Visionz, he is a member of
various key music organizations, actively contributing to the recognition and celebration of
musical excellence; he is an esteemed voting member of the Academy of Country Music,
Gospel Music Association, Country Music Association, and Recording Academy; and
WHEREAS, his impactful work has garnered recognition in People Magazine for his
song and music video "Other Side," performed by Taylor Sanders, which he wrote, produced,
and directed; T-RAN's songs "Overcomer" and "Save Me" have also positively impacted others
in the music industry, such as Grammy-award winning platinum artist NE-YO; and
WHEREAS, as a pastor and spiritual counselor through his nonprofit organization Live
And Not Die, Inc., T-RAN provides spiritual support to prominent figures in the music industry
and has been a source of inspiration and guidance; he has also profoundly strengthened the
faith of countless community members, standing as a beacon of light and hope, and praying for
everyone he encounters; and
WHEREAS, he provides solace and comfort by singing at funeral services, offering hope
to grieving families and friends, including community members he has never met; he has

organized community outreach events, delivering essential items to the homeless and families
in need, and has, through his music and motivational speaking, provided spiritual support to
college students at various universities, colleges, and HBCUs; and
WHEREAS, Terran "T-RAN" Gilbert graduated from Lee University with a Bachelor of
Science degree in Telecommunications in 2007, and in 2008, he launched 22Visionz, LLC, a
production, management, and entertainment company specializing in video production,
marketing, music production, record-label services, graphic and logo design, motion graphics,
and live events; and
WHEREAS, T-RAN was recently honored for his video production work, claiming Music
Video of the Year (Female) at the 2024 Josie Music Awards for Taylor Sanders's "Firecracker"
video, which he directed; and
WHEREAS, he has also been recognized by his hometown of Chattanooga with a key to
the city, and in 2021, he was honored by the Chattanooga City Council for being a "Heroic and
Selfless" individual in the community; and
WHEREAS, he is wholly devoted to his family, including his wife, Alexis Rosez Gilbert,
and their children, Amor√© Rose Gilbert and Ayla Rain Gilbert; and
WHEREAS, T-RAN is an ambassador of the music industry and entertainment for the
State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud T-RAN for his dedication
to the entertainment industry and the State of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend Terran Jerrell "T-RAN" Gilbert for his innumerable
contributions to the entertainment industry and the citizens of this State and extend to him our
best wishes for continued success in all his future endeavors.
