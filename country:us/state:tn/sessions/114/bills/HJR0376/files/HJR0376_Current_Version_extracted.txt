HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 376
By Cepicky
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate the 2025 Mule Day
Queen, Colbey Dawn Fritts.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
young citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as
outstanding Tennesseans of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Colbey Dawn Fritts, who was recently
selected as the 2025 Mule Day Court Queen; and
WHEREAS, one of the greatest community events in the State of Tennessee, Mule Day
is an annual celebration of all things related to mules and traces its roots back to the 1840s; and
WHEREAS, Mule Day is held annually in Columbia; this year's festivities will take place
the week of April 3-6, 2025, with an estimated 200,000 visitors and guests enjoying the mules,
traditional Appalachian food, music, dancing, and crafts; and
WHEREAS, 2025 Mule Day Queen Colbey Fritts is the daughter of Angela Kittrell and
Todd Fritts, and a senior at Mount Pleasant High School, where she is president of both the
student body and her senior class and a member of Beta Club; and
WHEREAS, she is also captain of the football competition cheer team, which recently
participated in the national cheerleading competition in Florida; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Fritts is a graduate of the Youth Leadership Maury program and is
currently a dual enrollment student at Columbia State Community College; upon graduation
from high school, she plans to attend a four-year university; and
WHEREAS, a community-oriented individual, Colbey Fritts is an advocate for blood and
organ donation and spends her time organizing blood drives and educating her peers about the
importance of blood and organ donation in helping to save lives; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Fritts's noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for her
family, her community, and the citizens of Maury County; and
WHEREAS, the Mule Day Queen and her court play a prominent role in the promotion of
Mule Day, participating in media interviews and serving as hostesses during the festivities; and
WHEREAS, 2025 Mule Day Queen Colbey Fritts epitomizes the spirit of distinction and
the commitment to greatness that are characteristic of true Tennesseans; her dedication to
excellence is clearly deserving of our respect and admiration; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to pause in our deliberations to acknowledge and
applaud this young woman for her remarkable achievement; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Colbey Dawn Fritts upon being selected as the
2025 Mule Day Queen, extending to her our best wishes for every future success and
happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
