HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 480
By Rudd
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Coordinator of Elections
Mark Goins for his ongoing service to the State
of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is the privilege of the members of this General Assembly to specially
recognize those outstanding public servants who have devoted their careers to serving the
citizens of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Mark Goins, Coordinator of Elections with
the Secretary of State's Office, who has distinguished himself as a professional of the greatest
dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Goins was appointed by Secretary of State Tre Hargett as the
Coordinator of Elections on February 11, 2009; during his distinguished tenure, Mr. Goins has
worked with the General Assembly to implement laws to ensure fair, secure, and free elections,
including requiring a photo ID, robust voting machine vendor ethics regulations, and anti-
election hacking laws; and
WHEREAS, Mark Goins also oversaw the rollout of the successful GoVoteTN voter app,
online voter registration, and the "I'm Registered to Vote. Are You?" campaign to encourage
voter registration; and
WHEREAS, under Secretary Hargett's and Coordinator Goins's leadership, the State of
Tennessee has been ranked No. 1 in election integrity for three consecutive years, and in 2023,
the Secretary of State's Office was awarded a national honor by the U.S. Election Assistance
Commission for piloting videos in American Sign Language for Constitutional Amendments; and
WHEREAS, a trusted and admired leader in his field, Mark Goins was selected by his
peers to chair the United States Election Assistance Commission (EAC) Standards Board in

2016; the 110-member EAC is an independent group of state and local election officials created
by the Help America Vote Act (HAVA) of 2002 to assist states in being HAVA compliant; and
WHEREAS, he has also been tapped to serve on the Elections Infrastructure
Government Coordinating Council and the National Association for State Election Directors,
which he has served as treasurer and secretary and currently serves as vice president; and
WHEREAS, he is frequently sought out to give presentations on disaster preparedness,
after Tennessee successfully conducted an election the morning after a deadly tornado ripped
through parts of the State; and
WHEREAS, prior to being appointed Coordinator of Elections, Mark Goins served on the
State Election Commission from March 2005 to February 2009; and
WHEREAS, from 1996 to 2002, he represented District 34 in the Tennessee House of
Representatives, during which time he served on the Elections Subcommittee and was selected
as the 2002 East Tennessee Development District Legislator of the Year; and
WHEREAS, a native of Campbell County, Mr. Goins received his Bachelor of Arts
degree in History from Lincoln Memorial University and his Doctor of Jurisprudence from Regent
University School of Law; he also studied comparative law at Oxford University in England; and
WHEREAS, in recognition of his distinguished professional career and faithful public
service, Lincoln Memorial University inducted him into its Professional Hall of Fame in 2018;
and
WHEREAS, Mark Goins is an inspiration to his colleagues, and his friendship and
counsel are highly valued by those who are fortunate to know him; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Goins epitomizes the ideal of the dedicated professional who is wholly
committed to the highest tenets of public service, and he is most worthy of our approbation;
now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Mark Goins for his many years of devoted service
as Coordinator of Elections with the Secretary of State's Office, extending our gratitude for his
steadfast integrity and invaluable leadership.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
