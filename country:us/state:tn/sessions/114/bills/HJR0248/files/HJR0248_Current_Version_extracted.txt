HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 248
By McCalmon
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Jay Ward Williams.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Jay Ward Williams; and
WHEREAS, Jay Ward Williams was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born to James Roy and Viola Louise Williams at Mercy Hospital in
Sacramento, California on July 8, 1949, Jay Williams was later baptized at Calvary Lutheran
Church, and he attended Rio Linda High School; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Williams went on to earn his associate's degree from American River
College, and he served in the U.S. Air Force from 1967 to 1975; and
WHEREAS, first working as a trucker for his own business, Mr. Williams later served
with acumen and alacrity as a correctional officer with the California Department of Corrections,
in which position he earned the respect of his peers and superiors alike for the exemplary
quality of his work; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Jay Williams always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Williams enjoyed the loving companionship of his wife, Lavada Sue
Cox, and was the proud father of James Ward Williams and Kenneth Edward Williams, the
grandfather of Austin Jay Williams and Candace Williams, and the great-grandfather of Viola
Louise Williams; and
WHEREAS, Jay Ward Williams leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity
in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his
chosen endeavors; and
HJR0248
003774
- 1 -
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Jay Ward Williams, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Williams.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
