HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 678
By Howell
A RESOLUTION to honor Jason Reuter on being named the
2024-2025 MaxPreps National Coach of the
Year.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
an excellent coach who has received a prestigious national honor for leading his team to
unprecedented success in athletic competition; and
WHEREAS, Jason Reuter, Head Coach of the Bradley Central High School girls'
basketball team, has been named the 2024-2025 MaxPreps National Coach of the Year, a most
befitting honor that recognizes his essential contributions to the Bearettes' incredibly successful
season; and
WHEREAS, this year, Coach Reuter led Bradley Central to the school's third
consecutive TSSAA Division I, Class 4A state championship, thus completing a three-peat with
a star-laden team that may be the best he has ever coached; and
WHEREAS, the 2025 championship marks the fourth state title overall that the Bearettes
have won under Coach Reuter's inspiring leadership, having previously claimed the Gold Ball
trophy in 2024, 2023, and 2019; and
WHEREAS, Coach Reuter is now 456-45 in fifteen seasons at the helm, with a program-
best 91 percent winning percentage; and
WHEREAS, in compiling a near-perfect 35-1 record in 2024-2025, Coach Reuter and the
Bearettes became the winningest program in state history; and
WHEREAS, Coach Reuter's Bearettes were ranked fourth nationally in the final
MaxPreps Top 25, and they proudly represented Bradley Central as one of only three public
school teams to finish in the Top 10 of the national rankings; and
HJR0678
007695
- 1 -
WHEREAS, Coach Reuter was fortunate to coach a veteran-laden team with six of the
eight seniors signed to play college basketball, including Harmonie Ware, TaTianna Stovall,
Avary Brewer, Malia Wilcox, Alliyah Osborn, and Love Howard, while standout junior Kimora
Fields earned MaxPreps Tennessee Player of the Year honors and was named Class 4A Miss
Basketball; and
WHEREAS, Coach Reuter has praised not only his players for their selfless play, but
lauded the contributions of his assistant coaches, Adam Belford, Amy Tinsley, and Keith
Freeman, each of whom helped keep the team's expectations high; and
WHEREAS, his many professional accomplishments aside, Jason Reuter is most
grateful for the love and companionship of his wife, Melissa; and
WHEREAS, Jason Reuter has distinguished himself as a great basketball coach,
tactician, and motivator of young women, and he should be appropriately recognized on this
special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Jason Reuter of Bradley Central High School
on being named the 2024-2025 MaxPreps National Coach of the Year, recognize his thirty-one-
year coaching career, and extend to him our best wishes for much continued success on the
court and in life.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
