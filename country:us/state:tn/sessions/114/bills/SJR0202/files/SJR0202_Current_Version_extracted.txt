SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 202
By Oliver
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Tennessee State
University Speech and Debate Team for their
exceptional performance at the Tennessee
Forensics Association State Championship.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to recognize those
outstanding collegians who bring honor to Tennessee as members of academic teams and act
as ambassadors for our State through successful academic competition; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee State University (TSU) Speech and Debate Team is one
such group of young people that has excelled in debate competition and solidified its reputation
as one of the most competitive programs in the State; and
WHEREAS, the TSU Speech and Debate Team once again proved its dominance at the
Tennessee Forensics Association State Championship, bringing home an impressive forty-three
awards and seven state championship titles; and
WHEREAS, in addition to individual success, the team took second place in Individual
Events Sweepstakes and second place overall in Team Sweepstakes; and
WHEREAS, junior Jared Howell showcased his exceptional debate skills at the State
Championship, earning the title of state champion as the first-place speaker in Parliamentary
Debate; his impressive performance also saw him advance to the semifinals in the event, further
demonstrating his expertise in argumentation and critical thinking; and
WHEREAS, also leading the charge was junior Aaron Anderson, who claimed first place
in Program Oral Interpretation, Prose Interpretation, and Pentathlon, along with top placements
in multiple events; and

WHEREAS, sophomore Jocelyn Floyd took home first place in Informative Speaking for
the second consecutive year, while De'Onta Newborn earned first place in Impromptu Speaking;
Trinity Ancrum, who is a junior and first-year competitor, took first place in Poetry Interpretation,
further showcasing the team's strength across multiple categories; and
WHEREAS, with another record-setting performance, the TSU Speech and Debate
Team remains a powerhouse in the forensics community; the team next sets its sights on
national competition, where it aims to build on its success as one of the leading programs in the
nation; and
WHEREAS, the award-winning members of TSU Speech and Debate Team also include
Mary Hooper, Chloe Spearmon, Jenny Miranda, Chardonnay Bowe, Amanda Perez, Alicia
Trevino, Makayla Montgomery, Deja Thomas, Valeria Arredondo, Danae Jackson, Sylvia
Johnson, and Destiny Irving; and
WHEREAS, the members of the TSU Speech and Debate Team demonstrated
extraordinary intellect, preparation, and poise during the debate competition, bringing great
pride to their families, their school, and the citizens of this great State; and
WHEREAS, this General Assembly finds it appropriate to pause in its deliberations to
honor and congratulate these exemplary students on their many accomplishments; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the members of the
Tennessee State University Speech and Debate Team for their exceptional performance at the
Tennessee Forensics Association State Championship and extend to them our best wishes for
every future success.
- 2 - 004578
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
