HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 384
By Freeman
A RESOLUTION to commend Thomas Rudolph Draper for
exemplary service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Rudolph Draper is one such individual, who has distinguished
himself in his role as a legislative intern for Representative Bob Freeman; Mr. Draper's work has
resulted in innumerable benefits for the legislators he assisted and the General Assembly as a
whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at Tennessee State University, where he is highly regarded by his
peers, Thomas Draper has achieved remarkable academic success in making the Dean's List
for three consecutive years while still participating in numerous extracurricular activities; after he
finishes his three-year accelerated graduation track in May, he plans to apply to law school in
the fall, a course of action he has well prepared for by majoring in political science with a minor
in English; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Draper is the president of the Tennessee State University prelaw
chapter of Phi Alpha Delta Law Fraternity, International, and a resident assistant on campus,
positions that further exemplify his exceptional leadership qualities and character; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the myriad aspects and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Mr. Draper brought his many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2025 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly, and his work on the Family Day Committee for the Tennessee Legislative
Internship program was impressive and indispensable; and

WHEREAS, throughout the 2025 legislative session, Thomas Draper has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Draper has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight
and rare decorum, and his efforts are surely a source of pride for his parents, Tiffany Draper
and Rudy Draper; his grandmothers, Patricia Grate and Bobbi Nelle; his university; and his
community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Thomas Rudolph Draper for outstanding service as a
legislative intern for Representative Bob Freeman and extend to him our best wishes for every
future success
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
