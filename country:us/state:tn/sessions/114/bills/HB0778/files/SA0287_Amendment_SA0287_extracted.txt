by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting instead the following:

Section 2:
SECTION 1.
(a) The office of research and education accountability (OREA), in the office of
the comptroller of the treasury, shall conduct a study and make recommendations for the
implementation of an incentive program for educators who have fifteen (15) or more
years' experience in education.
(b) As part of the study, OREA may consider:
(1) A compensation-based structure for awarding bonuses or salary
increases to educators based on their years of service;
(2) Including, for educators who have resigned or retired from teaching
only to return to teaching at a later date, all combined years of service spent as
an educator in the calculation of the educator's years of service;
(3) Excluding temporary absences in employment due to an educator's
use of approved leave for military service, maternity, adoption, recuperation of
health, or death of a relative, from being considered a break in service;
(4) Whether an educator has served as an educator in other states; and
(5) Including, for educators who transition from a teaching role to another
field or role in the school system, all years of service provided by the educator to
the school system through the alternative field or role in the calculation of the
educator's years of service, in addition to their years of service as an educator.

(c) OREA shall report its findings from the study and its recommendations for
implementation of the incentive program described in subsection (a) to the education
committee of the senate and the committee of the house of representatives having
jurisdiction over educator compensation and benefits by December 31, 2025.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
