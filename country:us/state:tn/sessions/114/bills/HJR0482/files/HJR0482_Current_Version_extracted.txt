HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 482
By Russell
A RESOLUTION to recognize Danny Everhart on the occasion of
his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
honor those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout
their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Danny Everhart is one such estimable individual, who, having rendered
forty years of sterling service to Monroe County Emergency Medical Service (EMS), has taken
his much-deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Everhart began his illustrious career with the Monroe County EMS in
1984 as a driver; he rose through the ranks to EMT in 1985 and, eventually, EMT IV; and
WHEREAS, Danny Everhart and his partner, Gary Smith, were the first ones in Monroe
County to use a semi-auto defibrillator to save a citizen's life; additionally, he was involved in the
first LIFESTAR transport, which accommodated an individual from Tellico Plains; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Everhart credits his many years of success to his instructor, Mike Frost;
EMS Director, Danny White; his partners, Gary Smith, Mike Blankenship, and Brett Warren; and
Mayor Mitch Ingram; and
WHEREAS, his professional accomplishments aside, Mr. Everhart is most grateful for
the love and support of his beloved wife, Carmalitta Everhart; and
WHEREAS, the success of the State of Tennessee, the strength of our communities,
and the overall vitality of our society depend, in great measure, on the dedication of people such
as Danny Everhart, who use their considerable talents and resources to serve others; and
HJR0482
005457

WHEREAS, Mr. Everhart is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that
have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially
recognized for his many accomplishments and devoted work; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize and commend Danny Everhart for his honorable and astute
service to the people of Tennessee as we offer our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling
retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
