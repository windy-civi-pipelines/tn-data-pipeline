HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 88
By Love
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Dr. Harold Willoughby
Jordan.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Dr. Harold Willoughby Jordan; and
WHEREAS, a preeminent physician and the first Black commissioner of the Tennessee
Department of Mental Health, Dr. Harold W. Jordan was an exemplary public servant and
consummate professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow
citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Newman, Georgia, on May 24, 1937, Dr. Harold W. Jordan was the
son of Edward P. and Dorothy W. Jordan; he decided at a young age to follow in the footsteps
of his grandfather, Dr. John Henry Jordan, who was the first Black physician in Coweta County,
Georgia; and
WHEREAS, in 1954, Dr. Jordan graduated as class president and valedictorian of
Warner High School and enrolled at Morehouse College, where he was a member of Alpha Phi
Alpha Fraternity, sang in the Morehouse Glee Club, and majored in biology with a minor in
chemistry; and
WHEREAS, after completing his studies at Morehouse College, he entered Meharry
Medical College in Nashville in 1958, following in the steps of both his grandfather and his
great-grandfather, Dr. Edward Ramsey, who was the first Black physician in Houston, Texas;
and
WHEREAS, in 1959, Dr. Jordan met his future wife, Geraldine Crawford, under
extraordinary circumstances; he had gone to Fisk University's gymnasium to see Dr. Martin

Luther King, Jr., speak when a bomb threat occurred and the building was evacuated. Dr.
Jordan and Ms. Crawford met during the evacuation; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Harold Jordan and Geraldine Crawford were joined in holy matrimony
on June 17, 1962, a week after his graduation from Meharry Medical College, and to their
joyous union were born four children, Harold II, Vincent Andre, Karen Dorothy Nicole, and Kristi
Yvette; and
WHEREAS, after completing his residency in internal medicine at Meharry Medical
College, Dr. Jordan accepted a residency in psychiatry at Vanderbilt University Medical Center,
becoming the first Black physician and resident in the history of the university; and
WHEREAS, upon completing his residency, he returned to Meharry Medical College,
where he was named an assistant professor and director of the Psychiatric Outpatient Clinic; he
served in several capacities at Meharry Medical College before taking a brief leave to serve as
assistant commissioner for the Tennessee Department of Mental Health for four years under
Governor Winfield Dunn; and
WHEREAS, in 1975, Dr. Harold Jordan became Tennessee's first Black commissioner of
the Department of Mental Health, and a State building, the Harold W. Jordan Habilitation
Center, was named in his honor; during his tenure as Commissioner of Mental Health, he
delivered the commencement address at Meharry Medical College and accomplished the rare
feat of the accreditation of every mental health facility in the State; and
WHEREAS, he later served as a longtime chair of the Department of Psychiatry and as
acting dean of the Medical School at Meharry; in 2019, Vanderbilt honored him by establishing
the annual Harold Jordan Lecture Series and Award Honoring Diversity, Inclusion, and Social
Justice, recognizing his time as the first African-American resident at Vanderbilt University
Medical Center, where he maintained a clinical appointment until 2016; and

WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Dr. Jordan received the Living
Legends Award from the Thomas J. Blocker Society in collaboration with the Morehouse
College Alumni Association; and
WHEREAS, his past civic and social membership included the American Psychiatric
Association, Distinguished Life Fellow; American Association of University Professors; R.F.
Boyd Medical Society; National Medical Association; Black Psychiatrists of America; Alpha
Omega Alpha Honor Medical Society; Nashville Mental Health Association; Board of Directors,
McKendree Manor Methodist Home for the Aged; Board of Directors, University School of
Nashville; Board of Directors, Martin Methodist College; Board of Trustees, 100 Black Men of
Middle Tennessee; Chi Boule of Sigma Pi Phi; Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity; NAACP Life Member;
and Board Member, Grace M. Eaton Day Home, to name just a few; and
WHEREAS, a man of deep and abiding faith, he was a longtime member of Clark
Memorial United Methodist Church, where he served as a Sunday school teacher as well as
chair of the Pastor Parish Relations Committee for many years; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Jordan enjoyed the love and support of his devoted wife of sixty-two
years, Geraldine C. Jordan; his sons, Harold W. (Michelle) Jordan II and Vincent A. Jordan; his
daughters, Karen Jordan and Kristi Y. Jordan; his brother, Emerson (Nicole) Jordan; his
grandchildren, Rachel L. (Leighton) Batiste, Kathryn A. Jordan, Christopher H. Jordan, and
Dontera (Angel) Scaggs; his great-grandchildren, Carter Scaggs and Jordan Ahmad Batiste;
special children, Sondra (Carlo) Dade, Alice (Maurico) Croom, Ruth (Jeff) Gates, and Robert
(Monica) Anderson, Jr.; special daughter, Dorothy (Deedie) Davis; and godson, Christopher
Davis; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his cousins, Lucille
Harris, Bernice Small, Reverend McAllister Hollins, Jocelyn (Joseph) Fowler, Veta Jonas,
- 3 - 002401
Edward D. Jonas, Justine (Joseph) Harris, and Detra Robinson and a host of extended family
and dear friends; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents; his sister, Vertelle; and his
brother, Edward Jordan II; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Harold W. Jordan leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and
probity in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Dr. Harold Willoughby Jordan, reflecting fondly
upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with
courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Dr. Jordan.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
