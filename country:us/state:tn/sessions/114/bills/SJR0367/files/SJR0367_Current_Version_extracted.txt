SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 367
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor Robert "Buz" Lacy on being inducted
into the Boys & Girls Clubs of Tennessee Hall of
Fame.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those stalwart citizens who are dedicated to improving the quality of life for the youth
in their communities; and
WHEREAS, Robert "Buz" Lacy, longtime board member of the Boys & Girls Club of
Morristown, is one such outstanding servant-leader; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lacy has been inducted into the Boys & Girls Clubs of Tennessee Hall
of Fame in recognition of his hard work and generosity in support of this worthy organization;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lacy has provided valuable leadership for the Boys & Girls Club of
Morristown in numerous roles, including board president, vice president, chair of the executive
committee, and chair and emcee of its annual benefit dinner and fundraising event; and
WHEREAS, he has also served on numerous board committees and has been a leading
fundraiser for many years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lacy is a charter member of the Boys & Girls Club Foundation of
Morristown, a nonprofit organization whose sole purpose is to raise funds and resources to
support the local club; and
WHEREAS, he has served the larger community with the same dedication and
distinction as chair of TCAT Morristown, president and Paul Harris Fellow of the local Rotary
Club, president of the Cherokee Guidance board of directors, and a board member of the local
Red Cross and the Morristown Chamber of Commerce; and
SJR0367

WHEREAS, Mr. Lacy served in the U.S. Coast Guard for six years before matriculating
at Carson-Newman College and earning his Bachelor of Science degree in Education; and
WHEREAS, his studies at Vanderbilt University led him to pursue a career in banking
that thrived for fifty years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lacy expresses his faith as a member of Morristown's First Presbyterian
Church, where he has served as an elder and president of the men's Sunday School class; and
WHEREAS, his many professional and civic achievements aside, he is most
appreciative of the love and companionship he shares with his wife of fifty-six years, Martha
Lacy; their sons and daughters-in-law, Todd and Kristi and Matt and Kristy; five grandchildren;
and four great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Buz Lacy is wholly committed to the noble precepts of youth empowerment
that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and it is appropriate that he
should be specially recognized on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Robert "Buz" Lacy on
being inducted into the Boys & Girls Clubs of Tennessee Hall of Fame and thank him for his
ongoing commitment to his community and the youth of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
