HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 124
By Sparks
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Fire Chief William
Culbertson, Jr., on the occasion of his retirement
from the Smyrna Fire Department.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those citizens who have performed
with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm in securing the safety of their community; and
WHEREAS, Fire Chief William Culbertson, Jr., is one such estimable individual, who
served with distinction for more than thirty years as the fire chief of the Smyrna Fire Department
and will soon embark upon a much-deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, Chief William Culbertson comes from a line of respected firefighters; his
grandfather became Smyrna's first fire chief in 1924, and his father was the fire and police
commissioner, vice mayor, and finance commissioner, providing a superlative example of civil
service that Chief Culbertson could follow in his career; and
WHEREAS, Chief William Culbertson worked his first fire department job painting fire
hydrants when he was only twelve, and he went on to graduate from Middle Tennessee
Christian School and Smyrna High School; and
WHEREAS, when he was hired by the Smyrna Fire Department in 1989 after serving on
a volunteer basis, Chief Culbertson became the first full-time employee in the department's
history, and since his tenure's commencement, the city has grown to more than 57,400
residents, requiring the construction of five new fire stations, the renovation of two stations, and
the hiring of 108 full-time personnel; and
WHEREAS, an innovator in fire service, Chief Culbertson helped found a youth camp
program to familiarize children with the apparel and procedures of emergency service personnel
in action, helping to instill trust in young people before any misfortune requires it; and
WHEREAS, in addition to maintaining an Insurance Services Office (ISO) score of 3,
Chief Culbertson has implemented numerous community initiatives and department programs to

both enrich the town's culture and protect its people: the town's annual Independence Day
Celebration and Easter egg hunt, the Fire and Police Youth Academy, the award-winning
Smyrna Fire Department Honor Guard, the annual Fire Prevention Week Open House, and the
annual food drive benefitting Nourish Food Bank; and
WHEREAS, he extended the department's capacity for fire prevention by hiring a fire
codes and origin investigator in 2019 and then expanded that capacity even further by creating
a fire codes inspector unit that currently has fifteen inspectors; the department's drone program,
which began in 2021, now has twenty certified drone pilots at the department who utilize cutting-
edge technology to ensure the city's safety; and
WHEREAS, under his leadership, the department assisted during the tragic United
States Navy Blue Angels jet crash in 2016, collaborating with the Navy, National Guard,
Tennessee Highway Patrol, and Smyrna Police Department; and
WHEREAS, apart from his work in fire service, Chief Culbertson is a passionate
photographer, a skill he hopes to further develop and utilize as he plans his retirement travels;
and
WHEREAS, he exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family, especially his seven
children and four grandchildren, and community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean;
and
WHEREAS, throughout his years in public service, Chief William Culbertson
demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the respect and
admiration of his fellow firefighters and the residents of Rutherford County; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Fire Chief William Culbertson, Jr., of the Smyrna
Fire Department for his exemplary career in fire service and extend to him our heartfelt wishes
for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
