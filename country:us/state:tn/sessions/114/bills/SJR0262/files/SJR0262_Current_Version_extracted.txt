SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 262
By Hensley
A RESOLUTION to commemorate "General Pulaski Memorial
Day."
WHEREAS, October 11, 1779, is the date in American history that marks the heroic
death of Brigadier General Casimir Pulaski, who died from wounds received on October 8,
1779, at the siege of Savannah, Georgia, during the Revolutionary War; and
WHEREAS, often referred to as "The Father of American Cavalry" or "The Soldier of
Liberty," General Casimir Pulaski raised Pulaski's Legion for the Continental Army and reformed
the American cavalry as a whole; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that October 11 of each year be commemorated with suitable
patriotic and public exercises to remember the sacrifice and memorialize the death of this great
American hero of the Revolutionary War; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we join with the citizens of Pulaski in
commemorating October 11 as "General Pulaski Memorial Day" to honor the life of Brigadier
General Casimir Pulaski and his many contributions to freedom as a military commander for the
Continental Army during the Revolutionary War.
SJR0262
