HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 706
By Sherrell
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Tommy Myers, Jr., of
McMinnville.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Tommy Myers, Jr., of
McMinnville; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Myers was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, a native of Warren County, Mr. Myers was born on July 19, 1961, to
Thomas Myers, Sr., and Brenda Roberts Myers of McMinnville
WHEREAS, for more than three decades, Tommy Myers served the citizens of Warren
County with honor, integrity, and dedication as sheriff; he was known for his commitment to the
community and beloved by those whom he so ably served and protected; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Myers began his law enforcement career with the Warren County
Sheriff's Department in 1991 as a jailer; he worked his way through the ranks, serving as chief
deputy for nine years before assuming the post of sheriff, serving in that capacity for four years
before retiring in 2022; and
WHEREAS, during his time as sheriff, he advocated for inmates in a meaningful way to
interrupt the cycle of incarceration, seeking rehabilitation over punishment and working closely
with the judicial system and drug court; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Myers was a member of the Baptist faith and loved the outdoors; a
motorcycle enthusiast, he was a professional motocross driver from the age of sixteen to forty-
four; and

WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Mr. Myers will be sadly mourned but fondly
remembered by his beloved wife of twenty-one years, Chrystal Turner Myers; his sons, Clayton
(Hannah) Myers and Ty Myers; and his grandchildren, Paisley Myers and Paxton Myers; and
WHEREAS, he is further survived by his sister, Debra (Kenny) Thompson; his mother-in-
law, Joan Turner; his father-in-law, Hugh Turner; his brother-in-law and sister-in-law, Jeff and
Tyra Turner; and many nieces, nephews, great-nieces, great-nephews, aunts, uncles, cousins,
and special friends; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Myers leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Tommy Myers, Jr., reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Myers.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
