SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 82
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor Dr. Dearl Douglas Lampley upon the
occasion of his retirement from Columbia State
Community College.
WHEREAS, Dr. Dearl Douglas Lampley has taken a much-deserved retirement from
Columbia State Community College after dedicating more than a quarter of a century to the
school; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Lampley spent his outstanding professional career in higher education
serving in various roles, including instructor, program director, faculty Senate president, and
dean; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Lampley joined the Columbia State faculty in 1998 and served as dean
for its science, technology, and math division from 2010 until the spring of 2018; and
WHEREAS, as dean, Dr. Lampley excelled at developing courses and academic
programs, articulating with secondary school and university partners, and building relationships
with industry partners; and
WHEREAS, his foresight and guidance led to such initiatives as the advanced integrated
industrial technology AAS degree, the Hampshire Unit School project-based learning program,
STEM events geared specifically toward girls and young women, and fully embedded college
programs in area high schools, including Fairview and Centennial; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Lampley was also instrumental in developing workforce training and
educational programs for industry partners, including several major corporations; and
WHEREAS, a native of Fairview in Williamson County, Dr. Lampley earned his
bachelor's degree in animal science and vocational education from the University of Tennessee,
Knoxville (UTK) and then conducted research at Ames Plantation Education and Research

Center as part of the requirements for obtaining his master's degree in animal science from
UTK; and
WHEREAS, he continued his education at East Tennessee State University, where he
earned a doctorate in educational leadership and policy analysis; his doctoral dissertation was a
study of higher education appropriations in relation to student success factors; and
WHEREAS, the American Association of University Administrators recognized the
quality of this work by honoring Dr. Lampley with the 2018 Leo and Margaret Goodman-
Malamuth Dissertation Award; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Lampley recently retired as vice president of the Williamson campus
and external services for Columbia State; and
WHEREAS, at the end of his distinguished career as an educator and administrator with
Columbia State, we wish to express our heartfelt gratitude to Dr. Lampley for his outstanding
contributions and dedication to that institution in particular and science and technology
education in general; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Dr. Dearl Douglas Lampley upon his
exemplary career with Columbia State Community College, praise his work in developing
academic programs and workforce training complementary to the science and technology of
today's world, and extend to him our best wishes for success and happiness during his
retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
