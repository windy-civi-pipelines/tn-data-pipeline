HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 663
By Hawk
A RESOLUTION to recognize Kenneth Robert Carpenter of the
Greene County Sheriff's Office on the occasion
of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of service; and
WHEREAS, Kenneth Robert Carpenter of the Greene County Sheriff's Office is one such
estimable individual, who has devoted his life to the preservation of law and order and the
protection of his fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, after an outstanding career in law enforcement, Kenneth Carpenter will take
his well-deserved retirement on June 26, 2025; and
WHEREAS, born on June 17, 1938, in Gowen, Michigan, Kenneth Robert Carpenter
served in the Michigan National Guard from 1955 to 1957 and joined the United States Air
Force in 1958, seeing duty in Greenland, before receiving his honorable discharge in 1963; and
WHEREAS, relocating to Tennessee in 1964, Mr. Carpenter worked as a salesman for
Yamaha and then Pitney Bowes before embarking on his distinguished career in law
enforcement in 1969; and
WHEREAS, an exemplary law enforcement professional, Mr. Carpenter has served in a
number of capacities, most recently working courthouse security as a bailiff for several of the
judges in Greene County; and
WHEREAS, an active part of his community, he is a member of the Jaycees and the
Moose Lodge and has served as an official with the TSSAA for more than sixty-one years; and
WHEREAS, a man of deep and abiding faith, he is a longtime member of the
Reformation Lutheran Church; and

WHEREAS, his many accomplishments aside, Kenneth Carpenter is most grateful for
the love and companionship he shares with his wife of sixty-four years, Judith Borders
Carpenter, and he is a devoted father of two children, Lisa and Brent, and the doting
grandfather of three grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Kenneth Carpenter exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and
community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his remarkable career, Kenneth Carpenter has performed his
duties with the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, helping his fellow citizens in any
way he could and proving himself to be a tremendous asset to the Greene County Sheriff's
Office; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor Kenneth Robert Carpenter of the Greene County Sheriff's Office
on the occasion of his retirement, commend his many years of distinguished service, and wish
him every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
