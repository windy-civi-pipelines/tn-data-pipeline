SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 261
By Watson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Ty Campbell on the
occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should specially recognize those outstanding
professionals who have devoted their careers to providing the highest-quality education and
learning environments for Tennessee's students; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Ty Campbell, who, having distinguished
herself as a professional of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity throughout her career,
is taking a much-deserved retirement as the Gifted Eligibility and Services Lead for the Hamilton
County Department of Education; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Campbell holds a Master of Education degree in Curriculum and
Instruction and a Bachelor of Science degree in Multi-disciplinary Education from the University
of Tennessee at Chattanooga; she began her exemplary career in education in 2000; and
WHEREAS, over the past twenty-five years, Ty Campbell has excelled as a regular
education teacher, teacher of gifted students, and achievement assessment specialist, serving
in urban, suburban, and rural settings, and administering individual achievement testing and
creative thinking assessments; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Campbell directed Project Explore from 2002 to 2008, a summer
program for high-ability and gifted learners from Title I schools in Hamilton County, and was an
ETS National Praxis Item writer and reviewer from 2013 to 2017; and
WHEREAS, since 2018, she has served as the Gifted Eligibility and Services Lead for
the Hamilton County Department of Education, where she has excelled in hiring and training
new gifted teachers, provided support for twenty-three itinerant gifted teachers serving
approximately seventy schools, managed oversight for up to 1,500 gifted-eligible K-12 students,
responded to parent and administrator concerns, and ensured compliance with district and State
procedures; and

WHEREAS, Ty Campbell has been actively involved in her profession at the school,
district, state, national, and international levels, contributing to professional learning
communities, presenting at conferences, writing grant proposals, and serving on advisory
boards; and
WHEREAS, duly recognized for her excellence and dedication as a teacher, Ms.
Campbell was a recipient of the Walmart Teacher of the Year Award in 2005 and 2007 and
received a $10,000 Fund for Teacher Grant in 2015 to explore environmental and cultural
aspects of Alaska and the Oregon Coast; and
WHEREAS, throughout her estimable career, Ty Campbell has demonstrated the utmost
professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of her
colleagues, her students, and the citizens she has so ably served; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Ty Campbell for her
exceptional service to the students and schools of Tennessee, salute her professional expertise
and personal excellence, and wish her much continued success and happiness during her
retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
