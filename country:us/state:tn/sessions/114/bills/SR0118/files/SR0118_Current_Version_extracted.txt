SENATE RESOLUTION 118
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION to commend Marie Garroway for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, the members of this legislative body are pleased to
recognize certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the
betterment of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Marie Garroway is one such individual, who has distinguished herself in her
role as a legislative intern for Senator London Lamar; Ms. Garroway's work has resulted in
innumerable benefits for the legislators she assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Garroway, who is driven by her passion for public service, has worked
alongside legislators, nonprofit advocates, and local government entities to advance policy
solutions and promote civil engagement; and
WHEREAS, a senior at Vanderbilt University, where she is highly regarded by her peers,
Ms. Garroway has achieved academic success while pursuing a degree in public policy studies
with a minor in sociology and preparing an honors thesis that explores the job and housing
imbalance in the suburbs of twenty-first-century Atlanta, Georgia; and
WHEREAS, on campus, Marie Garroway is involved with Vandy Votes, helping to
register student voters and ultimately contributing to Vanderbilt's designation as one of the most
politically engaged campuses in the nation; she is also a member of the Generation Action for
the Planned Parenthood Policy Committee; and
WHEREAS, prior to her current work, Ms. Garroway was an intern in the Metro Nashville
Office of Family Safety, where she compiled a detailed report on the legal and policy precedent
surrounding the use of body-worn cameras in domestic violence cases; and

WHEREAS, in her hometown of Atlanta, Georgia, Marie Garroway worked on state
legislative campaigns and served as the intern coordinator and call time manager for Susie
Greenberg in 2024 and a fellow for Representative Shea Roberts's 2020 campaign; she also
served with the Georgia Coalition Against Domestic Violence, conducting research on the
criminalization of domestic violence survivors; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies as a public policy professional, Marie
Garroway brought her many abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2025 session
of the One Hundred Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2025 legislative session, Ms. Garroway has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Marie Garroway has performed her many duties with extraordinary insight
and rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby commend Marie
Garroway for outstanding service as a legislative intern for Senator London Lamar and extend
to her our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
