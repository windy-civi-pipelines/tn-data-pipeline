HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 296
By Brooks
A RESOLUTION recognizing Patricia and Manuel Oliver for their
unwavering fight against gun violence.
WHEREAS, on February 14, 2018, Patricia and Manuel Oliver experienced the tragic
loss of their beloved son, Joaquin "Guac" Oliver, in the Parkland, Florida, school shooting; and
WHEREAS, determined to ensure that their son's memory does not become just another
statistic, Patricia and Manuel Oliver have turned their pain into relentless action, using every tool
available, including activism and confrontation, to demand real change; and
WHEREAS, through their nonprofit organization, Change the Ref (CTR), the Olivers
have worked tirelessly to hold politicians, the gun industry, and complacent leaders
accountable; and
WHEREAS, the fight against gun violence is deeply personal for the Olivers, as they
have made it their mission to be the voice of their son, Joaquin, who cannot be here to speak for
himself; and
WHEREAS, their bold activism has sparked real change, exemplified by their demand
for the creation of the White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention, which was established
as a result of their and many others' impassioned advocacy; and
WHEREAS, Patricia and Manuel Oliver's activism is characterized by its loud,
unapologetic, and impossible-to-ignore nature, using art, storytelling, and unexpected platforms
to reach people in impactful ways; and
WHEREAS, some of the Olivers' most notable projects include the NRA Children's
Museum, a traveling exhibit exposing the deadly consequences of America's obsession with
guns; The Lost Class, a symbolic graduation ceremony for victims of gun violence; The Final

Exam, a video game that educates players on gun safety bills; and murals and public art that
bring Joaquin Oliver's story to life and remind people of what they must fight for; and
WHEREAS, their latest project, "Guac," is a powerful stage performance that takes
audiences through the Olivers' journey of loss, activism, and resilience, challenging people to
act against gun violence; and
WHEREAS, Patricia and Manuel Oliver have dedicated their lives to ensuring that their
son's story saves lives, pledging that so long as gun violence continues to destroy families, they
will continue to speak out, push forward, and make sure his voice is heard; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Patricia and Manuel Oliver for their unwavering
dedication to fighting gun violence in memory of their son, Joaquin "Guac" Oliver.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
