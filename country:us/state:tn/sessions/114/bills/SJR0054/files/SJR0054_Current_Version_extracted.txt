SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 54
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of James Ralph Inman, Sr.,
of Kingston.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of James Ralph Inman,
Sr.; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Inman was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, born to Robert Taylor and Dora Alice Inman in Roane County, Mr. Inman
attended school in Meigs County; and
WHEREAS, a longtime resident of Kingston, Mr. Inman was a highly respected member
of his community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Inman proudly served his country in the United States Army; he
graduated from Tennessee Polytechnic Institute and served with acumen and alacrity at Oak
Ridge National Laboratory until his retirement in 1988, having earned the respect of his peers
and supervisors alike for the superior quality of his work; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Inman was an active and devout member of First Baptist Church of
Kingston, which he served faithfully for many years; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Mr. Inman always endeavored to remain true
to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Inman enjoyed the loving companionship of his wife, Jacquelyn Cox
Inman, and was the proud father of James Ralph (Janet) Inman, Jr., Steven Clyde (Lynda)
Inman, and Gordon Scott (Cara) Inman; and

002469

WHEREAS, he is further survived by his brother, John Peak Inman, and a host of other
family members and friends; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his brothers, R.T.
Inman, Jr., and Charles Inman, and his sisters, Blanche and Betty; and
WHEREAS, leaving behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public life and
compassion and loyalty in private life, Mr. Inman exercised diligence and dedication in all of his
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of this esteemed individual,
James Ralph Inman, Sr., reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character, his many
contributions to the people of Tennessee, and his stalwart commitment to living the examined
life with courage and conviction. We express our sympathy and extend our condolences to the
family of Mr. Inman.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
