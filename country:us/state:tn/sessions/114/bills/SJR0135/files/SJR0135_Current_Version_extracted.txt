SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 135
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Wanda Perrin Gray of
Dandridge.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Wanda Perrin Gray; and
WHEREAS, Wanda Gray was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Gray served with acumen and alacrity as a barber at Rubles Barber
Shop in Newport, in which position she earned the respect of her colleagues and clients alike for
the exemplary quality of her work; and
WHEREAS, Wanda Gray was an active and devout member of Swann's Chapel Baptist
Church, which she served faithfully for many years; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to her family, Mrs. Gray always endeavored to remain true
to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Wanda Gray enjoyed the loving companionship of her husband of thirty-
eight years, Roy Gray, and was the proud mother of Pete (Beth) Higgs, Alisa (Bob) Lemons,
Jackie (San Michael) Showalter; grandmother of seven grandchildren; and great-grandmother of
eleven grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Gray is further survived by her brother, Billy Ray Perrin; several nieces
and nephews; and special friends, Everett and Louise McMurry; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents, Jim Perrin and Elizabeth "Bessie" Clowers Perrin,
Mrs. Gray was preceded in death by her siblings, Bud Perrin, Troy Perrin, Harold Perrin, Jean
Miller, and Alva Walker, and sister-in-law, Mildred Perrin; and

WHEREAS, Wanda Perrin Gray leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity
in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Wanda Perrin Gray,
reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mrs. Gray.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
