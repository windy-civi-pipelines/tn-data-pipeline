SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 224
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to commend Bayleigh J. Griffith for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Bayleigh J. Griffith is one such individual, who has distinguished herself in
her role as a legislative intern for Senator Becky Massey; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Griffith's work, her pleasant demeanor, and her respectful manner of
communication have resulted in innumerable benefits for the legislators she assisted and the
General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where she is highly
regarded by her peers, Ms. Griffith has achieved academic success while pursuing a Bachelor
of Arts degree in Political Science with a minor in English; and
WHEREAS, Bayleigh Griffith was born and raised in Oneida, where she excelled as a
dancer and as an assistant choreographer for the drama program at her high school; and
WHEREAS, following her first semester of college, Ms. Griffith moved back to her
hometown, where she was honored to be the first former student of her dance studio to become
an associate instructor; and
WHEREAS, Bayleigh Griffith's extensive background in dance and drama have truly
shaped her leadership abilities and taught her the importance of commitment and determination;
and

WHEREAS, Ms. Griffith went on to serve local lawyer Lori Phillips-Jones as a legal
assistant in 2023, drafting legal documents and assisting with legal research, and then as her
campaign assistant during Mayor Jones's mayoral campaign; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Bayleigh Griffith brought her many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2025 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2025 legislative session, Ms. Griffith has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern, serving with professionalism,
organization, courtesy, and kindness to all; and
WHEREAS, Bayleigh Griffith has performed her many duties with extraordinary insight
and rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Bayleigh J. Griffith for
outstanding service as a legislative intern for Senator Becky Massey and extend to her our best
wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
