SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 434
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Clifford Allan Brunger on
the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
extraordinary Tennesseans who, through their remarkable and exceptional efforts, have gone
above and beyond the call of duty by dedicating their lives to protecting the citizens of this State
and nation; and
WHEREAS, Clifford Allan Brunger is one such estimable individual, who has served the
United States Navy and APM Group, Inc., with distinction and will embark upon his much-
deserved retirement on June 12, 2025; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Brunger grew up in Fairview, where he and his brother fulfilled their
household chores of transporting water from a nearby spring, cutting wood to heat the house,
and milking their grandfather's cows for milk while enduring familial hardships at home; and
WHEREAS, it was because of these obstacles that Clifford Brunger began working at
the age of fourteen, and during his high school career, he worked up to thirty hours each week
while concurrently maintaining pristine grades in school and challenging his teachers to
increase his workload; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Brunger graduated as Salutatorian of Fairview High School's Class of
1976 and then enlisted in the United States Navy, where he served as an electronics technician;
and
WHEREAS, he attended Nuclear Power School in Orlando, Florida, and nuclear
prototype training in Idaho Falls, Idaho, and, a year after his enlistment, Mr. Brunger was
promoted to E5; he received his nuclear power operator designation in 1978; and

WHEREAS, after he completed Naval Academy Preparatory School, Clifford Brunger
attended the United States Naval Academy in Annapolis, Maryland, graduating in 1984 with a
degree in electrical engineering; and
WHEREAS, following flight school and service as a naval pilot and training flight
instructor, Mr. Brunger was selected as an aeronautical duty officer and then selected for Naval
Postgraduate School, earning his master's degree in aeronautical engineering in 1994; and
WHEREAS, his other positions within the Navy include service as an operational test
director, an aircraft project engineer, and a lead for two programs with billion-dollar budgets; and
WHEREAS, in 2002, Mr. Brunger retired at the rank of Commander, following twenty-six
years of distinguished service to the United States Navy; and
WHEREAS, that same year, he established APM Group, Inc., providing engineering
services to the Department of Defense and analyzing naval contracts related to the manufacture
of helicopters and planes, among other machinery; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Brunger has served APM Group, Inc., as president and chief financial
officer for twenty-three years and will take his well-deserved retirement this year; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Brunger exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his illustrious career, Clifford Brunger has served these United
States and the State of Tennessee with honor and distinction, and it is wholly fitting that he be
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and recognize Clifford Allan Brunger upon
the occasion of his retirement, extend our gratitude for his many years of dutiful and valorous
service, and offer our best wishes for his every future happiness and success.
- 2 - 007020
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
