HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 287
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Omar Ernest "Ernie"
Bacon, Jr., of Franklin.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Omar Ernest "Ernie" Bacon, Jr., of Franklin; and
WHEREAS, Ernie Bacon was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Chattanooga on May 9, 1937, Mr. Bacon was the son of Omar
Ernest "O.E." Bacon, Jr., and Mary Elizabeth Sutton Bacon; and
WHEREAS, Ernie Bacon was a graduate of Tyner High School, and at the age of
seventeen, he enlisted in the United States Naval Reserve and proudly served his nation for
eight years; and
WHEREAS, he attended the University of Tennessee, where he served as the senior
class president, a member of the 4-H Club, and a member of the Zeta Chapter of Pi Kappa
Alpha; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bacon obtained his Master of Hospital Administration degree from the
Medical College of Virginia and enjoyed a fruitful career in hospital administration and
healthcare management; and
WHEREAS, he earned a Doctor of Pharmacy degree from the University of Tennessee
in Memphis, where he met his wife, Nell, and was a member of Kappa Psi Pharmaceutical
Fraternity and Rho Chi Academic Honor Society in Pharmacy; and
WHEREAS, Ernie Bacon and his wife were the first pharmacists to work at the Moccasin
Bend Psychiatric Hospital; he served as president of the Georgia Hospital Association and

chairman of the Southeastern Hospital Association and had residencies throughout Georgia,
South Carolina, and Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, following his retirement from his position as senior vice president and chief
operating officer of Community Health Systems, Mr. Bacon conducted independent consulting
for numerous companies and hospitals and used his business background for long-term service
as an Independence Trust Company board member; and
WHEREAS, Ernie Bacon was a published author on healthcare ethics and management
and an adjunct professor for several colleges, such as Virginia Commonwealth, Georgia State,
University of Alabama-Birmingham, and Washington University; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bacon was a history enthusiast and an active part of Civil War and
historic preservation endeavors; he served as a founding member and president of Franklin's
Charge; board member, vice chair, and treasurer of the Tennessee Preservation Trust; past
chair of the Historic Carnton Plantation and the Heritage Foundation of Franklin and Williamson
County; initial member and vice chair of the City of Franklin Battlefield Commission; and board
member of the Tennessee Civil War Preservation Association, the Battle of Franklin Trust, and
the Tennessee Civil War National Heritage Area; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bacon was twice elected as an Alderman of the City of Franklin, a
position he held from 1999 to 2007, and during that time, he founded Franklin Tomorrow,
Franklin Sister Cities, and Franklin Transit Authority; and
WHEREAS, he was appointed as chairman of the Hamilton County School Board by the
Hamilton County Quarterly Court, and during his tenure, he served as the youngest school
board member in Tennessee; additionally, former Governor Bill Haslam appointed him to the
Tennessee Historical Commission in 2015; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Ernie Bacon received the Bob
Ragsdale Award from the Tennessee Civil War Preservation Association, the Anne T.
- 2 - 003659
Rutherford Exemplary Community Volunteer Award, and the SAGE Award from the Council of
Aging of Middle Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, perhaps his greatest contributions as a public official were collaborating
alongside Dr. Thomas Frist, Sr., and Minnie Pearl in creating the Sarah Cannon Cancer Center;
developing future hospital executives; and developing three public parks, including Harlinsdale
Farm, Eastern Flank Battlefield, and Carter Hill Battlefield Park; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bacon was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly missed but fondly remembered by his beloved wife of sixty-
six years, Nell Crawford Bacon; his children, Lisa Dawn Bacon (Clay) Hackney and Steven
Ernest (LaDonna) Bacon; his grandchildren, Julia Louise Hackney (Mitch) Cagle, Amanda Erin
Hackney (Charles) Thomas, and Jackson Ross Bacon; his great-grandchildren, Hayden Michael
Cagle, Leah Anderson Cagle, Charlotte Nell Cagle, Charles Broadrick Thomas, and Erin Rose
Thomas; his siblings, Marialice Baker and Ellis Sutton Bacon; and several nieces and nephews;
and
WHEREAS, Ernie and Nell Bacon were proud residents of Fourth Avenue South for
more than thirty years, welcoming their neighbors and the community into their home, known as
the Bacon-Campbell House, and featuring their home on several home tours; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bacon was also wholly devoted to his faith and attended Franklin First
United Methodist Church with his family; and
WHEREAS, Ernie Bacon leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
- 3 - 003659
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Omar Ernest "Ernie" Bacon, Jr., reflecting fondly
upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with
courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. Bacon.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
