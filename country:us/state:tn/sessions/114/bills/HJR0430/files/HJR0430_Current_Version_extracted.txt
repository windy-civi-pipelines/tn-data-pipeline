HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 430
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor and recognize Camryn Brewer, 2025
Top Ten Senior of Mt. Pleasant High School.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to pay
tribute to those students who, through their natural talents and unflagging commitment to
academic excellence, have distinguished themselves in the classroom; and
WHEREAS, Camryn Brewer is one such exceptional student, who was recently named a
Top Ten Senior of the 2025 graduating class at Mt. Pleasant High School; and
WHEREAS, through diligent study, steady composure during examinations, and
steadfast commitment of time and talent, Ms. Brewer has achieved outstanding academic
success and joined the ranks of the best and brightest students in Tennessee and our country;
and
WHEREAS, in her remarkable high school career, Camryn Brewer has earned an
exemplary grade point average of 99.48 while participating in numerous extracurricular
activities, including volleyball, softball, student council, Beta Club, Best Buddies, and HOSA;
and
WHEREAS, following her graduation, Ms. Brewer plans to study radiology; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Brewer's display of intellectual aptitude and ambition surely bodes well
for her future success; and
WHEREAS, her noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for her family,
her school, and her community; and
WHEREAS, Camryn Brewer's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of the
respect, admiration, and commendation of the members of this General Assembly; now,
therefore,
HJR0430
004852
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend and honor Camryn Brewer on being named a 2025
Mt. Pleasant High School Top Ten Senior, salute her impressive academic achievement, and
extend to her our best wishes for a happy, successful future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 2 - 004852
