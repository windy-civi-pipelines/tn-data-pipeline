SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 65
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to recognize Russell Treadway on the occasion
of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those citizens who have performed
with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Russell Treadway is one such estimable person, who rendered sterling
service for twenty-eight years as Sevierville's city administrator and retired from service on
January 3, 2025; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Treadway first accepted the position of city administrator in 1977 when
he was twenty-four years old and served until 1993; he returned to the position in 2012, serving
until his retirement; and
WHEREAS, as city administrator of Sevierville, Mr. Treadway served under four mayors
and oversaw numerous public infrastructure improvement projects, including the downtown
streetscapes project, police department headquarters expansion, and the creation of new fire
department headquarters and a new downtown fire station; and
WHEREAS, additionally, he supervised ongoing water and wastewater plant
expansions, ongoing expansion of the community center and city park, and significant increases
in the number of police officers and firefighters; and
WHEREAS, under his leadership as city administrator, Mr. Treadway deftly led the City
of Sevierville forward, capably directing the city through challenging times of change and
significant growth; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Treadway exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career as city administrator of Sevierville, Mr.
Treadway demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled
respect and admiration of his colleagues and community alike; now, therefore,
SJR0065
002364
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we extend to Russell Treadway our heartfelt wishes
for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
