HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 145
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Representative Richard
"Dick" Russwurm Clark, Sr., of Nashville.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of our former colleague,
Representative Richard "Dick" Russwurm Clark, Sr., of Nashville, a prominent Tennessean who
believed strongly in the power of service and dedication; and
WHEREAS, an eleven-term member of the House of Representatives, Dick Clark ably
represented the good people of the Antioch community in Davidson County during his illustrious
tenure in the General Assembly, which spanned the 88th through 98th General Assemblies; and
WHEREAS, first elected in 1972, Representative Clark worked tirelessly in support of
the working man and his fellow citizens with mental illness and addiction problems; and
WHEREAS, Representative Clark was one of the most influential pro-labor legislators
during his twenty-two-year tenure, and his commitment to public service ensured his legacy as a
leader who worked with integrity and compassion; and
WHEREAS, Dick Clark enlisted in the U.S. Marine Corps after graduating from high
school and served admirably until a genetic eye disease forced his discharge; and
WHEREAS, upon returning home to Nashville, Representative Clark married his high
school sweetheart, Sharon, and the couple built a happy home while he worked his way through
Middle Tennessee State University as an undergraduate and then Nashville School of Law,
from which he earned his juris doctorate; and
WHEREAS, as an attorney, Representative Clark focused on labor issues; no matter the
difficulty of the case at hand, he never allowed his limited vision to prevent him from effectively
representing his client; and

WHEREAS, after his political and legal careers ended, Dick Clark played an instrumental
role in establishing Foundations Clinic and Dual Diagnosis Management with his partners and
friends, Bob Nash and Mike Cartwright; and
WHEREAS, through his work with the clinic, he was a fierce advocate for people
suffering from mental illness and addiction; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clark's family was his greatest joy, and he took great pride in their
accomplishments while cherishing their happiness; and
WHEREAS, Dick Clark is survived by his brother, Jerry Clark; his former wife of thirty-
five years and the mother of his six children, Sharon Clark; his current wife, Deborah Clark; six
children; twenty grandchildren; and nine great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents, the late Beaula Mae Clark and
William "Bill" Clark, and his sister, Billie Ann Abrams; and
WHEREAS, Representative Clark touched many lives through his wisdom and
commitment to making Tennessee an even better place; and
WHEREAS, for twenty-two years, Dick Clark was a vital member of this body, and his
passing leaves a void in his community and this State that will not be easily filled; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of our former colleague Representative Richard
"Dick" Russwurm Clark, Sr., reflecting fondly upon his numerous contributions to good
government in Tennessee, his advocacy for the labor force as an attorney and legislator, and
his legacy of effective public service in his community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and extend our
condolences to Representative Clark's family and many friends.

for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
