HOUSE RESOLUTION 104
By Crawford
A RESOLUTION to commend Stephen Curtis for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, the members of this legislative body are pleased to
recognize certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the
betterment of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Stephen Curtis is one such individual, who has distinguished himself in his
role as a legislative intern for Representative John Crawford, Chairman of the House Local
Government Committee; Mr. Curtis's work has resulted in innumerable benefits for the
legislators he assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga (UTC), where he
is highly regarded by his peers, Mr. Curtis has achieved academic success while pursuing a
Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science with a minor in Economics and a concentration in
International Relations and Comparative Government; and
WHEREAS, on campus, Stephen Curtis is involved as a member of Pi Sigma Alpha and
serves UTC Campus Recreation as a lifeguard and swim instructor; and
WHEREAS, he previously served Campus Recreation as a logistical and staff
development student lead and a safety and risk management student lead, as well as the
Political Science Department as a research and teaching assistant; he participated in the UTC
Student Government Association, which he served as chief of staff; and
WHEREAS, prior to his current work with the legislature, Mr. Curtis participated in the
internship program for the campaign of Roe Elliot for the Chattanooga City Council; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Stephen Curtis brought his many

abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2025 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2025 legislative session, Mr. Curtis has surpassed all criteria
that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Stephen Curtis has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight
and rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby
commend Stephen Curtis for outstanding service as a legislative intern for Chairman John
Crawford and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
