SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 146
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Edwena Lorraine Crowe
of Oak Ridge.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Edwena Lorraine Crowe; and
WHEREAS, Edwena Crowe was an exemplary individual who worked assiduously to
improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Crowe was also deeply devoted to her family, and she always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, she enjoyed the loving companionship of her devoted husband, Charles
Crowe, and was the proud mother of Nicholas (Jameria) Crowe and Rebecca (Phillip) Hamilton
and beloved grandmother of three grandsons, Pierce, Reece Crowe, and Jordan Hamilton; and
WHEREAS, she will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by her brother, William
Dunbar; her sister-in-law, Connie Johnson; her nieces and nephews, Edward Dunbar, Martha
Richards, Vickey Lewis, John Dunbar, Kim Dunbar, and Joseph Scales; and a host of extended
family members, adopted children, and dear friends; and
WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her parents, Edward Coneser and Martha
Elizabeth Williams Dunbar, and her brothers, Edward Cleveland Dunbar, Sr., and John Wayne
Dunbar; and
WHEREAS, Edwena Crowe leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pause to remember the bountiful life of this
exceptional citizen and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
SJR0146
001850
- 1 -
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Edwena Lorraine Crowe,
reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mrs. Crowe.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
