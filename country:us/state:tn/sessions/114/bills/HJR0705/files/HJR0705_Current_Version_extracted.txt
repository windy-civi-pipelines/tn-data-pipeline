HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 705
By Carringer
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Costas "Gus" Brinias of
Knoxville.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Costas "Gus" Brinias;
and
WHEREAS, Gus Brinias was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Kesari, Korinthia, Greece, on January 9, 1931, Mr. Brinias was the
son of Dimitrios and Athanasia Brinias; he immigrated to Montreal, Canada, in the early 1950s,
working on a farm for many years to support his family in Greece and eventually transitioning to
work in the restaurant industry; and
WHEREAS, he and his brother, Frank, opened their first restaurant together in Montreal,
operating it for many years; after he met his beloved companion Voula, the two were wed in
1966 and moved their family to Knoxville in the early 1970s to pursue the American Dream; and
WHEREAS, Gus Brinias worked tirelessly to provide a better life for his family, becoming
a well-known restaurateur in Knoxville; he owned and operated Gus's Restaurant, located on
Gay Street, from 1974 to 1988, as well as The Original Louis Drive-In Restaurant, located on
Old Broadway, which he owned and operated until his retirement in 2005; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his passion for cooking, he enjoyed gardening and traveling;
he was a devoted member of Saint George Greek Orthodox Church and volunteered his time
and talents at the Greek Fest each year; and
WHEREAS, Gus Brinias was deeply devoted to his family; he loved spending time with
his wife and children and spoiling his grandchildren; and

WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his loving wife of fifty-
nine years, Voula Andriopoulos Brinias; son, Jimmy (Tammy) Brinias; daughter, Cathy (Bryan)
Johnson; grandchildren, Maria Brinias, Eleni Brinias, Dean Johnson, Linzy Johnson; and
Michael (Claire) Adams; great-grandchildren, Aidan Adams and Caroline Adams; special
nephew, Dr. Anthony Captain; and several godchildren, nieces, and nephews; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his brothers, Vlasi
Brinias and Frank Brinias, and sister, Vivian Captain; and
WHEREAS, Gus Brinias leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Costas "Gus" Brinias, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction. We extend our sympathy and offer our condolences to the family of Mr. Brinias.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
