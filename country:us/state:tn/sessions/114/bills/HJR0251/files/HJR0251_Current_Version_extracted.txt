HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 251
By Kumar
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Marjorie Ann Bowser
"Marjie" Mahar.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
remarkable efforts, have distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all be
proud; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Marjorie Ann Bowser "Marjie" Mahar, a
model citizen whose every endeavor exhibits her commitment to tradition, honor, faith, and
freedom; and
WHEREAS, born in New Kensington, Pennsylvania, Marjie Mahar is the youngest of
eight children born to Warren and Virginia Bowser; she attended a private Christian high school
in South Carolina and continued her education in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, to become a
licensed practical nurse; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Mahar also studied in Grand Rapids, Michigan, at what is now called
Cornerstone University, where she met her husband, Robert; the two have been married for
nearly fifty-six years and have been blessed with four children and their spouses, five
grandchildren, and grand puppies; and
WHEREAS, Marjie and Robert Mahar are active members of Temple Baptist Church in
White House; Mrs. Mahar is also a proud member of the Daughters of the American Revolution
General Jethro Sumner Chapter, which she current currently serves as chaplain; and
WHEREAS, Marjie Mahar is extremely proud of her rich heritage, which she traces back
to the Revolutionary War; before, during, and after the Korean War, four of her five brothers
served in the military, as well as her two brothers-in-law; and

WHEREAS, one of her brothers, Private First Class Roland Bowser, gave the ultimate
sacrifice, as he was captured in North Korea on November 2, 1950, and spent the remaining
months of his young life as a prisoner of war, until his death in June 1951; and
WHEREAS, to honor his memory, Mrs. Mahar read a letter she had written to her
deceased brother at the War Memorial in Seoul, South Korea, on Memorial Day in 2019; a copy
of her letter, written in Korean, was placed on the chair of every guest, so that, as she read her
letter aloud, the Korean War veterans and military members would be able to read it in their own
language; and
WHEREAS, she believes one of her missions in life is to keep Roland's memory alive by
sharing her family's story, sharing the poignant epic of family, love of God and country, sacrifice,
and America's contribution to freedom throughout the world; and
WHEREAS, the success of Tennessee, the strength of our communities, and the overall
vitality of our society depend, in great measure, on the dedication of people like Marjie Mahar,
who use their considerable talents and resources to serve others; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Mrs. Mahar for her many
contributions to honoring service members and serving those in her community; and
WHEREAS, Marjie Mahar is a public-spirited citizen of the highest order and most
worthy of our approbation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Marjorie Ann Bowser "Marjie" Mahar for her many
contributions to those in this State and beyond, extending our best wishes for her every
continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to

the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
