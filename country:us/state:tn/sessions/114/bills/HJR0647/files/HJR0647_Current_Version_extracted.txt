HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 647
By Camper
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Kimberley Nicole Maclin
of Memphis.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the untimely passing of Kimberley Nicole Maclin; and
WHEREAS, Kimberley Maclin was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, a 2001 graduate of Kingsbury High School, Kim Maclin loved to travel,
shop, and decorate; most of all, she loved spending time with her family; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Maclin served with acumen and alacrity in an assistant manager/human
resources position for the Paradies Shops of Memphis International Airport for nineteen years,
in which position she was respected for the exemplary quality of her work; and
WHEREAS, Kim Maclin was baptized at an early age at New Sherron Baptist Church,
and she held strong to her faith throughout her life; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to her family, Kimberley Maclin always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Maclin is survived by her parents, Wallace and Brenda Maclin; her
grandmothers, Bettye Brown and Joella Maclin; her siblings, Terrance (Yoshamira) Maclin,
Tamika Adkins, Shondra Brown, and Ashley Maclin; and ten nieces, six nephews, one great-
nephew, and a host of aunts, uncles, cousins, and friends; and
WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her grandfathers, Benjamin Maclin and
James Brown; her aunts, Cynthia Sutton and Jean Hollis; her cousin, Cortez Sutton; and her
uncle, Bobby Maclin; and

WHEREAS, Kimberley Maclin leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity
in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Kimberley Nicole Maclin, reflecting fondly upon
her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage
and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Ms. Maclin.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
