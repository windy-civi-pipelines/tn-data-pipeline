HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 560
By Barrett
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Virginia Gray of Dickson
County.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Virginia Gray; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Gray was a beloved member of her community who worked
assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, the daughter of Warren and Edith Rosenbalm, Virginia Gray was born on
March 6, 1940, and raised alongside her older brother, Donald, in humble beginnings but with
big dreams; and
WHEREAS, she met the love of her life, Earl Gray, while in college; the young couple
married in 1963 and built a beautiful life together on Gray's Hilltop Farm in Dickson County, their
partnership marked by laughter, hard work, shared values, and an abiding love for one another;
and
WHEREAS, a proud graduate of Dickson High School, where she was voted "Most
Intellectual," and of Austin Peay State University, where she earned two degrees, Virginia Gray
dedicated her life to learning and sharing knowledge as a gifted educator, serving more than
three decades in Dickson County Schools as a teacher, supervisor, and principal; and
WHEREAS, even after taking a well-deserved retirement, Mrs. Gray remained
committed to teaching, as she tutored Japanese families and remained deeply engaged in her
community; and
WHEREAS, Virginia Gray was a civic-minded leader, giving generously of her time and
talents as a Dickson County commissioner, Rotary Club president, chair of the Dickson County
Retired Teachers Association scholarship committee, member of the Library Board, and
member of the Renaissance Choir; and
WHEREAS, her legendary gatherings—from her famous Thanksgiving feasts that
welcomed fifty or more guests to the unforgettable Gray Boys Old Time Wheat Threshing

events—brought people together with warmth, music, food, and storytelling, creating lasting
memories and deep connections; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Gray was renowned for her homemade biscuits, fried chicken, sharp
wit, artistic talents, and the ability to find and share joy in the everyday, especially through
stories of life on the farm; and
WHEREAS, left to cherish her memory are adoring husband, Earl; daughter, Ginny; son-
in-law, Craig; and grandson, Gray; she also cherished her extended family of brothers- and
sisters-in-law, nieces, nephews, and many friends; and
WHEREAS, Virginia Gray leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Virginia Gray, reflecting fondly upon her
impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mrs. Gray.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
