SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 338
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor Brylan Rhodes of Greeneville High
School on winning the 2025 TSSAA Division I,
Class A State wrestling championship in the
106-pound weight class.
WHEREAS, we take great pleasure in recognizing those dedicated student athletes who
serve as the heart and soul of their schools and communities while wearing the crown of a
champion; and
WHEREAS, Brylan Rhodes, a freshman at Greeneville High School, recently won the
2025 TSSAA Division I, Class A State wrestling championship in the 106-pound weight class at
the TSSAA State wrestling tournament held in Franklin; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Rhodes demolished his competition on the way to claiming his first state
crown, pinning Samuel Everett School of Innovation's Colton Jenkins in 59 seconds and
Stratford High School's Jeremiah Fesmire in 3:14, before pinning Signal Mountain High School's
Luke Higdon in 5:52 to reach the title match against Upperman High School's Jacob Stinnett,
whom he defeated in a 10-4 decision to claim the crown; and
WHEREAS, Brylan Rhodes epitomizes all that is good in today's student athlete, as he
has achieved a premier level of success in competition while exhibiting the highest degree of
character in all aspects of life; and
WHEREAS, his perseverance, determination, work ethic, and talent not only made
possible a championship season but also surely bode well for his future success; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Brylan Rhodes as an
exceptional state champion of whom we can all be proud; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
SJR0338
006080
- 1 -
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Brylan Rhodes of
Greeneville High School on winning the 2025 TSSAA Division I, Class A State wrestling
championship in the 106-pound weight class and wish him all the best in future competition.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
