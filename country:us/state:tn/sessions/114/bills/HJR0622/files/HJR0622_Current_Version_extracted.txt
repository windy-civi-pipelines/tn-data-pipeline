HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 622
By Grills
A RESOLUTION to commemorate Child Abuse Awareness
Month, April 2025.
WHEREAS, our children are our most valuable resources and will shape the future of
our State; and
WHEREAS, child abuse and neglect are serious and growing problems affecting every
segment of our community, and finding solutions requires input and action from everyone; and
WHEREAS, child abuse affects the lives of nearly four million children annually and
often results in long-term psychological, emotional, and physical effects that have lasting
consequences for victims of abuse; and
WHEREAS, having reached epidemic proportions in all regions of our nation, child
abuse respects no racial, religious, socio-economic, or geographical boundaries and can
prevent children from following the road to a successful future; and
WHEREAS, effective child abuse prevention activities and training succeed because of
the partnerships created between child welfare professionals, education, health, community-
and faith-based organizations, businesses, law enforcement agencies, and families; and
WHEREAS, we must work together as a community to increase awareness about child
abuse and contribute to promoting the social and emotional well-being of children and families
in a safe, stable, and nurturing environment; and
WHEREAS, the Carl Perkins Center for the Prevention of Child Abuse, through its
parent training, educational programs, and community service activities, supports the
community in preventing and dealing with child abuse to improve the quality of life for our
children and families; now, therefore,
HJR0622
007022
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we join with the dedicated professionals of the Carl Perkins Center for the
Prevention of Child Abuse as they commemorate April 2025 as Child Abuse Awareness Month.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we commend the Carl Perkins Center for the
Prevention of Child Abuse for its continued success in helping families break free from the cycle
of abuse.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
