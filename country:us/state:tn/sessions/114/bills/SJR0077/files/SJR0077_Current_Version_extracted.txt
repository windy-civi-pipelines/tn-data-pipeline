SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 77
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to recognize Coach Jason Lowe on the occasion
of his retirement.
WHEREAS, this General Assembly is honored to recognize those coaches and
educators throughout the State who have spent years in unflagging pursuit of victory, inspiring a
countless number of Tennessee's students to succeed, both as athletes and as scholars; and
WHEREAS, one such person is Jason Lowe, who has served with distinction for the past
eight years as head baseball coach at North Greene High School and recently announced his
retirement as a coach; and
WHEREAS, Coach Jason Lowe established himself as the heart and soul of the North
Greene High School baseball program during his tenure and guided the Huskies to
unprecedented heights of achievement on the diamond; and
WHEREAS, under Coach Lowe's guidance, the North Greene Huskies reached the state
tournament twice and the final four in 2021; and
WHEREAS, Jason Lowe enjoyed seventeen years as a coach at both the middle and
high school levels and leaves North Greene with an impressive 135-82 coaching record; and
WHEREAS, Coach Lowe epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of
a true Tennessean; he has served with great diligence and dedication, positively influencing the
lives of countless young people with his display of character and integrity; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Jason Lowe for his years
of coaching excellence and his contributions to baseball in Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Coach Jason Lowe for his
outstanding achievements as head baseball coach at North Greene High School and extend to
him our heartfelt wishes for every continued success in his future endeavors.

- 1 -
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
