Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 54, Chapter 5, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
If the commissioner seeks to exercise any power of condemnation by eminent
domain that the department has under law for the purpose of acquiring any lands or
estates, or interests therein, for a right-of-way, then the commissioner shall include in
any petition or other complaint seeking the condemnation, or in an amendment to the
petition or complaint, a complete and accurate metes and bounds description of that
portion of the owner's lands, estate, or interests therein, and a clear and precise
description of the property rights, sought to be condemned. A lawful taking or
appropriation, by order of possession of, or transfer of title to, the lands or estates or
interests therein must not occur until after a petition or amendment meeting the
requirements of this section has been filed and served upon the owner. The
determination of the date of lawful taking or appropriation and calculation of the
compensation to which the owner is entitled to be paid in the condemnation proceeding
must take into consideration the date on which the petition or amendment was served
pursuant to this section and the description provided in the petition or amendment.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
