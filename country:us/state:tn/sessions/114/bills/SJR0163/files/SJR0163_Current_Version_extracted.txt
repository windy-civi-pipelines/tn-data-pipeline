SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 163
By Oliver
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Haynes Park
Neighborhood Association on the occasion of its
twentieth anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we pay tribute to those exemplary organizations that work
with purpose and conviction to improve the quality of life in their respective communities; and
WHEREAS, one such esteemed organization is the Haynes Park Neighborhood
Association, which is committed to making its community a vibrant, beautiful, and safe
neighborhood where all families can flourish economically and in harmony; and
WHEREAS, this esteemed group seeks to (1) promote good will and communication
amongst neighborhood residents; (2) build a community network to work together to find
solutions to community problems; (3) focus on environmental controls to promote safety and
beautification; (4) discourage crime and crime-related activities and any situation that may be
hazardous or unhealthy to persons living within the community; and (5) promote community
involvement; and
WHEREAS, the Neighborhood Infrastructure-Block Captains of the Haynes Park
Neighborhood Association are Michelle Matlock, Haynes Park Court; Regina Baugh, Eve Court;
Erma Johnson, Walters Court; Al and Annie Batson, East Fairview Court/Boyce Court; Andrew
and Gloria Ward, Kingview Drive/Dyer Court; Gina Coleman, Haynes Park/Charles Court; and
Kelsey and Jordan, Kingview Court; and
WHEREAS, at their recent anniversary celebration, the members of the Haynes Park
Neighborhood Association paid respectful tribute to the memories of their late neighbors: Ruby
Patterson, Reverend Dennie Sweatt, Jr., Junelle Wilson, Dawson Smith, Loretta Smith, Henry
Cotton, Eve Cotton, Sammie Pointer, Napolean Pointer, Steve Dickerson, and Robert Snell; and

WHEREAS, organizations such as the Haynes Park Neighborhood Association take
compassionate action to ensure that their fellow Tennesseans have the opportunity to thrive;
and
WHEREAS, it is our privilege to honor this most venerable group on this noteworthy
occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Haynes Park
Neighborhood Association on the occasion of the group's twentieth anniversary, extending our
gratitude for its continued contributions to the community and our best wishes for every
continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
