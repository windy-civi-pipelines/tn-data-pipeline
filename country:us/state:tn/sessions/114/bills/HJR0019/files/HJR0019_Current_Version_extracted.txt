HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 19
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to honor Tom "Doc" Weddle on completing five
U.S. Super Slams in the sport of wild turkey
hunting.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing a
skilled outdoorsman who has attained the very pinnacle of his chosen sport; and
WHEREAS, Tom "Doc" Weddle of Bloomington, Indiana, has dedicated most of his free
time over the past forty years to perfecting the art and science of wild turkey hunting; and
WHEREAS, after harvesting his first turkey in 1983, Mr. Weddle immediately fell in love
with the sport and has since become an expert hunter of the wily and elusive birds; and
WHEREAS, each spring, he takes off three months from his work as a carpenter to
pursue his passion and a rare hunting feat known as the U.S. Super Slam; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Weddle has completed an incredible number of U.S. Super Slams, five
times harvesting a turkey in each of the forty-nine states that have a wild turkey hunting season;
and
WHEREAS, he has also recorded numerous Grand Slams by bagging all four
subspecies of turkey and several Double Grand Slams by taking two birds of each type; and
WHEREAS, a gifted caller, Mr. Weddle relishes both the challenge of the hunt and the
thrill of interacting with the wild turkey; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his bow and shotgun, Doc Weddle has adopted the pen as a
valuable tool; he has written four volumes of Turkey Tails and Tales from Across the U.S.A., in
which he intersperses valuable advice on turkey hunting with stories about the many exciting
adventures that have made him the hunter and conservationist he is today; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Weddle has distinguished himself as a hunter of the greatest ability,
commitment, and patience and a paragon of the wild turkey hunting community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Tom "Doc" Weddle on completing five U.S.
Super Slams in the sport of wild turkey hunting and extend to him our best wishes for happy
hunting in the years to come.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
