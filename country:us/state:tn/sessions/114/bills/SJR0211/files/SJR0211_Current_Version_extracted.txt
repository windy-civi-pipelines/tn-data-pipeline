SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 211
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to recognize Garrett McNeill on becoming an
Eagle Scout.
WHEREAS, at a time when this nation's youth face difficult challenges, the Boy Scouts
of America is one of our finest assets, providing young people with an educational program that
contributes significantly to their character development, citizenship training, and improved
mental and physical fitness; and
WHEREAS, the highest rank a Scout can attain in the Boy Scouts of America Scouts
BSA program is that of Eagle Scout, and Garrett McNeill, after considerable effort and diligent
preparation, has joined the prestigious Eagle Court of Honor; and
WHEREAS, Garrett began his service with the Cub Scouts, where he earned the Arrow
of Light and transitioned to Troop 16; he embraced challenges, earning merit badges,
participating in outdoor activities, and even traveling to the Northern Tier for a six-day canoeing
expedition to gain skills and resilience; and
WHEREAS, for his Eagle project, he constructed two dog houses for the Williamson
County Animal Shelter to donate to families who would like to adopt dogs but cannot afford a
dog house; the endeavor, which required ninety-five hours of volunteer service, greatly
contributed to the betterment of his community and reflected the true spirit of volunteerism; and
WHEREAS, within his troop, Garrett McNeill served as a patrol leader and scribe and
demonstrated character leadership and a strong commitment to serving his community; and
WHEREAS, currently a football player at Summit High School and baseball player with
the Franklin Baseball Club, Garrett McNeill actively participates in Fellowship of Christian
Athletes and Young Life; he has demonstrated servant leadership through his participation in his

church's youth mission trips, during which he has engaged with and provided food for the
homeless, assisted with construction projects, and completed yard work tasks; and
WHEREAS, after high school, Garrett plans to attend college and major in aerospace
and professional piloting; he also hopes to obtain his commercial pilot's license; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should pause to recognize Garrett McNeill as one of
the most outstanding young people in our State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate Garrett McNeill
on becoming an Eagle Scout and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
