SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 231
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Dr. Erika Stevens
upon her receipt of the Distance Learning
Teaching Award for Higher Education from the
American Council on the Teaching of Foreign
Languages.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as educational leaders of whom we can all
be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Dr. Erika Stevens, who recently received the Distance
Learning Teaching Award for Higher Education from the American Council on the Teaching of
Foreign Languages in recognition of her dedication to foreign language education; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Stevens currently serves with acumen and alacrity as a professor of
Spanish at Walters State Community College, where she has established a Spanish academic
certificate program that has attracted both current students and students who are not affiliated
with the school; and
WHEREAS, the certificate, which expands the role of learning world languages at a
community college, can be taken completely online and features enhanced security features;
and
WHEREAS, as a recipient of the Distance Learning Teaching Award, Dr. Stevens will
teach a webinar titled Designing Online World Language Courses to Meet the Needs of
Students and the Community for members of the American Council on the Teaching of Foreign
Languages; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Stevens epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of
a true Tennessean; and
SJR0231
004692
- 1 -
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly find it appropriate to acknowledge
and applaud Dr. Erika Stevens for her dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate Dr. Erika
Stevens upon her receipt of the Distance Learning Teaching Award for Higher Education from
the American Council on the Teaching of Foreign Languages and extend to her our best wishes
for continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
