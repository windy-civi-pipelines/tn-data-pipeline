HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 123
By Farmer
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Honorable Norma
McGee Ogle for her distinguished service as a
member of the Tennessee Court of Criminal
Appeals.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those jurists who have performed with
uncommon devotion and probity throughout their years on the bench; and
WHEREAS, the Honorable Norma McGee Ogle is one such individual, who, after an
illustrious judicial career, took her well-earned retirement from the Tennessee Court of Criminal
Appeals; and
WHEREAS, born in Lawrenceburg, Judge Ogle graduated from Loretto High School and
the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1974;
she went on to earn her Juris Doctor degree from the University of Tennessee College of Law in
1977 and was admitted to the Tennessee Bar shortly thereafter; and
WHEREAS, early in her career, Judge Ogle was employed with Memphis Area Legal
Services from 1977 to 1979, and she was in private practice from 1979 to 1998; she served as
the city attorney for Pigeon Forge from 1990 to 1998, and she also served as the city attorney
for Pittman Center; and
WHEREAS, in November 1998, the judiciary became the beneficiary of Judge Ogle's
wisdom and expertise when Governor Don Sundquist appointed her as a judge on the
Tennessee Court of Criminal Appeals; and
WHEREAS, she holds the distinction of being the first female judge of that honorable
court, and she continued there as a judge until her retirement on June 30, 2022; and

WHEREAS, Judge Ogle's work has left a positive lasting impact on the Tennessee Court
of Criminal Appeals and the citizens of Tennessee, and even today, she continues to serve as a
mentor and role model to many; and
WHEREAS, Judge Ogle is a member of the Tennessee Bar Association, the Sevier
County Bar Association, the Knoxville Bar Association, the Sevier County Library Foundation,
and the Advisory Board for Walters State Community College; and
WHEREAS, her tremendous record of public service includes tenures as a member of
the State of Tennessee Local Planning Advisory Board from 1997 to 1998; the Board of Judicial
Conduct from 2015 to 2018; the Tennessee Human Rights Commission from 1995 to 1998,
serving as a commissioner and chairperson; and the Sevier County Board of Education from
1982 to 1994, serving as member and chairperson; and
WHEREAS, an active part of her community, she has delivered meals to homebound
seniors for Knoxville Mobile Meals since 1999 and is a longtime member of First United
Methodist Church of Sevierville; and
WHEREAS, although Judge Ogle's commitment to the great State of Tennessee is self-
evident and supremely honorable, her greatest love is and has always been her family: her
parents, Norman and Jimmie McGee; her husband, Judge Rex Henry Ogle; her son and
daughter-in-law, Daniel and Erin Ogle; and her granddaughter, Georgia Ogle; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we applaud and commend the extraordinary talents,
character, and career of such a remarkable human being, who personified grace, compassion,
and wisdom and whose contributions to the judiciary and to the people of the great State of
Tennessee will always be appreciated; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Honorable Norma McGee Ogle for more than

twenty-three years of service on the bench in service to the good people of the State of
Tennessee and extend to her our best wishes for every future success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
