Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-6-2206(a), is amended by
deleting the subsection and substituting the following as a new subsection (a):
(a) An LEA shall not use or permit to be used in any school, including virtual
schools, any textbooks and instructional materials upon any subject to the exclusion of
the textbooks and instructional materials listed by the commission and approved by the
state board of education; provided, that this prohibition does not apply to textbooks and
instructional materials previously listed and purchased with public funds. Upon
application of the local board of education, the state board of education may waive this
restriction for any school, including virtual schools, when, in the state board’s judgement,
the unique or unusual needs of the LEA require it. In making waiver determinations, the
state board of education must receive assistance from the department of education. The
state board shall outline in its rules specific timeframes when waiver applications may be
submitted by a local board of education for any school, including virtual schools;
provided, that the state board of education shall allow applications to be submitted
outside of the established timeframes in emergency circumstance, as defined by the
state board in its rules. The state board is authorized to establish a separate timeframe
for virtual schools to submit a waiver application pursuant to subsection (e). A public
charter school may request a waiver for the use of textbooks and instructional materials
in accordance with § 49-13-111.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-6-2206, is amended by adding
the following as a new, appropriately designated subsection:
(1) All virtual schools established by an LEA shall use a curriculum and
instructional materials that meet or exceed the academic standards adopted by the state
board of education. Notwithstanding subsection (a), the state board of education shall
not waive this requirement upon the application of a virtual school.
(2)
(A) Each LEA that has established a virtual school may apply to the state
board of education for a waiver from the prohibition in subsection (a) against
using textbooks and instructional materials upon any subject to the exclusion of
the textbooks and instructional materials listed by the commission and approved
by the state board of education. In addition to any other information required by
the state board of education to complete a waiver application, the LEA shall
include a verification of the academic standards adopted by the state board of
education alignment for the courses requested in the waiver application. The
state board of education shall review each completed waiver application and, if
the verification of academic standards alignment included with the application is
complete and meets or exceeds the academic standards adopted by the state
board of education, approve the application for use of the virtual school
instructional materials submitted. The state board may promulgate rules to
effectuate the implementation of this waiver application for virtual school
instructional materials. The rules must be promulgated in accordance with the
Uniform Administrative Procedures Act, compiled in title 4, chapter 5.
(B) The department of education shall post on its website all waiver
applications that are approved by the state board of education under subdivision
( )(2)(A).
