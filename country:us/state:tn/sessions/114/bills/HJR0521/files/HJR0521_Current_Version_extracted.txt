HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 521
By Marsh
A RESOLUTION honor and commend R. Kinney Poynter, CPA,
for his distinguished service to the state
government accountability community and, more
specifically, for his dedicated contributions to the
State of Tennessee's Annual Government
Auditing Training Seminar.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly recognize the importance of
leadership, commitment, and exceptional service in advancing the integrity, transparency, and
effectiveness of government financial oversight; and
WHEREAS, R. Kinney Poynter, since joining the National Association of State Auditors,
Comptrollers, and Treasurers (NASACT) in 1989 and ascending to executive director in 2003,
has championed government accountability, transparency, and efficiency, fostering
intergovernmental cooperation across federal, state, and local levels; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Poynter's visionary leadership and steadfast advocacy have
strengthened the role of auditors nationwide, fostering collaboration, professional development,
and the adoption of best practices that benefit state and local governments and the citizens they
serve; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Poynter has provided invaluable guidance, planning, insight, and
expertise to the State of Tennessee's Annual Government Auditing Training Seminar, enriching
the educational experience of countless auditing professionals and enhancing the state's
commitment to effective oversight and governance; and
WHEREAS, his contributions to the auditor community have not only elevated
professional standards but also inspired a culture of excellence and innovation among public
servants; and

WHEREAS, in 2024, Kinney Poynter was honored with the prestigious National State
Auditors Association's William R. Snodgrass Distinguished Leadership Award, which reflects his
unwavering commitment to excellence in public financial management; and
WHEREAS, after a long and distinguished career with NASACT, Mr. Poynter retired,
effective March 31, 2025; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting and proper that the members of this General Assembly recognize
Mr. Poynter for his outstanding service and enduring legacy of leadership within the field of
government financial accountability; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend R. Kinney Poynter for his exemplary service to
the state auditor community, his substantial contributions to the State of Tennessee's Annual
Government Auditing Seminar, and his unwavering commitment to fostering accountability,
transparency, and excellence in public service.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
