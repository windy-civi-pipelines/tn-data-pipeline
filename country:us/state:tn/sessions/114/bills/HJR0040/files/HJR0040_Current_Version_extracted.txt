HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 40
By Love
A RESOLUTION to honor Inez Gibbs Crutchfield of Nashville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Inez Gibbs Crutchfield; and
WHEREAS, Inez Crutchfield was a beloved Nashville social justice icon and educator
who spent her life being a voice for the most vulnerable within her community and working for
equal rights for all; and
WHEREAS, with a passion for civic engagement and an unwavering dedication to her
quest for equality, Mrs. Crutchfield witnessed firsthand Civil Rights history in Nashville and
claimed many firsts as an African-American woman; and
WHEREAS, born into a family of sharecroppers, Inez Crutchfield was raised in
Watertown by her father, Dee Gibbs, a World War I veteran and businessman, and her mother,
Bessie, an educator; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Crutchfield earned her bachelor's and master's degrees in health
education from Tennessee A&I State College, now Tennessee State University; and
WHEREAS, she went on to serve as an assistant professor of health education at
Tennessee State University and worked in the health and physical education department for
nearly forty years; it was this position that brought her closer to the 1960 student sit-ins during
the Civil Rights Movement; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Crutchfield was proud to offer students a solid support system during
the sit-ins; she was known to transport her students to meetings to organize demonstrations,
deliver food when students were arrested, and comfort students' parents; and

WHEREAS, a highly respected member of her community, Inez Crutchfield served as a
member of a federal judicial selection committee and the board of the Bethlehem Center; she
was also a member of the board at Meharry Medical College, where she helped guide the
school's merger with Metro General Hospital; and
WHEREAS, Inez Crutchfield played a critical role in breaking race barriers, especially
within the Davidson County Democratic Party Women's Club; she began as a member, and in
1975, she became the first African-American president of the Club; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Crutchfield went on to serve as Tennessee's first African-American
woman representative on the Democratic National Committee and the first African American to
hold an appointed and elected statewide position in the Tennessee State Federation of
Democratic Women; and
WHEREAS, she remained active in politics even after her retirement, and in 2008, she
served as a superdelegate supporting the then-future President Barack Obama; and
WHEREAS, although she generally opted to do her work behind the scenes, Inez
Crutchfield was often credited with the political successes of several individuals; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Mrs. Crutchfield was the first African-
American woman to receive the Athena Award in 1994, a most befitting honor that recognized
her professional excellence; more recently, she received the Joe Kraft Humanitarian Award in
2019 in recognition of her commitment to community, home and hearth, and individuals in need;
and
WHEREAS, Inez Crutchfield was deeply devoted to her family, and she always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Crutchfield enjoyed many years of marriage to the love of her life, Carl
Crutchfield, and together, the couple were blessed with two cherished children, Carlton and
Beth; and
- 2 - 001355
WHEREAS, the epitome of perseverance and generosity, Inez Crutchfield was a wise
and altruistic soul who impacted the lives of those who were blessed to know her, and she
leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public life, compassion and loyalty in
private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Crutchfield's quest for equality and her tireless efforts to bring about a
more just, humane society will continue to inspire generations to come; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we should pay tribute to her bountiful life of purpose
and service; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Inez Gibbs Crutchfield, reflecting fondly upon her
enduring legacy in Nashville and beyond.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
