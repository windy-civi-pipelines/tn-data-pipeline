SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 143
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Michael Timme,
executive director of the Tennessee Advisory
Commission on Intergovernmental Relations.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Michael Timme, executive director of the Tennessee Advisory Commission on
Intergovernmental Relations; and
WHEREAS, Michael Timme, throughout his exemplary thirty-five-year tenure with the
State of Tennessee, distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the highest order and as
an exceptional asset to his community and his State; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Timme was born in Olean, New York, to Nancy and the late Maynard
Timme but moved to Tennessee in 1985 to attend Middle Tennessee State University, where he
earned his Bachelor of Business Administration and Master of Business Administration degrees;
and
WHEREAS, in 1990, Mr. Timme began his career with the State of Tennessee as an
employee of the Office of the Comptroller of the Treasury, Division of State Audit, where he
worked for two years before moving to the Department of Finance and Administration to serve
as manager of grants tracking and later as the deputy director of contracts review; and
WHEREAS, he joined the staff of the Department of Education in 2002, serving as the
director of contracts and purchasing for three years before moving in 2005 to the Tennessee
Advisory Commission on Intergovernmental Relations, where he faithfully served as the director
of administration; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his work as a state employee, Mr. Timme worked as an
instructor for the National College of Business and Technology for eleven years; and
WHEREAS, Michael Timme, known for his wry wit and sense of humor, was often heard
telling staff, "I keep the thermostat in the office on cool to keep the comedy fresh"; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Timme hosted his own YouTube channel of music videos of him playing
the guitar or ukulele and other videos featuring his band, The Ukedelics; and
WHEREAS, he recorded heartwarming music videos with family members, particularly
with his beloved mother, Nancy, who is ninety-four, that can be viewed to brighten one's day;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Timme often entertained staff members at office lunches by playing his
Collings OM1 Pete Huttlinger limited edition acoustic guitar. His herd of archtops included a
Gibson ES-165 and a Washburn J-600, as well as an Eastman; and
WHEREAS, as an outdoor enthusiast, Michael Timme paused to enjoy the hiking,
camping, and beautiful scenery offered by Tennessee and states across the nation; and
WHEREAS, his many accomplishments aside, Michael Timme was fortunate to spend
time with his beloved mother Nancy; his children, Ruth, Clara, Anna, and Carl; grandchildren,
Ivy, Abigail, Maddie, Edie, Ruby, and Eloise; and his loving partner, Carolyn Breda; and
WHEREAS, Michael Timme was wholly committed to the noble precepts of public
service and ensuring the efficient and effective use of public funds, and he should be specially
remembered and recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Michael Timme,
commending his many years of distinguished and astute service to the good people of the State
of Tennessee and his stalwart commitment to a job well done.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Timme.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
