HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 636
By McKenzie
A RESOLUTION to honor and recognize Al'isha Roach, 2025
Salutatorian of Austin-East Magnet High School.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to pay
tribute to those students who, through their natural talents and unflagging commitment to
academic excellence, have distinguished themselves in the classroom; and
WHEREAS, Al'isha Roach is one such exceptional student, who was recently named the
Salutatorian of the 2025 graduating class at Austin-East Magnet High School; and
WHEREAS, through diligent study, steady composure during examinations, and
steadfast commitment of time and talent, Ms. Roach has achieved outstanding academic
success and joined the ranks of the best and brightest students in Tennessee and our country;
and
WHEREAS, in her remarkable high school career, Al'isha Roach has earned an
exemplary grade point average while participating in numerous extracurricular activities; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Roach's display of intellectual aptitude and ambition surely bodes well
for her future success; and
WHEREAS, Al'isha Roach's noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for
her family, her school, and her community; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Roach's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of the respect,
admiration, and commendation of this General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend and honor the 2025 Austin-East Magnet High School
Salutatorian, Al'isha Roach, on her impressive academic achievement and extend to her our
best wishes for a happy, successful future.
HJR0636
007302
- 1 -
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
