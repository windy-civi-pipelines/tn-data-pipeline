SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 314
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to recognize the K-25 site in Oak Ridge upon its
designation as a National Historic Chemical
Landmark by the American Chemical Society.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those sites of historical, scientific, and technological significance that have contributed to the
advancement of our State and nation; and
WHEREAS, the K-25 site, located in Oak Ridge, was a pivotal facility in the Manhattan
Project and played a crucial role in the separation of uranium isotopes using gaseous diffusion
technology; and
WHEREAS, the American Chemical Society has officially designated the K-25 site as a
National Historic Chemical Landmark, recognizing its groundbreaking contributions to chemistry,
engineering, and national security; and
WHEREAS, this designation highlights the scientific and historical importance of the K-
25 site and serves to educate future generations about its role in the development of nuclear
energy and the broader field of chemistry; and
WHEREAS, the K-25 Gaseous Diffusion Plant was once the world's largest building
under one roof and employed thousands of Tennesseans, contributing to the nation's efforts in
World War II and the Cold War, while also fostering significant advancements in nuclear science
and technology; and
WHEREAS, the technologies pioneered at K-25 laid the foundation for the commercial
nuclear energy industry and contributed to U.S. leadership in nonproliferation policy by
advancing peaceful applications of nuclear technology and safeguarding materials worldwide;
and

006253
- 1 -
WHEREAS, the K-25 History Center and the recently constructed K-25 Viewing Platform
and Interpretive Center preserve and interpret the history of the site, showcasing over 250
artifacts and nearly 1,000 oral histories to educate the public on the significance of Oak Ridge's
scientific contributions; and
WHEREAS, we take great pride in the rich scientific legacy of Oak Ridge and its
continuing contributions to research, technology, and innovation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize the K-25 site in Oak Ridge upon its
designation as a National Historic Chemical Landmark by the American Chemical Society and
celebrate its lasting contributions to science, technology, and national security.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
