HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 665
By Littleton
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Norma Binkley on her
receipt of the Governor's Volunteer Stars Award.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should specially recognize those estimable citizens who
have dedicated their time and energies to helping others; and
WHEREAS, each year, 1.3 million Tennessee volunteers give over 99 million hours of
community service, contributing $2.6 billion to Tennessee's economy; and
WHEREAS, these volunteers are the backbone of our communities, and by donating
their time and talents to fulfill needs that would otherwise go unmet, they embody the spirit of
giving; and
WHEREAS, Norma Binkley of Cheatham County is one such exemplary person, who is
a recipient of the Governor's Volunteer Stars Award in recognition of her laudable commitment
to her community; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Binkley has devoted her time, talents, and resources to bringing
people together in her community in an effort to resolve the silent social crisis of loneliness; and
WHEREAS, to fulfill this mission, Mrs. Binkley, along with her husband, assumed
responsibility for the maintenance of a 1950s-era building, which had never been owned by
anyone and could not be sold; and
WHEREAS, Norma Binkley has been proud to coordinate events at the building that
have brought people together, giving individuals an opportunity to make new connections and
engage in activities that have ultimately made the community a better place to live; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Binkley is a model citizen; her commitment to helping others through
volunteerism epitomizes the spirit of the Volunteer State, and she should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
HJR0665
007692
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor Norma Binkley for her outstanding community leadership,
congratulate her as an esteemed recipient of the Governor's Volunteer Stars Award, and extend
to her our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
