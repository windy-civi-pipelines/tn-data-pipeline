HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 543
By Slater
A RESOLUTION to honor Joi Wasill upon receiving the Strong
and Courageous Award.
WHEREAS, this General Assembly takes great pleasure in recognizing an individual
who has demonstrated "strength and courage" in everyday life by utilizing her faith to positively
affect her work, her neighborhood, and her community; and
WHEREAS, Joi Wasill is the president and director of Resource Development for DCO,
an organization she has led for the past twenty-three years, serving over 280,000 students and
youth in Tennessee, Alabama, and other states with sexual risk avoidance/abstinence
education programs; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Wasill's career in education began in 1988, after she completed
teacher education certification courses at Middle Tennessee State University following the
receipt of her Bachelor of Public Administration degree from the University of Mississippi; and
WHEREAS, as a public and private school secondary education teacher with more than
three decades of experience, Joi Wasill understands the nature of the youth population and
curriculum and instruction implementation and evaluation; and
WHEREAS, in addition to holding a teacher's license from the State of Tennessee, she
is certified as a Sexual Risk Avoidance Education Specialist (SRAS) by the nation's top training
organization in the field of sexual risk avoidance education; and
WHEREAS, a leader in her field, Mrs. Wasill is a member of Protect Child Health
Coalition and has been a workshop leader and speaker at CareNet, the National Council for
Adoption, and Teach Them Diligently conferences; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Wasill has been duly recognized with a Who's Who in American
Educators distinction, Young Educator of the Year Award from the Hendersonville Chamber of

Commerce, the "Top 30 Most Influential People in Sumner County" distinction, the
Congressional Coalition on Adoption's Angel in Adoption Award, and the Impact Award on
behalf of DCO from the White House Chamber of Commerce; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Wasill enjoys the love and support of her husband of forty years and
local business owner, Bill, and their son, Jacob, and daughter-in-law, Caitlyn; Joi and Bill Wasill
have been active members of their church and community for many decades; and
WHEREAS, Joi Wasill has demonstrated strength and courage in the business and
professional community in Sumner County; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Wasill is committed to living her Christian faith to the fullest and
helping others do likewise, and she is worthy of our approbation on this special occasion; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Joi Wasill upon receiving the Strong and
Courageous Award from Welch College and extend to her our best wishes for much continued
success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
