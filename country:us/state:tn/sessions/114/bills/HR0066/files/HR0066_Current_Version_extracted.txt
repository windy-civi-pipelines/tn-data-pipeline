HOUSE RESOLUTION 66
By Sparks
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Douglas L. Atkin of
Smyrna.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body were greatly saddened to learn of the
passing of Douglas L. Atkin; and
WHEREAS, Doug Atkin was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, the son of Evelyn and Wendell Atkin, Doug Atkin graduated from
Willoughby Union High School in Willoughby, Ohio, in 1956; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Atkin was a talented musician; as a high school student, he played
drums in the Rangers Marching Band and in The Four Plaids, the area's first rock and roll band;
he continued to play music as an adult in the band "The Lamplighters"; and
WHEREAS, Doug Atkin evinced his religious faith at All Saints Episcopal Church in
Smyrna and St. Patrick's Anglican Church in Murfreesboro, both of which he served as a
founding member; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Mr. Atkin always endeavored to remain true to
family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his wife of sixty-six years, Linda Maitland Atkin; his sons,
Larry (Cindy) Atkin, Ken Atkin, Lucas Atkin, and Kerry Gleason; his grandchildren, Ashley
(Adam) Bond, Dustin (Morgan) Atkin, Christopher (Julie), Courtney (Eric) Bennett, and Robert
(Kandace); and his great-grandchildren, Addison, Alivia, Annlynn, Amelia, Lynnex, Caden,
Camren, Nathan, and Franke; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Atkin is further survived by his brother, Denny Atkin, and a host of
friends who loved him dearly; and

WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Doug Atkin was preceded in death by his
daughter, Melissa Renee, and his brother, Don (Barbara); and
WHEREAS, as a native of Ohio, Doug Atkin was an avid fan of the Cleveland Browns
and The Ohio State University football team and marching band; he loved coaching his sons'
baseball teams, playing drums and guitar, writing and performing music, and enjoying his
beagles; he was also a wonderful cook and often thought of authoring a cookbook; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Atkin leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this legislative body should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor the
memory of Douglas L. Atkin, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart
commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Atkin.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
