SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 375
By Hensley
A RESOLUTION to commend Nathan Walsh for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Nathan Walsh is one such individual, who has distinguished himself in his
role as a legislative intern for Senator Joey Hensley; Mr. Walsh's work has resulted in
innumerable benefits for the legislators he assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at Belmont University, where he is highly regarded by his peers,
Mr. Walsh has achieved academic success while pursuing a Bachelor of Science degree in
History; and
WHEREAS, while pursuing his undergraduate degree, Nathan Walsh has been an active
member of the History Society, Pre-Law Society, Model United Nations, Pre-Graduate Club, and
Belmont Symposium; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Walsh has also collaborated with Dr. Kuryla of Belmont University's
History Department in conducting research for Dr. Kuryla's book on the history of the American
jail; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Nathan Walsh brought his many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2025 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2025 legislative session, Mr. Walsh has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
SJR0375
006864
- 1 -
WHEREAS, Nathan Walsh has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Nathan Walsh for outstanding
service as a legislative intern for Senator Joey Hensley and extend to him our best wishes for
every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
