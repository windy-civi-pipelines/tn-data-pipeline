HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 515
By Jones R
A RESOLUTION to recognize and commend the sixty-fifth annual
Southeastern Autorama car show.
WHEREAS, the Southeastern Autorama car show has been a beloved tradition for over
six decades, marking its sixty-fifth anniversary in 2025; and
WHEREAS, this renowned event, held annually in Erwin, showcases an extraordinary
array of classic and modern vehicles, including hot rods, muscle cars, trucks, and motorcycles,
and attracting car enthusiasts and families alike; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee's longest-running consecutive car show, Southeastern
Autorama was founded in 1960 after Conrad Beam, who was the owner of the Big Hat Grill,
discussed ways to draw people into town with fellow antique car enthusiasts Jim Hobbs and
Earl Walker; and
WHEREAS, the Southeastern Autorama car show has since become a cherished
tradition, showcasing a wide variety of vehicles while benefiting the local community through
donations for scholarships, lighting for the local high school track field, and charitable efforts
such as raising funds for victims of Hurricane Helene; and
WHEREAS, in addition to celebrating automotive culture, the Southeastern Autorama
also introduced the Miss Autorama beauty pageant, with Cynthia Lewis as the first to claim the
crown; and
WHEREAS, the sixty-fifth annual Southeastern Autorama, to be held in Erwin on
October 11, 2025, will once again provide a venue for car lovers to share their passion, engage
with fellow enthusiasts, and contribute to the spirit of community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
HJR0515
006593
- 1 -
CONCURRING, that we recognize and commend the sixty-fifth annual Southeastern Autorama
car show, extending to the event's organizers, sponsors, participants, and attendees our best
wishes for every success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
