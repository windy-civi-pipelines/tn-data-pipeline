HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 683
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor the Children's Center of the
Cumberlands on its twenty-fifth anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
an institution that is dedicated to improving the lives of Tennessee's children, our most precious
natural resource, in a safe and welcoming environment; and
WHEREAS, the Children's Center of the Cumberlands in Oneida is celebrating its
twenty-fifth anniversary of serving our most vulnerable children, and its satellite location in
Jamestown is observing it twentieth anniversary this year; and
WHEREAS, under the leadership of executive director Angel Sexton, the Children's
Center of the Cumberlands serves the community as a child advocacy center, providing direct
mental health, medical, and prevention education services to the region's minor victims of
physical and sexual abuse and neglect and drug-endangered children and their non-offending
family members; and
WHEREAS, the Children's Center of the Cumberlands facilitates the diagnosis and
treatment of injuries suffered by victims of child abuse, the collection of forensic evidence for
legal purposes through the SANE medical program, and the provision of mental health and child
and family counseling through a full spectrum of behavioral health services to improve clients'
psychological wellness and quality of life; and
WHEREAS, for twenty-five years, the Children's Center of the Cumberlands has worked
to assure the safety and overall well-being of children who have been abused or neglected by
their parents or guardians, a noble calling that serves not only those children but the community
at large by helping to rebuild their future; and

WHEREAS, children who have been subjected to harm deserve our compassion and
understanding, but they also need the best available treatment to help them lead their best
possible lives; and
WHEREAS, understanding the importance of this mission, the administration and staff of
the Children's Center of the Cumberlands work hard every day to protect and serve child victims
of abuse and neglect, and they are most worthy of our approbation on this special occasion;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we join in commemorating the twenty-fifth anniversary of the Children's
Center of the Cumberlands and thank the center's administration and staff for their ongoing
commitment to Tennessee's most vulnerable children.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
