SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 335
By Oliver
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Middle Tennessee
Chapter of the National Action Network.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly pay tribute to those
exemplary organizations that work with purpose and conviction to improve the quality of life in
their respective communities; and
WHEREAS, one such esteemed organization is the Middle Tennessee Chapter of the
National Action Network, a grassroots effort that has worked tirelessly to address local issues,
champion social justice, and promote equality; and
WHEREAS, on May 15, 2025, the chapter will host the 2nd Annual Black Diamond Ball,
an event that promises to inspire, empower, and drive positive change while celebrating
equality, diversity, and social justice and
WHEREAS, founded in 1991 by Reverend Al Sharpton, the National Action Network
(NAN) is one of the leading civil rights organizations in the country, with chapters throughout the
United States; the organization works within the spirit and tradition of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.,
to promote a modern civil rights agenda that includes the fight for one standard of justice,
decency, and equal opportunities for all people; and
WHEREAS, the Middle Tennessee Chapter of NAN is dedicated to the pursuit of justice,
equality, and human rights for all citizens; the chapter is guided by a mission to empower
individuals and communities to actively engage in the fight against discrimination, bigotry, and
systemic injustice; and
WHEREAS, the chapter is devoted to promoting criminal justice reform, supporting
police accountability, upholding voting rights, assisting with crisis intake and victim assistance,
and advocating for youth leadership; and

WHEREAS, the initiatives of the Middle Tennessee Chapter of NAN often lead to the
creation of supportive networks, educational opportunities, and essential resources for
marginalized communities; and
WHEREAS, the chapter strives to cultivate a sense of belonging and shared purpose
among residents, thereby strengthening the social fabric of neighborhoods; by raising
awareness, mobilizing resources, and influencing policies, activists inspire individuals to engage
in meaningful dialogue, challenge injustice, and contribute to the betterment of the community;
and
WHEREAS, community activism is the cornerstone of a vibrant, inclusive society, driving
positive transformations and fostering a sense of unity among its members; it is most fitting that
we recognize this important organization and its continued good work; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Middle Tennessee
Chapter of the National Action Network, commending the chapter's role in fostering positive
change throughout the State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
