SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 292
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor Chief Richard Hickey of the Brentwood
Police Department on the occasion of his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
careers in law enforcement; and
WHEREAS, Chief Richard Hickey of the Brentwood Police Department (BPD) is one
such estimable individual, who has devoted his life to the preservation of law and order and the
protection of his fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, after more than thirty-four years of distinguished service to the City of
Brentwood, Chief Richard Hickey took his much-deserved retirement on February 28, 2025,
having dedicated himself to the highest tenets of public service and law enforcement throughout
his career; and
WHEREAS, Chief Hickey began his illustrious tenure with the Brentwood Police
Department in 1991 as a patrol officer, and for the past three decades, he has served his
community in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, following service as the assistant chief, Chief Richard Hickey was named
Chief of Police for the City of Brentwood in 2022, becoming the city's fourth police chief; and
WHEREAS, during his law enforcement career, Chief Hickey was involved in all aspects
of police work; he served as a field training officer, crime scene technician, detective, and
captain, and alongside several other BPD officers, he played an essential role in stopping an
armed assailant following a bank robbery in Brentwood in 2002; and

WHEREAS, Chief Richard Hickey is originally from McMinnville and attended Lipscomb
University; he earned a communication degree from Middle Tennessee State University, and
after he joined the Brentwood Police Department, he became only the second Brentwood officer
to attend and graduate from the prestigious FBI National Academy; and
WHEREAS, Chief Hickey has performed his duties with professionalism and integrity,
helping his fellow citizens in any way that he could and proving himself time and again to be a
tremendous asset to the Brentwood Police Department; and
WHEREAS, Chief Richard Hickey exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to community that
are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career, Chief Hickey has demonstrated the utmost
ability and honor, winning the sincere respect and admiration of his colleagues and the citizens
of Brentwood; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Chief Richard Hickey on
his exemplary career with the Brentwood Police Department and extend to him our best wishes
for much continued success and happiness in his retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
