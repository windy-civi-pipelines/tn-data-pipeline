SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 194
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to recognize Ken Rea on the occasion of his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Ken Rea is one such estimable person, who served the First Tennessee
Development District with distinction for thirty-nine years and will take his much-deserved
retirement on June 30, 2025; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Rea currently serves the First Tennessee Development District as
deputy director for economic and community development, a station he has held for fifteen
years, and for much of that time, he was the only development district director in the State with
a degree in economics; and
WHEREAS, during his distinguished tenure, Mr. Rea successfully established the
Economic and Community Development Department and was a key part of applying for,
receiving, and executing dozens of grants and local projects; and
WHEREAS, these projects proved transformational to the community's economic
development and included extending water lines to more than 4,000 citizens, in addition to
organizing infrastructure improvements at Exit 23 in Greene County, which brought a number of
employers and jobs to the area; and
WHEREAS, Ken Rea exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community that
are characteristic of a true Tennessean, and his legacy of local infrastructure and economic
development will continue to inspire his community for years to come; and

004197
- 1 -
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career, Ken Rea demonstrated the utmost
professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of his
colleagues and his community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we extend to Ken Rea our heartfelt wishes for a
happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
