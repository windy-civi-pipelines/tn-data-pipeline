SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 181
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of R.W. Groom III of
Nashville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the untimely passing of R.W. Groom III; and
WHEREAS, R.W. Groom was a creative soul with a gentle, humble spirit who did his
utmost to improve the well-being of all who were blessed to know him; and
WHEREAS, the only child of R.W. Groom, Jr., and Bernice Vance Groom, Mr. Groom
was born in Lebanon and moved with his family at the age of nine to El Paso, Texas, where his
father was stationed at Fort Bliss as a member of the U.S. Army; and
WHEREAS, the Groom family later moved to Murfreesboro, and R.W. Groom attended
Mitchell Nielson Elementary School, where his mother was a teacher; he went on to attend
Murfreesboro Central High School, where he was a member of the tennis team, played
trombone in the band, joined the Sigma Chi Omega fraternity, and was a pitcher in recreation
league baseball; and
WHEREAS, R.W. Groom earned his bachelor's degree in English from the University of
Tennessee at Martin, where he played tennis, partook in theatrical play productions, and sang
and played guitar around town; he was inspired by his high school English teachers to become
an English teacher himself and ultimately went on to earn his Master of English degree and
Education Specialist degree in Administration from Middle Tennessee State University; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Groom taught English for grades 7-12 for forty years at various schools,
including Fayetteville Junior High School, Apollo Middle School, Hillsboro High School, Cohn
Learning Center, and Antioch High School, where he met the love of his life, Pam, who was also
a teacher there; and

WHEREAS, during his tenure in education, he coached tennis, served as yearbook
supervisor, started the Hillsboro recycling site, led students on various trips, and worked for
Homework Hotline; he also coached his son's basketball, soccer, and baseball teams and his
daughter's softball and basketball teams; and
WHEREAS, a man of faith, he accepted Christ as his savior at the age of ten at First
Baptist Church in Murfreesboro, where he was active in youth group and choir; he and his wife
were members of Woodmont Christian Church, where he served as deacon on two occasions,
chaired Room in the Inn, chaired Outreach Committee, worked with Habitat for Humanity, taught
Sunday School, and chaperoned mission trips; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Groom is survived by his wife of thirty-one years, Pamela Jones Groom;
son, Taylor Groom; daughter, Anna Kathryn Groom; nieces, Juliet Jones, Abigail Jones, and
Mackenzie Burgess; nephew, Austin Burgess; sisters-in- law, Kim Burgess and Mandy Jones;
brother-in-law, Rod Burgess; mother-in-law, Mary Jane Jones; and many beloved cousins and
extended family members; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his brother-in-law
Brad Jones, his mother-in-law Betty Jones, his father-in-law Gary Jones, and his beloved aunts
and uncles on the Vance/Groom side; and
WHEREAS, throughout his many worthy endeavors, R.W. Groom distinguished himself
as a beacon of kindness and wisdom; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to the bountiful life of this exceptional
Tennessean; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
- 2 - 004062
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of R.W. Groom III, reflecting
fondly upon his ability to live the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and condolences to the
family and friends of Mr. Groom.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
