SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 459
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor Mary Decker upon her career in special
needs education.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those outstanding citizens who devoted their careers to teaching special needs students; and
WHEREAS, Mary Decker distinguished herself as a professional of the greatest
dedication, ability, and integrity during her tenure with the Anoka-Hennepin School District in
Minnesota from 1991 to 2015, when she retired from teaching; and
WHEREAS, she began her career there as a paraprofessional working in elementary
classrooms and then served as a long-term substitute teacher in special needs classrooms of all
categories; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Decker next served as a special needs teacher at Anoka High School
and Blaine High School and then concluded her career as a reading teacher for struggling
readers at Blaine High School; and
WHEREAS, for fifteen of the seventeen years she was an employee of the school
district, she was a homebound teacher for all categories of special needs students; and
WHEREAS, Mary Decker received her Bachelor of Science degree in Elementary
Education with a minor in Special Education from St. Cloud State University and later earned a
master's degree in Education and Reading Instructional Licensure from Hamline University; she
also completed training in the Sonday System for teaching reading to students with dyslexia;
and
WHEREAS, after retiring and relocating to the Volunteer State, she volunteered at a
local elementary school for a time and still maintains her membership at Love's Way Church in
Lebanon, where she practices her faith; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Decker is most appreciative of the love, support, and companionship
she shares with her beloved husband of fifty years, Randall Decker, who was a school bus

Milbrath; and their grandchildren, Logan Brown, Kayden Brown, and Caleb Brown; and
WHEREAS, during her distinguished tenure in the classroom, Mary Decker was wholly
committed to the highest ideals of public service and quality education for special needs
students, and she should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
