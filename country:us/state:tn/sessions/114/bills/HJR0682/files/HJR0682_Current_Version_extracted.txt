HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 682
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Maggie June McClard
Shrum of Macon County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Maggie June McClard Shrum of Macon County; and
WHEREAS, born on December 29, 1934, in her beloved Macon County, June Shrum
was the daughter of Hessie Mae Doss McClard and Elzie Lee McClard; and
WHEREAS, passionate about local history, June Shrum was an esteemed member of
her community and had served as the manager of the Macon County Historical Society since
2009, working tirelessly to preserve the area's rich heritage; and
WHEREAS, during her professional career, Mrs. Shrum served as secretary and
manager for Macon County Memorial Gardens and Holland Office Equipment and as manager
and bookkeeper for Roger Ford Tractor; and
WHEREAS, her employment history also included tenures as an employee of The Merle
Norman Studio and Gift Shop in Kentucky and in advertising for WEEN Radio; she and her
husband, J.T. Shrum, also pursued their passion for real estate and auctions; and
WHEREAS, she was an active and devout member of Grace Baptist Church; she taught
the Bible for over fifty years and served as a church secretary for two decades in addition to
being active in various church programs, including Vacation Bible School; and
WHEREAS, she was also deeply devoted to her family, and she always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Shrum enjoyed the loving companionship of her husband, J.T. Shrum,
for many years until his passing and was the proud mother of Angela (Jeff) Beam and the
treasured grandmother of Scott (Amber) East; and

WHEREAS, she will be fondly remembered by her stepchildren, Mark (Jennene) Shrum,
David Shrum and companion, Joyce Gregory, and Tammy (Gary) Cox; step-grandchildren,
Laura (Clint) Law, Lindsay (Daniel) Carr, Hunter Shrum, Halle (Austin) Herbert, Holden Shrum,
and Taylor Cox; five step-great-grandchildren; and a host of extended family members and dear
friends; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents and her husband, Mrs. Shrum was preceded in
death by her step-grandson, Hayden Shrum; brother and sister-in-law, Jackie and Mauvis
McClard; sister, Billie Randall; father- and mother-in-law, Elmer and Evie Shrum; and sisters-in-
law, Polly Gann and Helen Jenkins; and
WHEREAS, June Shrum leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Maggie June McClard Shrum, reflecting fondly
upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the examined life with
courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mrs. Shrum.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
