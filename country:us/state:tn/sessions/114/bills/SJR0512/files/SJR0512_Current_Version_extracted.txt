SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 512
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to recognize Beth McGhee-Folsom upon the
occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we specially recognize those citizens who have performed
with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their years of service; and
WHEREAS, Beth McGhee-Folsom is one such estimable individual, who, following thirty
years of service as a leader and industry representative, will take her much-deserved retirement
from her position as director of regulatory affairs at ColorTech; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. McGhee-Folsom began her career at ColorTech following the closing
of the BASF plant, during which time ColorTech was still a relatively small operation; since then,
the company has grown from a mere thirty-two employees to more than 300; and
WHEREAS, a leader in her industry, Mrs. McGhee-Folsom served the Plastics Industry
Association as a national board chair and tnAchieves as a mentor; she is an active member of
the Rotary Club and recently started her term as a board member of the United Way; and
WHEREAS, her professional accomplishments aside, Beth McGhee-Folsom is most
grateful for the love and support she shares with her husband, Ted; and
WHEREAS, she is also active in her faith and attends First Presbyterian Church of
Jefferson City; and
WHEREAS, throughout her estimable career, Mrs. McGhee-Folsom has demonstrated
the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the respect and admiration of her
community and colleagues; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we extend to Beth McGhee-Folsom our heartfelt
SJR0512

wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement, offering our deepest gratitude for her distinguished
service with ColorTech and wishing her every continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
