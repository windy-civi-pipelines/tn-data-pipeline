HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 481
By Littleton
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Robert Earl "Bobby"
Duke of Dickson.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Robert Earl "Bobby" Duke of Dickson; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Duke was a man of faith who loved his family and the outdoors and
led by example, enriching the lives of all who were fortunate to know him; and
WHEREAS, a native and lifelong resident of Dickson, Mr. Duke was born on February 2,
1938, to Marvin and Lorelle Johnson Duke; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Duke dedicated his early years to working as a community butcher
and farmer alongside his family; he later made his living as a factory manager at Schrader Valve
and Quebecor Printing; and
WHEREAS, he was also a Community Beautification Expert, winning numerous awards
for his meticulously maintained lawn and taking it upon himself to care for several public flower
beds throughout Dickson, leaving the town more vibrant and beautiful for all who live there; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Duke was an active and devout member of both Rock Church of
Christ and Walnut Street Church of Christ; he took great pride in serving as president of the
Rock Church Cemetery Association, ensuring the cemetery's care and preservation for
generations to come; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Duke's legacy is carried on by his wife of nearly sixty-five years, Wilma
Maxine Woodard Duke; children, Robert Perry (Jena) Duke, Vandy (Sharon) Duke, and Cherie
(Doug) Welch; grandchildren, Scarlet Bryant, Johnathan Duke, Brooke Duke Houschildt,

Stephen Duke, Anastasia Duke, Andrew Welch, Jacey Duke, and Alexander Welch; and great-
grandchildren, Lorelai Bryant, Kayden Bryant, Harbor Duke, Allie Houschildt, Vladimir Collette,
Mila Duke, Daxton Houschildt, Arya Collette, and Eloise Duke; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his brothers, Larry
Duke, Jimmy Duke, and Clark Duke; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Duke leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Robert Earl "Bobby" Duke, reflecting fondly upon
his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage
and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Duke.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
