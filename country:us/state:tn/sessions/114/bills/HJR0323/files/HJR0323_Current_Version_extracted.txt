HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 323
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Connor Britt on his
perfect ACT score.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to those students who, through their
natural talents and unflagging commitment to academic excellence, have distinguished
themselves in the classroom and beyond; and
WHEREAS, Connor Britt, a senior at Independence High School, is one such
exceptional student, who has received a perfect composite score of 36 on the ACT exam; and
WHEREAS, through diligent study, steady composure during examinations, and
steadfast commitment of time and talent, Mr. Britt has achieved outstanding academic success
and joined the ranks of the best and brightest students in Tennessee and our country; and
WHEREAS, his laudable dedication and drive are similarly exhibited in his
extracurricular activities and school projects, and all his labors speak of a passion for learning
and self-betterment; and
WHEREAS, Connor Britt's display of intellectual aptitude and ambition surely bodes well
for his future success as he embarks upon his collegiate career, during which he hopes to earn
a degree in mechanical engineering, a field in which he hopes to bring rejuvenating innovation;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Britt's noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for his
family, his school, and his community; and
WHEREAS, Connor Britt's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of our respect,
admiration, and commendation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
HJR0323
004696
- 1 -
CONCURRING, that we commend and honor Connor Britt of Independence High School on his
perfect ACT score, salute his impressive academic achievement, and extend to him our best
wishes for a happy, successful future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 2 - 004696
