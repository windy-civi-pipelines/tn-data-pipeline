SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 306
By Yarbro
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Jonathan Brocco on
his selection as the Tennessee Charter School
Center's 2024 Administrator of the Year.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those outstanding professionals who have devoted their careers to providing the
highest quality education for Tennessee's students; and
WHEREAS, Jonathan Brocco is one such estimable educator, who has distinguished
himself as a professional of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, the principal at LEAD Southeast Middle School in Nashville, Mr. Brocco was
named the 2024 Administrator of the Year by the Tennessee Charter School Center at the
Charter Champion Awards, an honor most befitting his outstanding tenure as an educator, as it
recognizes the positive influence he has had on so many young lives; and
WHEREAS, Jonathan Brocco is an extremely talented and compassionate educator who
works assiduously to motivate his students to reach their full potential as scholars and citizens;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Brocco truly epitomizes the ideal of the dedicated educator who is
wholly committed to the highest precepts of public service and quality education for all, and he
should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate Jonathan Brocco
upon being named the 2024 Administrator of the Year by the Tennessee Charter School Center,
SJR0306
001102
- 1 -
commend his exceptional service to the children of Tennessee, salute his professional expertise
and personal excellence, and wish him all the best in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
