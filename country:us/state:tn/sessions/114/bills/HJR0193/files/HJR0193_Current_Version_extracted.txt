HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 193
By Lamberth
A RESOLUTION to confirm the appointment of Mark Rogers to
the state forestry commission.
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 11-4-201, provides for the creation of
a state forestry commission within the department of agriculture; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 11-4-201, further provides that the
governor shall appoint seven (7) members to the commission; and
WHEREAS, Governor Bill Lee has appointed Mark Rogers to the state forestry
commission as the representative of an owner of five hundred (500) or more acres of forests
within the state for a term that expires on June 30, 2029; and
WHEREAS, a nearly thirty-year veteran of forest management, Mark Rogers is a Society
of American Foresters certified forester who has worked with private industry, state forestry
agencies, and conservation organizations; and
WHEREAS, currently employed as the Central Appalachian forest manager for The
Nature Conservancy, Mr. Rogers is committed to employee/contractor safety, sustainable forest
management, water quality, forest products industry development, and forestry education; and
WHEREAS, he is a member of the Society of American Foresters and the Tennessee
Forestry Association; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Rogers earned both his bachelor's degree and master's degree in
forestry from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 11-4-201, provides that each member
appointed by the governor to the state forestry commission shall be confirmed by the general
assembly; now, therefore,
HJR0193
003263
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we confirm the appointment of Mark Rogers to the state forestry
commission for a term that expires on June 30, 2029.
