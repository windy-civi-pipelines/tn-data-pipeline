SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 56
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to recognize Danny England Motors on the
occasion of its fiftieth anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those historic Tennessee businesses that have a long-standing commitment to providing
exceptional service to their customers; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable entity is Danny England Motors, located in New
Tazewell, which celebrates its fiftieth anniversary this year, a commendable milestone for this
family-owned business; and
WHEREAS, established at its current location in 1975, Danny England Motors is
comprised of a consumer-centered philosophy, which delivers superior automotive selections
and customer care services; and
WHEREAS, in this, Danny England Motors's fiftieth year of operation, the business
remains true to its tradition of automotive excellence under the leadership of Danny England
and Barbara England, the second generation to operate the independent dealership; and
WHEREAS, together, the Englands manage the showroom floor each day with
unmatched energy, honoring their values and deepening the footprint of family service and
philanthropy through service on various nonprofit boards and with community-driven missions;
and
WHEREAS, for five decades, Danny England Motors has provided exceptional
automotive selections and superior customer service to the people of New Tazewell, and it is
fitting that the business be recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
SJR0056
002500
- 1 -
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we congratulate Danny England Motors on fifty
years of operation, wishing the iconic Tennessee business much continued success in the
future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
