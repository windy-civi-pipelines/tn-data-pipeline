HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 119
By Parkinson
A RESOLUTION to honor Adrian Maclin upon being named the
2025 Grammy Music Educator of the Year.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we specially recognize those outstanding teachers who have
devoted their careers to providing the highest quality education for Tennessee's students; and
WHEREAS, Adrian Maclin is one such estimable educator, who has distinguished
himself as a professional of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, a music teacher and choir director at Cordova High School, Mr. Maclin was
recently named the 2025 Grammy Music Educator of the Year, an honor most befitting his
devotion to his students and his profession; and
WHEREAS, the Music Educator of the Year Award was created to recognize current
educators who have made a significant and lasting contribution to the field of music education
and who demonstrate a commitment to the broader cause of maintaining music education in the
schools; and
WHEREAS, this year's award was announced as part of a segment featuring Mr. Maclin
during the CBS Mornings show; his Grammy prize includes a $10,000 honorarium and a
matching $10,000 for his school's music program; and
WHEREAS, currently in his thirteenth year as Director of Choirs at Cordova High School
and his nineteenth year of teaching, Adrian Maclin has built an elite music program at the
school, with his concert singers winning so many awards that they are running out of space to
hang them on the walls; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Maclin's groups have performed at the Southern Division of the
American Choral Directors Association conference, the Tennessee Music Educators

Association conference, and in numerous music halls throughout the country, including
Carnegie Hall; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his work at Cordova, he is on the Memphis-Shelby County
School District Music Leadership team, in which capacity he supports his choral colleagues and
novice directors; he also serves on the board of the West Tennessee Vocal Music Educator
Association, which he served as president; the Tennessee Music Educators Association, which
he currently serves as chair; and the board for the State Chapter of the American Choral
Directors Association; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Maclin attended Memphis City Schools, where he was inspired by his
middle and high school choral directors to become a music educator; he earned his Bachelor of
Arts degree in Music, with a minor in Vocal Performance, from Philander Smith University; and
WHEREAS, for twenty years, Adrian Maclin has served Mount Moriah – East Baptist
Church as Minister of Music; he is also the Assistant Artistic Director for the Memphis Choral
Arts and the primary conductor for the Memphis Men's Chorale; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his most recent honor from the Recording Academy, Mr.
Maclin was honored by the Country Music Association Foundation with the Music Teachers of
Excellence Award in 2020; and
WHEREAS, his many successes aside, he is most proud of his daughters, Madison and
McKenzie; and
WHEREAS, Adrian Maclin is an extremely talented and compassionate educator who
has worked assiduously throughout his career in education to motivate his students, fellow
teachers, and staff to reach their full potential as scholars, professionals, and citizens; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Maclin truly serves as an inspiration to the faculty and staff at Cordova
High School, and his friendship and counsel are highly valued by those who are fortunate to
know him; and

WHEREAS, Adrian Maclin epitomizes the dedicated educator who is wholly committed
to the highest ideals of public service and quality education for all, and he should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Adrian Maclin on being selected as the 2025
Grammy Music Educator of the Year, commend his exceptional service to the children of
Tennessee, salute his professional expertise and personal excellence, and wish him all the best
in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
