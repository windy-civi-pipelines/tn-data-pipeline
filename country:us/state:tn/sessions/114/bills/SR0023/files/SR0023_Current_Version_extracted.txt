SENATE RESOLUTION 23
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION to honor Dr. Carol Rawls Johnson Dean in
celebration of Women's Day on the Hill.
WHEREAS, Women's Day on the Hill is a time to honor the achievements of those
Tennessee women who have paved the way for every person to have the opportunity to pursue
the American dream; and
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is proud to be the home of the awe-inspiring Dr. Carol
Rawls Johnson Dean, a longtime educator and education administrator who recently stepped
down as Interim President of LeMoyne-Owen College; and
WHEREAS, prior to this most recent role, she had served as the Executive Director of
New Leaders, South Region, a national organization providing leadership development for
aspiring principals and teachers; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Carol Johnson Dean's career in education includes serving as a
teacher, principal, and superintendent in school systems in Boston, Massachusetts;
Minneapolis, Minnesota; and Memphis, and for her good work, she was named Minnesota
Superintendent of the Year and received the nation's highest honor for urban education
leadership, the Richard R. Green Award; and
WHEREAS, she has further served her profession and her community as a member of
several boards, including those of Facing History, City Year, the Assisi Foundation, Overton
Park Conservancy, First 8 Advisory, Memphis Teacher Residency, Memphis Symphony
Orchestra, and Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.; and
WHEREAS, in saluting Dr. Johnson Dean on this special occasion, let us remember that
when women succeed, America succeeds, as does Tennessee; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby honor and commend
Dr. Carol Rawls Johnson Dean on her many contributions to the State of Tennessee as we
celebrate Women's Day on the Hill.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
