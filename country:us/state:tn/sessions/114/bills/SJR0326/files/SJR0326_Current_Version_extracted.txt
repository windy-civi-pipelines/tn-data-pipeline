SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 326
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Brian Glenn Wilson on
fifty years of service in the broadcasting industry.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those radio broadcasters who have achieved luminary status within their communities and
beyond, becoming true industry legends; and
WHEREAS, one such gentleman is Brian Glenn Wilson, former Fox News anchor and
host of The Drive with Brian Wilson on SuperTalk 99.7 WTN in Nashville, who recently
celebrated fifty years in the broadcasting industry; and
WHEREAS, born on February 15, 1956, Mr. Wilson was raised in Odessa, Texas; he
earned a master's degree in print journalism from American University, where he also served as
an adjunct professor in the communication school; and
WHEREAS, Brian Wilson began his broadcasting career in 1975 at the age of nineteen
in his hometown of Odessa, Texas, at KOZA radio as a news director; and
WHEREAS, a few short years later, Mr. Wilson started his television career at KAMR
TV, the NBC affiliate in Amarillo, Texas, before moving to KFDA TV Amarillo, the CBS affiliate;
he later returned to Odessa to KTPX of NBC, and in 1984, he moved to Mobile, Alabama, to
WKRG of CBS, where he first served as a city hall reporter and then as an evening news
anchor; and
WHEREAS, in 1986, Brian Wilson moved to Washington, D.C., to work as a reporter and
quickly became an anchor for the evening news on WTTG; in 1998, he moved to Fox News as a
senior correspondent and anchor, and in 2010, he moved back to WMAL radio to host the
morning show; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Wilson found his final home in 2018 as the radio host of the morning
drive with SuperTalk 99.7 WTN; he moved to the afternoons in 2021 to host The Drive with
Brian Wilson; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure at Fox News, Brian Wilson covered the White House,
Capitol Hill, and the Supreme Court; he also covered the events of 9/11 from the Pentagon; and
WHEREAS, his professional accomplishments aside, Brian Wilson is most grateful for
the love and support of his wife, Dawn; his daughter, Jessica Wilson; his son, Christopher Klein;
and his sister, Gwen Hicks; and
WHEREAS, for five decades, Mr. Wilson has demonstrated a remarkable commitment to
informing and entertaining his audiences, and we wish to recognize and commend him on this
special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Brian Glenn Wilson, the
former voice of The Drive with Brian Wilson on SuperTalk 99.7 WTN in Nashville, on fifty years
of service in the broadcasting industry and wish him many more years of success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
