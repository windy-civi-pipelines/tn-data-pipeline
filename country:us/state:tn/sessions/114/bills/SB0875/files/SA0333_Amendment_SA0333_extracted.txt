by deleting SECTION 2 and substituting instead:

Section 2:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 67-6-103(a)(3), is amended
by adding the following as new subdivisions:
(G) Notwithstanding the right or ability of a county or municipality to take a
special census at any time during the interim between the regular decennial federal
census under this section or another law, the department of economic and community
development shall revise and certify the population of each county and municipality,
and the aggregate population of the state, prior to July 1 each year during the interim
between the regular decennial federal census according to this subdivision (a)(3)(G)
for purposes of ensuring equitable allocation and distribution of moneys under this
subdivision (a)(3). The Boyd Center for Business and Economic Research at the
University of Tennessee, through the Tennessee state data center, shall generate
annual population estimates for each county and municipality and provide a report that
includes such estimates to the department of economic and community development
for review and certification. Prior to July 1, the department of economic and
community development shall provide the revised, certified populations of each county
and municipality to the department of revenue and the department of finance and
administration. The allocation and distribution of moneys pursuant to this subdivision
(a)(3) must be made according to the revised populations certified under this
subdivision (a)(3)(G), except in instances where a jurisdiction's population is revised as
a result of a special census or in a year in which populations are revised as a result of

the regular decennial federal census.
(H)
(i) Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, prior to the
distributions provided for in this subdivision (a)(3), there is apportioned and
distributed from the sales tax revenue collected pursuant to this chapter an
amount not to exceed forty-two thousand dollars ($42,000) annually to the
University of Tennessee for use by the Boyd Center for Business and
Economic Research. Such funds must be used exclusively to support the
preparation, analysis, and reporting of annual population estimates required
under subdivision (a)(3)(G) and certified by the department of economic and
community development.
(ii) The department of economic and community development and the
University of Tennessee shall review annually the amount distributed under this
subdivision (a)(3)(H)(i), and, notwithstanding the limitations provided in
subdivision (a)(3)(H)(i), may recommend an increase to the allocation, which
must be approved by the commissioner of finance and administration. If the
commissioner of finance and administration approves such an increase, the
commissioner shall publish the new amount on the department's website.
(iii) Notwithstanding this subdivision (a)(3)(H) to the contrary, no portion
of the revenue derived from the increase in the rate of sales and use tax
allocated to educational purposes, pursuant to chapter 529, § 9 of the Public
Acts of 1992, and no portion of the revenue derived from the increase in the
rate of sales and use tax from six percent (6%) to seven percent (7%),
pursuant to chapter 856, § 4 of the Public Acts of 2002, must be apportioned
and distributed pursuant to this subdivision (a)(3)(H). All such revenue must
continue to be allocated as provided in chapter 529, § 9 of the Public Acts of
1992, and chapter 856, § 4 of the Public Acts of 2002, respectively.
