Section 1:
Senate State and Local Government 1
Amendment No. 1 to SB1201
Briggs
Signature of Sponsor
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:

Section 2:
SECTION 1.
(a) The department of correction shall study the issues associated with
constructing and operating a reentry facility that would house eligible inmates who are
working toward return from incarceration into the community.
(b) The study required pursuant to subsection (a) must include:
(1) A list of criteria to be used to determine the inmates best suited for
being housed in the reentry facility, including educational and rehabilitative
criteria;
(2) The percentage of inmates who qualify for work, educational, or
vocational training programs currently and who are not able to participate in such
programs due to unavailability of positions or other prohibitive factors;
(3) The most beneficial time in which an eligible inmate would qualify to
be housed at a reentry facility that is no earlier than two (2) years prior to the
inmate's release eligibility date;
(4) An evaluation of the issues associated with housing inmates in an
environment that serves as a residential reentry facility, allowing eligible inmates
to work during the day and return to the facility in the evenings;
(5) An assessment of the resources needed to successfully operate a
reentry facility, including, but not limited to, community and law enforcement
partnerships;

(6) A survey of other similar facilities in other states that may be in
operation and the outcomes and issues associated with operating those facilities;
(7) An implementation strategy for constructing and operating a reentry
facility in this state;
(8) The costs associated with constructing and operating a reentry
facility, including an evaluation of both a state-funded and a public-private
partnership option, and recommended locations for a reentry facility; and
(9) A list and evaluation of any other needs associated with constructing
and operating a reentry facility, including, but not limited to, information sharing,
intelligence exchange, community engagement, and transitional social services.
(c) All appropriate agencies of state government shall provide assistance to the
department for purposes of conducting the study pursuant to this act.
(d) The department shall compile a report of the department's findings and
recommendations, which may include potential legislation. The report must be
submitted to the speaker of the house of representatives and the speaker of the senate
no later than January 1, 2026.
(e) The department shall pay any costs associated with the study required by
subsection (a) from the department's existing resources, which may include the
department's reserve fund.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
