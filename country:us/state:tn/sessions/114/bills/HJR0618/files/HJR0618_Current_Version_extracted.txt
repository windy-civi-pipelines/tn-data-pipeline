HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 618
By Vaughan
A RESOLUTION to recognize Collierville Mayor Stan R. Joyner on
the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm
throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, one such outstanding individual is Mayor Stan R. Joyner, a lifelong resident
of Collierville, who is taking a well-earned retirement after a distinguished career in public
service spanning more than two decades; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Joyner began his service to the Town of Collierville in 1999 as a
member of the Board of Mayor and Aldermen and was serving his third term when he was
elected as mayor in 2008; and
WHEREAS, a trusted and experienced leader, Stan Joyner was re-elected in 2012,
2016, and 2020, faithfully serving four terms as mayor and demonstrating tireless devotion to
the growth, development, and well-being of his hometown; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure as alderman, he lent his expertise to the Planning
Commission, the Employee Retirement Plan Committee, and the Pension Committee, laying the
groundwork for his practical, highly involved, and community-focused approach to leadership as
mayor; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Joyner's steady guidance was rooted in the principles of
stewardship, safety, service, and schools for the Collierville community; and
WHEREAS, having earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Education from Memphis
State University in 1972, Stan Joyner holds a personal and professional interest in the subject of
education; as mayor, he was instrumental in the creation of the Collierville School System,

leading efforts to establish a municipal school district, elect its first school board, and hire a
superintendent; and
WHEREAS, his integrity, transparency, and financially prudent approach to government
have earned him the respect and admiration of citizens and colleagues alike, and his dedication
has not gone unnoticed: in 2016, he was named Person of the Year by the Collierville Chamber
of Commerce and received the Bobby Donavant Public Servant Award from the Rotary Club of
East Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Joyner's many accolades also include the Tennessee Congress of
Parents and Teachers Life Achievement Award, the National PTA Life Achievement Award, the
H.R. Houston Award from Leadership Collierville, and induction into the Collierville High School
Alumni Hall of Fame; and
WHEREAS, a civic-minded individual, Stan Joyner is a member of the Collierville
Chamber of Commerce, a Paul Harris Fellow with the Collierville Rotary Club, a graduate of
Leadership Collierville, and a steadfast supporter of countless community events and causes;
and
WHEREAS, Mayor Joyner is a man of faith and a longtime member of Collierville United
Methodist Church, where he formerly taught Sunday School; and
WHEREAS, his many accomplishments aside, Stan Joyner is most appreciative of the
love and support he shares with his wife of forty-eight years, Linda; they are the proud parents
of three sons, Doug, Adam, and Tyler, and the doting grandparents of four granddaughters and
four grandsons; and
WHEREAS, throughout his illustrious tenure, Stan Joyner carried out his responsibilities
as Mayor of Collierville with diligence and integrity; his remarkable success as a public servant
and civic leader is directly attributable to his reliance on the time-honored values of hard work
and common sense and his uncommon ability to work well with people from all walks of life; and
- 2 - 007243
WHEREAS, a strong advocate for the citizens of Collierville, Mayor Joyner compiled a
singular record of success during his exemplary public service career, and he should be
specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend Stan R. Joyner for his honorable and astute service to the
good people of Tennessee as Mayor of Collierville, salute his unswerving commitment to living
the examined life with courage and conviction, and extend our best wishes for a happy and
fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
