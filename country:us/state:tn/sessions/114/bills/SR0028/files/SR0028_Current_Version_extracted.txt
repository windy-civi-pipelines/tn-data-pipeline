SENATE RESOLUTION 28
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION to honor Dr. Katrina Green in celebration of
Women's Day on the Hill.
WHEREAS, Women's Day on the Hill is a time to honor the achievements of those
Tennessee women who have paved the way for every person to have the opportunity to pursue
the American dream; and
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is proud to be the home of the awe-inspiring Dr. Katrina
Green, a stalwart advocate for the health and safety of Tennesseans, who spent the first ten
years of her medical career at Skyline Medical Center before transferring to Nashville General
Hospital in early 2021; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Green has been involved in advocacy regarding gun violence prevention
since 2019 and has also advocated for health care through Protect My Care, meeting with
legislators about many different healthcare-related issues; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Green has served as the Doctor of the Day at the General Assembly,
representing the Tennessee Medical Association; and
WHEREAS, she has further served the medical profession as a proud member, former
advocacy committee chair, and current member of the board of directors of the American
Medical Women's Association and an at-large board member of the Tennessee chapter of the
American Association of Emergency Medicine; and
WHEREAS, in saluting Dr. Green on this special occasion, let us remember that when
women succeed, America succeeds, as does Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby honor and commend
SR0028
003940
- 1 -
Dr. Katrina Green on her many contributions to the State of Tennessee as we celebrate
Women's Day on the Hill.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
