SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 100
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to confirm the appointment of Sharon Jean-
Phillipe to the state forestry commission.
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 11-4-201, provides for the creation of
a state forestry commission within the department of agriculture; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 11-4-201, further provides that the
governor shall appoint seven (7) members to the commission; and
WHEREAS, Governor Bill Lee has appointed Sharon Jean-Phillipe to the state forestry
commission as an at-large member for a term that expires on June 30, 2029; and
WHEREAS, Sharon Jean-Phillipe received her master's degree in botany from the
University of Tennessee (UT) in 2005 and then returned to Knoxville to earn her Ph.D. in natural
resources; and
WHEREAS, beginning her career as an assistant professor at UT, Knoxville in 2010, Dr.
Jean-Phillipe became a full-time professor of urban forestry in 2021; and
WHEREAS, she currently serves as the faculty director of Undergraduate Research and
Fellowships in the university's Division for Student Success; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Jean-Phillipe is also a UT-Ag Extension specialist and an International
Society of Arboriculture certified arborist; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 11-4-201, provides that each member
appointed by the governor to the state forestry commission shall be confirmed by the general
assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
SJR0100
001550
- 1 -
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we confirm the appointment of Sharon Jean-Phillipe
to the state forestry commission for a term that expires on June 30, 2029.
