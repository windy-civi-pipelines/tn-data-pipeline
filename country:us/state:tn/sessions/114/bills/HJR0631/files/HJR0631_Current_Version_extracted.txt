HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 631
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to commend A.J. Starling for his honorable
service as a member of the Davidson County
Election Commission.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to
improving the quality of life for their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, A.J. Starling is one such estimable person who evinces the greatest
integrity and probity in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, having served with distinction since 2003 as a member of the Davidson
County Election Commission, A.J. Starling is taking his well-deserved retirement from that
esteemed body; and
WHEREAS, currently serving as the secretary/treasurer of the Tennessee AFL-CIO, Mr.
Starling has enjoyed a storied career in the labor movement since 1971, when he was a bus
operator and member of the Amalgamated Transit Union Local 1235 in Nashville; and
WHEREAS, he was later elected to the Union Executive Board and eventually
Recording Secretary of Local 1235, where he served for twelve years; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure as a bus operator, Mr. Starling received recognition as
Operator of the Year and received the Perfect Attendance Award and Safe Driving Award for
eighteen years, as well as the Brooks-Starling Award, named in honor of Mr. Starling and his
supervisor, Frank Brooks, which is given annually to the operator who has gone above and
beyond the call of duty; and
WHEREAS, in 1988, Mr. Starling was selected to serve as labor coordinator in the
Technical Assistance Program for the Tennessee AFL-CIO, where he worked with the
Tennessee Department of Labor in the Dislocated Workers Unit, assisting hundreds of people to
secure training and re-employment; and

WHEREAS, within two years, he was promoted to Director of the Technical Assistance
Program, where he planned and supervised all phases and operations; under his leadership,
the program's staff doubled, and the annual budget grew to over half a million dollars before he
was named Director of Political Affairs for the Tennessee AFL-CIO in 2003; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Starling has lent his time and talents as a member of the Workforce
Investment Board (WIA#9), the State of Tennessee Tax Structure Study Commission, the Red
Cross Board, state president of the Asa Phillip Randolph Institute, and trustee of Westwood
Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, a devoted husband and father, A.J. Starling enjoys the love and support of
his wife, Marieta, and his two sons, Jeff and Andrae; and
WHEREAS, throughout his exemplary tenure as a member of the Davidson County
Election Commission, A.J. Starling has distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the
highest order and as an exceptional asset to his community; and
WHEREAS, A.J. Starling is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that
have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend A.J. Starling for his honorable and astute service to
the good people of Tennessee as a member of the Davidson County Election Commission and
salute his unswerving commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
