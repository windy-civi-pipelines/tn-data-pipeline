SENATE RESOLUTION 18
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION to honor Betina Hunt in celebration of Women's
Day on the Hill.
WHEREAS, Women's Day on the Hill is a time to honor the achievements of those
Tennessee women who have paved the way for every person to have the opportunity to pursue
the American dream; and
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is proud to be the home of the awe-inspiring Betina
Hunt, a native Memphian who has more than thirty years of experience in private- and public-
sector accounting and is the founder and president of You First Community Foundation, a
nonprofit dedicated to reducing recidivism and ending period poverty; and
WHEREAS, through You First Community Foundation and its ReStart Reentry Program
and Sister Keepers Youth Mentoring Program, Ms. Hunt provides mentorship, skill-building, and
workforce development opportunities for justice-impacted women and female juveniles, helping
to break cycles of hardship and create pathways for success; and
WHEREAS, Betina Hunt is also the owner of BU Beauty & Apparel and BU Clothing
Line, which inspire confidence and self-expression through fashion, and Premier Bookkeeping,
through which she educates entrepreneurs and business leaders on financial sustainability and
recession-proof strategies; and
WHEREAS, she has further served her community as a former president of the Greater
Memphis Chapter of the International Association of Women and through helping establish
Period Advocacy Day on the Hill to raise awareness and push for legislative change in
menstrual health policies; and
WHEREAS, in saluting Ms. Hunt on this special occasion, let us remember that when
women succeed, America succeeds, as does Tennessee; now, therefore,
SR0018
003927
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby honor and commend
Betina Hunt on her many contributions to the State of Tennessee as we celebrate Women's Day
on the Hill.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
