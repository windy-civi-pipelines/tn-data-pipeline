HOUSE RESOLUTION 119
By Parkinson
A RESOLUTION to honor Chase Kee for his contributions to the
96-0 Elmore Park Middle School Eagles, state
champions for the third consecutive year.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this legislative body should salute those student athletes
who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as true champions of
whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, Chase Kee, a six-foot seventh grader, is a valuable member of Bartlett's
Elmore Park Middle School Eagles boys' basketball team that recently won its third consecutive
TMSAA Class AAA state championship, capping a third consecutive undefeated season with a
perfect 31-0 record; and
WHEREAS, the Eagles' overall record during this three-peat championship run is an
incredible 96-0; the team's record for the last four years is 118-3; and
WHEREAS, Elmore Park's three consecutive state titles with an undefeated record in
each season is a first at any level of basketball in the State of Tennessee, truly an incredible
feat; and
WHEREAS, in the 2025 TMSAA Class AAA state tournament at Rockvale, Elmore Park
crushed Page 51-24 and then trounced Richview 53-32 to earn the three-peat and bring home
the gold; and
WHEREAS, Chase Kee epitomizes all that is good in today's student athletes, as he has
achieved a premier level of success in competition while exhibiting the highest degree of
character in all aspects of life; and

WHEREAS, the perseverance, determination, work ethic, and talent of this young man
and his teammates not only made possible a championship but also surely bode well for their
future success; and
WHEREAS, Chase Kee played an important role in the Elmore Park Eagles winning
their third consecutive state championship, and he is most worthy of our approbation and
congratulations on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor
Chase Kee for his contributions to the 96-0 Elmore Park Middle School Eagles, TMSAA Class
AAA boys' basketball state champions for the third consecutive year, and extend to him our best
wishes for every continued success in all his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
