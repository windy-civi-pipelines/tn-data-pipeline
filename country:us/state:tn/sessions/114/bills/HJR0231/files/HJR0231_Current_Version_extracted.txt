HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 231
By McCalmon
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Fire Chief David
Windrow on the occasion of his retirement from
the Nolensville Fire Department.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those citizens who have performed
with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm in securing the safety of their community; and
WHEREAS, Fire Chief David Windrow of the Nolensville Fire Department is one such
estimable individual, who has served with distinction as a firefighter for more than forty-one
years and recently embarked upon his much-deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, a proud military veteran, Chief David Windrow served in the United States
Air Force prior to beginning his fire service career, attaining the rank of Master Chief before
transitioning to the Air Force Reserve, where he was assigned to the Dallas Fort Worth Airport
in Texas; and
WHEREAS, relocating to Tennessee, he was hired as the training officer for the City of
Brentwood Fire Department, where he advanced to deputy chief, before seizing the opportunity
to become the first fire chief for Nolensville, the city in which he resided; and
WHEREAS, as the first fire chief for the Nolensville Fire Department, Chief Windrow was
tasked with starting the combination department, which involved hiring the first members,
purchasing a new engine and aerial apparatus, building a new fire station, purchasing the land
for the second station, and securing grants for equipment and a SAFER grant for six paid
positions; and
WHEREAS, his exemplary efforts drastically improved the services provided to the
residents of Nolensville and the surrounding community, including life safety inspections, child
safety seat installations, home safety checks, and numerous public education services provided
through public events and area schools; and

WHEREAS, certified as a Chief Fire Officer by the Center for Public Safety and
Excellence, Chief Windrow has completed the National Fire Academy's Executive Fire Officer
program and holds a Master of Public Administration degree and a number of other
certifications; and
WHEREAS, for the past five years, Chief Windrow has served as chairman of the
legislative committee of the Tennessee Fire Chiefs, helping to win passage of a number of
landmark pieces of legislation, including a cancer presumption bill; he has also chaired the
fundraising efforts for the fire service coalition, which supports the activities of the Firefighter
Legislative Action Group PAC; and
WHEREAS, he has spoken at many seminars and training events, assisted in creating
several training programs that are still utilized by the State of Tennessee, and mentored many
young firefighters, whom he helped prepare for their advancement by the guidance he gave and
the personal example he set as a leader; and
WHEREAS, Chief Windrow was the co-chair of the Tennessee Ultimate Fire Fighter
(TUFF) competition for ten years, which has raised over $100,000 for the operation of the
Tennessee Fallen Fire Fighter Memorial; and
WHEREAS, apart from his work in fire service, Chief Windrow is a former president of
the Rotary Club and a two-time recipient of the Brentwood Rotarian of the Year honors; he is a
member of the Board of Trustees of Southern Hills Hospital in Nashville, an elder with his
church, and an organizer of one of the largest blood drives in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, throughout his years in public service, Chief David Windrow has
demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the respect and
admiration of his fellow firefighters and the residents of Nolensville; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Fire Chief David Windrow of the Nolensville Fire

Department for his exemplary career in fire service and extend to him our heartfelt wishes for a
happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
