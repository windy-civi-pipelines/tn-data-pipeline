SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 399
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to recognize and thank Tennessee's retailers for
collecting sales taxes for the State.
WHEREAS, Tennessee's retailers collected and remitted fourteen billion dollars in sales
taxes in 2024, as mandated by the State for the convenience of the State; and
WHEREAS, consumers pay for goods and services with credit cards and debit cards,
and transaction fees are charged to facilitate the transaction between the consumer and the
merchant; and
WHEREAS, these fees are assessed on the total transaction amount, including the sales
tax portion of the transaction; and
WHEREAS, the sales tax portion of the transaction is separate and only benefits the
State; and
WHEREAS, the retailer bears the card transaction fees associated with the sales tax
transaction on behalf of the State; and
WHEREAS, the State and retailers should work together to address the burdens of
collecting and remitting sales taxes; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that in recognition and appreciation of their essential role
and the cost burden they currently bear relative to card transaction fees, we thank Tennessee's
retailers for their efficient collection of sales taxes for the benefit of State government.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
