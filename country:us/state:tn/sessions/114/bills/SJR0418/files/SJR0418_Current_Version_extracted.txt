SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 418
By Kyle
A RESOLUTION to commend Joshua Blackwell for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this body is pleased to recognize certain persons
who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, Joshua Blackwell is one such individual who has distinguished himself in his
role as a legislative intern for Senator Sara Kyle; Joshua's work has resulted in innumerable
benefits for the legislators he assisted, Senator Kyle, and the General Assembly as a whole;
and
WHEREAS, a student at the University of Memphis (UM), where he is highly regarded
by his peers, Joshua Blackwell has achieved academic success while pursuing a major in
political science and economics with a minor in public administration and participating in
numerous extracurricular activities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Blackwell is involved in UM Student Government as a member of the
Student Court and the Tennessee Intercollegiate State Legislature as a representative, and he
serves as director of membership for the Honors Student Organization; and
WHEREAS, he was formerly Environmental Chair for the First Year Senate and a
member of the Student Government Association's Environmental Committee; and
WHEREAS, Joshua gives back to his community as a dedicated volunteer for Protect
Our Aquifer; he has also served as an intern in the Urban Fellows Program for the City of
Memphis Office of Community Affairs; and
WHEREAS, after graduation, he plans to pursue a Juris Doctorate and a master's
degree in public policy to gain expertise in the intersections between policy and law; and

WHEREAS, a native of Memphis, Joshua Blackwell is the son of Camillia Hill and
Joseph Blackwell; and
WHEREAS, he is a graduate of White Station High School, where he was a member of
the bowling team, marching band, and Key Club; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Joshua Blackwell brought his many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2025 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2025 legislative session, Joshua Blackwell has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, he has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and rare
decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend Joshua Blackwell for outstanding
service as a legislative intern for Senator Sara Kyle and extend to him our best wishes for every
future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
