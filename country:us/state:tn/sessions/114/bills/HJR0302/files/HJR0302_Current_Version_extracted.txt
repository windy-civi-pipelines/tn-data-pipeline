HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 302
By Clemmons
A RESOLUTION to recognize Pat Nolan of NewsChannel 5 on the
occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor a journalist who has performed with
uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout his career; and
WHEREAS, a Tennessee broadcasting legend, Pat Nolan has appeared on the
airwaves of NewsChannel 5 (WTVF) in Nashville in some capacity for more than fifty years, and
he will take his much-deserved retirement in March; and
WHEREAS, a lifelong Nashvillian, Pat Nolan graduated from Father Ryan High School in
1969 and earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science with a minor in History from
Vanderbilt University in 1973; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Nolan began his distinguished journalism career at WPLN News in 1973
and was recruited to join NewsChannel 5 as a reporter in 1975 by legendary anchor and news
director Chris Clark; and
WHEREAS, after ten years with NewsChannel 5, Mr. Nolan left the station in 1985 to
become Nashville Mayor Richard Fulton's executive assistant and press secretary; when Mayor
Fulton's term ended in 1987, Mr. Nolan returned to NewsChannel5 and served as its on-air
political analyst for more than thirty-one years, from 1987 to 1992 and from 1999 to 2005; and
WHEREAS, he went on to work full time in public relations, working at Hart & Company,
Dye Van Mol & Lawrence, and finally DVL Seigenthaler, where he rose to the rank of senior vice
president of public relations and advertising before retiring from that post in 2019; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Nolan also served as the anchor/announcer for live television coverage
of the Metropolitan Nashville Council meetings from 1973 to 1975 and later from 2019 to 2023;
and

WHEREAS, in 2006, he began hosting Inside Politics on NewsChannel 5+, a long-
running weekly political interview program that originated as Inside this Week with Chris Clark,
with Mr. Nolan taking over upon Mr. Clark's retirement from broadcasting; and
WHEREAS, over the years, Mr. Nolan has interviewed politicians, state leaders, mayors,
councilmembers, lobbyists, historians, experts, analysts, and people of all backgrounds for his
show. He is known for thoroughly researching and crafting his questions, then trying to fit in as
many of his questions as possible; and
WHEREAS, although Pat Nolan is retiring as both NewsChannel 5's political analyst and
host of Inside Politics, he has indicated that he is still willing to do interviews about political
stories even after his retirement; and
WHEREAS, an active part of his community, he has served on several civic and
education boards, including as a founding member of the Father Ryan High School Board of
Trust, where he completed four-year tenures as chair and vice chair and served on the
Executive Committee; and
WHEREAS, he was also a founding member of the Board of Trust at Saint Bernard
Academy (SBA) when a parent-led group purchased SBA from the Sisters of Mercy in 1989. He
attended St. Bernard from kindergarten through fifth grade, and his two daughters, Katie and
Kelly, are both graduates of Saint Bernard and Father Ryan; and
WHEREAS, sharing his time and talents, he rendered sterling service as a member of
the Metro Nashville Historical Commission and the Community Television Access Board; and
WHEREAS, devoted to his family, Mr. Nolan enjoys the love and support of his wife of
nearly fifty-one years, Betty Lee Nolan, their children, and grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Pat Nolan exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community that
are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
- 2 - 004149
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career, Pat Nolan has demonstrated the utmost
professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of his
colleagues and the viewing public; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we extend to Pat Nolan our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling
retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
