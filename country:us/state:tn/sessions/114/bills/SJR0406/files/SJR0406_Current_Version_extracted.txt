SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 406
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Becky Kegley for her
contributions to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Becky Kegley of Lebanon, a highly
respected member of her community who has dedicated herself to improving the quality of life
for her fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, a career educator, Mrs. Kegley spent thirty-three years in education in
Tennessee, with the vast majority being in Wilson County, where she served as an English
teacher, a school principal, and, most recently, the assistant director of schools for the Lebanon
Special School District; and
WHEREAS, she further served her community as a board member of the Wilson County
Chamber of Commerce for three years; and
WHEREAS, raised in Chattanooga, Becky Kegley has lived in Lebanon since the early
1990s and has dedicated her life and career to helping others, especially children; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Kegley serves as an inspiration to her colleagues and the generations
of students she taught, and her friendship and counsel are highly valued by those who are
fortunate to know her; and
WHEREAS, a devoted wife, mother, and grandmother, Becky Kegley enjoys the love
and support of her husband, Billy Kegley; her children, Brent Easley, Matt Kegley, Kyle Kegley,
and April Moseley; and her seven grandchildren; and
SJR0406
006953
- 1 -
WHEREAS, Becky Kegley truly epitomizes the dedicated educator who is wholly
committed to the highest ideals of public service and quality education for all, and she should be
specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Becky Kegley for her
exceptional service to the children of Tennessee, salute her professional expertise and personal
excellence, and wish her all the best in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
