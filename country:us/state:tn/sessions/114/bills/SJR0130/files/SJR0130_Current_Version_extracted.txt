SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 130
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Helen E. Stirewalt of
Talbott.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Helen E. Stirewalt; and
WHEREAS, Helen Stirewalt was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Stirewalt served with acumen and alacrity at U.S. Steele, in which
position she earned the respect of her peers and superiors alike for the exemplary quality of her
work; and
WHEREAS, Helen Stirewalt was an active and devout member of Manley Baptist
Church, which she served faithfully for many years; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to her family, Ms. Stirewalt always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Helen Stirewalt is survived by her son, David (Karen) Trent; daughter,
Melissa (Mike) Watts; grandchildren, Eric (Denise) Watts, Amanda (Matt) Lay, and Eddie Trent;
and great-grandchildren, Adeline Watts, Aubree Lay, Blake Lay, Dawson Trent, Colin Trent, and
Madison Trent; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents, Carl and Lucille Estepp, Ms. Stirewalt was
preceded in death by her husband, Fred Stirewalt, and her infant daughter, Mary Susan Trent;
and
WHEREAS, Helen Stirewalt leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
SJR0130
003441
- 1 -
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Helen E. Stirewalt,
reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Ms. Stirewalt.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
