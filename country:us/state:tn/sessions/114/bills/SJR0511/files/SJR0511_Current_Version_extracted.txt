SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 511
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor the members of Unicoi County High
School's Future Health Professionals chapter for
their volunteer work during Hurricane Helene.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
an outstanding group of young Tennesseans who provided vital assistance and support to their
community during an emergency; and
WHEREAS, embodying a spirit of service, the members of Unicoi County High School's
Future Health Professionals (FHP) chapter sprang into action when Hurricane Helene wreaked
havoc in Northeast Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, despite facing their own losses and hardships, these courageous students
stepped up to provide help and hope to those affected by the hurricane's devastation, providing
compassionate leadership in a chaotic situation; and
WHEREAS, in addition to their more tangible contributions, the FHP students became a
source of strength for their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, each student demonstrated exemplary character, a commitment to
humanitarian values, and a big heart by providing care, comfort, and solace to the good people
of Unicoi County in their time of need; and
WHEREAS, Future Health Professionals is a global student-led organization whose
mission is to empower students to become leaders in the health community through education,
collaboration, and experience; and
WHEREAS, the Unicoi County High School FHP students well prepared themselves for
the future challenges of their professions through their valiant community service during
Hurricane Helene; these outstanding young leaders include Rhett Allen, Rosalin Andrade,
SJR0511
007983
- 1 -
Natalie Black, Peyton Bowling, Brianna Butler, Kinley Carver, Karrah Dean, Nancy Diaz, Kyndal
Engle, Chloe Fletcher, Piper Foltz, Sadie Griffith, Peyton Higgins, Isabella Laws, Alexis
Menkowitz, Abigail Petterson, Norah Ray, Melissa Reynoso, Issabella Sams, Candy Scott,
Abigail Silva, Abigail Vidrio, Laci Wall, and Matthew Whitson, who were inspired by the example
of their teacher, Rachel Rice; and
WHEREAS, these young Tennesseans are deserving of our approbation for their
courage and commitment during a time of crisis; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the twenty-four members of Unicoi County
High School's Future Health Professionals chapter for their volunteer work during Hurricane
Helene, praise their generous spirit in helping others while facing hardships of their own, and
wish them all the best in their future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
