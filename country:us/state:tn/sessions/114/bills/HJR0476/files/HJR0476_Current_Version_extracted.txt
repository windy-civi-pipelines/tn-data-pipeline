HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 476
By Hale
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Mayor Roger Turney of
Auburntown on his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of the General Assembly specially recognize
those estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to improving the quality of life
for their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Auburntown Mayor Roger Turney is one such estimable person, who
evinces the greatest integrity and probity in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Turney recently took his much-deserved retirement from his post in the
City of Auburntown, following over forty years of service to his community; and
WHEREAS, throughout his distinguished tenure, Mayor Roger Turney has provided solid
leadership and thoughtful guidance for his fellow citizens, and as a model servant-leader, he
personally joined in the necessary manual labor and maintenance that the town has required
during his career; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his civic office, Mayor Turney concurrently and successfully
fulfilled the role of principal of Auburntown Elementary School until 2010, an eloquent testament
to his abiding concern for every member of his community; and
WHEREAS, Roger Turney has been a strong advocate for the citizens of Auburntown;
his remarkable success as a civic leader is directly attributable to his reliance on the time-
honored values of hard work and common sense and his uncommon ability to work well with
people from all walks of life; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Turney is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service
that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
HJR0476
005403
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend Roger Turney for his honorable and astute service to the
good people of Tennessee as Mayor of Auburntown, salute his unswerving commitment to living
the examined life with courage and conviction, and extend our best wishes for a happy and
fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
