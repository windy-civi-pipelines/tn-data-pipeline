HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 464
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor and recognize Weston Lineberry, 2025
Top Ten Senior at Frank Hughes School.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
those students who, through their natural talents and unflagging commitment to academic
excellence, have distinguished themselves in the classroom; and
WHEREAS, Weston Lineberry is one such exceptional student, who was recently named
a Top Ten Senior of the 2025 graduating class at Frank Hughes School; and
WHEREAS, through diligent study, steady composure during examinations, and
steadfast commitment of time and talent, Weston Lineberry has achieved outstanding academic
success and joined the ranks of the best and brightest students in Tennessee and our country;
and
WHEREAS, in his remarkable high school career, Mr. Lineberry has earned an
exemplary grade point average and participated in numerous extracurricular activities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lineberry's display of intellectual aptitude and ambition surely bodes
well for his future success as he enters the workforce after graduation; and
WHEREAS, his noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for his parents,
Chris and Chrystal Lineberry, his family, his school, and his community; and
WHEREAS, Weston Lineberry's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of our
respect, admiration, and commendation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend and honor Weston Lineberry of Frank Hughes
HJR0464
005232
- 1 -
School on being named a 2025 Top Ten Senior, salute his impressive academic achievement,
and extend to him our best wishes for a happy, successful future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
