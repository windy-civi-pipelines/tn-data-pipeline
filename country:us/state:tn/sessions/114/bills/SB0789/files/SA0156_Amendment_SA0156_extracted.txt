Section 2:
SECTION 4. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-22-116, is amended by deleting
subsection (a) and substituting:
(a)
(1) The board may license, without examination, a professional counselor
applicant with a valid, unencumbered license issued by another state, territory, or
federal district of the United States at an equivalent level of licensure under the
following conditions:
(A) The applicant has a sixty-hour graduate degree in counseling
or a related field, meets the requirements of § 63-22-104(4)-(7), and has
actively practiced without supervision at the level of licensure for which
the applicant has applied for at least two (2) of the three (3) most recent
years; or
(B) The applicant has a conferred graduate degree consisting of a
minimum of fifty-two (52) hours in counseling or a related field from an
accredited program that were earned prior to 2016, has practiced as a
licensed professional counselor for a minimum of twenty-five (25) years,
and meets the requirements of § 63-22-104(4)-(7).
(2) A professional counselor applicant at the highest level of licensure
must meet the requirements of § 63-22-120, as well as the requirements of either
subdivision (a)(1)(A) or (a)(1)(B).
