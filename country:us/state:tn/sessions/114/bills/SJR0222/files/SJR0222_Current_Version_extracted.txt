SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 222
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to commend Jackson Fox for exemplary service
as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this body is pleased to recognize certain persons
who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, Jackson Fox is one such individual who has distinguished himself in his role
as a legislative intern; his work has resulted in innumerable benefits for the legislators he
assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Fox is a student at Lipscomb University, where he is highly regarded by
his peers; he has achieved academic success and participated in numerous extracurricular
activities while pursuing his degree; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Jackson Fox brought his many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2025 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2025 legislative session, Jackson Fox has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Jackson Fox has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Jackson Fox for outstanding
service as a legislative intern and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
