SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 513
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Ellieonna Stetson for her
outstanding achievement in the Baileyton
Veterans Memorial design contest.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should recognize and celebrate the
accomplishments of young Tennesseans who demonstrate exceptional creativity, civic spirit,
and artistic talent; and
WHEREAS, Ellieonna Stetson, a student at North Greene Middle School, distinguished
herself as the third-place winner in the Baileyton Veterans Memorial sign design contest, a
competition that drew an impressive 147 entries from her peers; and
WHEREAS, the contest invited students to design a new road sign for the Baileyton
Veterans Memorial, which honors the service and sacrifice of our nation's veterans; and
WHEREAS, Miss Stetson's artistic contribution placed her design among the top three
chosen by the Town of Baileyton and reflected her talent, thoughtfulness, and respect for those
who have served; and
WHEREAS, Ellieonna Stetson's recognition came during a meeting of the Baileyton
Board of Mayor and Aldermen at Town Hall, where she stood proudly alongside her fellow
honorees, Lily Brake and Chloey Marshall, who received first and second place respectively;
and
WHEREAS, Miss Stetson's achievement is a great source of pride for her family, her
school, and her community, and it is most appropriate that she be specially honored by the
members of this General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
SJR0513
007985
- 1 -
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Ellieonna Stetson for
winning third place in the Baileyton Veterans Memorial sign design contest, applaud her
creativity and civic engagement, and extend to her our best wishes for every success in all
future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 2 - 007985
