HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 99
By Faison
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Representative Ira Curtis
Halford of Dyer.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of our colleague
Representative Curtis Halford of Dyer, a lifelong Tennessean who believed strongly in the
power of service, love, and dedication; and
WHEREAS, a seven-term member of the House of Representatives, Curtis Halford ably
represented the good people of Gibson and part of Carroll counties during his illustrious tenure
in the General Assembly, which spanned the 106th through 112th General Assemblies; and
WHEREAS, first elected in 2008, Representative Halford worked tirelessly to advocate
for his constituents, earning the respect of both his colleagues and the communities he
represented; and
WHEREAS, his commitment to public service and his dedication to improving the lives of
his fellow Tennesseans ensured his legacy as a leader who worked with integrity and
compassion; and
WHEREAS, Curtis Halford served as chair of the House Agriculture and Natural
Resources Committee for ten years and was a member of the National Conference of State
Legislatures' Environment Standing Committee on Agriculture and Natural Resources; and
WHEREAS, he was also a valuable member of the House committees on Calendar and
Rules, State Government, and Transportation; and
WHEREAS, Representative Halford had been honored with numerous awards and
accolades for his dedicated legislative service, including being named the Northwest Tennessee
Development District's Legislator of the Year for 2011 and 2015 and the Tennessee Veterinary
Medicine Association's Legislator of the Year for 2015 and receiving the HBAT Housing Hero

Award in 2017 and the Emergency Management Association of Tennessee Award of
Appreciation in 2011; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Halford began his illustrious career in public service as a member of the
Gibson County Commission and had also served on the Gibson County Planning Commission
and with the Gibson County Chamber of Commerce; and
WHEREAS, a proud veteran, Curtis Halford had served in the United States Air Force
Reserve and as Civilian Advisor to the Commanding General of the Tennessee State Guard;
and
WHEREAS, he was a proud graduate of Kenton High School, the Newbern Area
Vocational Technical School of Drafting, and Draughons Business College; and
WHEREAS, a devoted Christian, Curtis Halford worshipped at Cumberland Presbyterian
Church of Dyer, where he served as an Elder; and
WHEREAS, his family was his greatest joy, and he took great pride in their
accomplishments while cherishing their happiness; and
WHEREAS, Curtis Halford is survived by his wife of fifty-nine years, Charlotte; children,
Lori and Brad; grandchildren, Kate, Charlotte, Noah, Jeremiah, and Titus; and great-grandchild,
Asher; and
WHEREAS, Representative Halford touched many lives through his kindness, wisdom,
and commitment to making Tennessee an even better place, and he will be fondly remembered
for his warm and generous spirit; and
WHEREAS, for fourteen years, Curtis Halford was a vital member of this body, and his
passing leaves a void in his community and this State that will not be easily filled; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
- 2 - 002347
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of our colleague Ira Curtis Halford, reflecting fondly
upon his numerous contributions to good government in Tennessee and his legacy of effective
public service in his community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and extend our
condolences to Representative Halford's family and many friends.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
