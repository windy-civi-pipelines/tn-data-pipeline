SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 515
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Lily Brake for her
outstanding achievement in the Baileyton
Veterans Memorial design contest.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should recognize and celebrate the
accomplishments of young Tennesseans who demonstrate exceptional creativity, civic spirit,
and artistic talent; and
WHEREAS, Lily Brake, a student at North Greene Middle School, distinguished herself
as the first-place winner in the Baileyton Veterans Memorial sign design contest, a competition
that drew an impressive 147 entries from her peers; and
WHEREAS, the contest invited students to design a new road sign for the Baileyton
Veterans Memorial, which honors the service and sacrifice of our nation's veterans; and
WHEREAS, Miss Brake's artistic contribution placed her design first among the top three
chosen by the Town of Baileyton and reflected her talent, thoughtfulness, and respect for those
who have served; and
WHEREAS, her winning design features an American flag and the silhouette of an
eagle, with lyrics from the national anthem inscribed within, and bears the words: "Baileyton
Veterans Memorial — Home of the Free Because of the Brave"; and
WHEREAS, Lily Brake's recognition came during a meeting of the Baileyton Board of
Mayor and Aldermen at Town Hall, where she stood proudly alongside her fellow honorees,
Ellieonna Stetson and Chloey Marshall, who received third and second place respectively; and
WHEREAS, Miss Brake's achievement is a great source of pride for her family, her
school, and her community, and it is most appropriate that she be specially honored by the
members of this General Assembly; now, therefore,
SJR0515
007987
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Lily Brake for winning first
place in the Baileyton Veterans Memorial sign design contest, applaud her creativity and civic
engagement, and extend to her our best wishes for every success in all future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
