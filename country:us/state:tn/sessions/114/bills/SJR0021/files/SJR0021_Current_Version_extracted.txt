SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 21
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Linda Ann Harkey
Bowling of Olive Hill.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Linda Ann Harkey Bowling; and
WHEREAS, Linda Bowling was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Waynesboro on April 27, 1948, Mrs. Bowling was the daughter of
Allen and Mary Len Harkey, and she spent her formative years on Hardin's Creek; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Bowling was a proud graduate of Frank Hughes School, where she
played basketball during a time when girls only played half court; she earned a bachelor's
degree from the University of Tennessee at Martin and a master's degree from the University of
North Alabama; and
WHEREAS, Linda Bowling began her estimable teaching career at Frank Hughes
School and briefly taught home economics; she went on to teach kindergarten for more than
thirty years, earning the respect and admiration of her colleagues and students before taking
her much-deserved retirement in 2006; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Bowling was deeply devoted to her family, and she always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; she was known for her love of cooking, her
Sunday dinners, and her yeast rolls, which were made from a secret family recipe; and
WHEREAS, Linda Bowling enjoyed the loving companionship of her husband of forty-
seven years, Larry Bowling; also left to cherish her memory are her daughters, Nicole (Brian)
Wilkerson and Lauri (Keith) Wilkerson; her grandchildren, Callie, Crew, Landrey, and Lynzley;

her sister, Barbara Wilburn; her brother, David Harkey; and a host of nieces, nephews, cousins,
and friends; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents, Linda Bowling was preceded in death by her
brother, Jim Harkey; and her brother-in-law, Ronnie Wilburn; and her niece, Angie Bryant; and
WHEREAS, she was also deeply committed to her faith and attended Olive Hill Baptist
Church, where she taught Sunday School, sang in the choir, and assisted with Vacation Bible
School; additionally, she served as chaplain of the Retired Teachers Association in Wayne
County; and
WHEREAS, Linda Bowling leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Linda Ann Harkey Bowling,
reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mrs. Bowling.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
