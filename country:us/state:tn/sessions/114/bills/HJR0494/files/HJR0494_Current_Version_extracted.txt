HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 494
By McKenzie
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Mary Elizabeth Goins.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Mary Elizabeth Goins; and
WHEREAS, Mary Goins was an exemplary and conscientious public servant and
consummate professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow
citizens; and
WHEREAS, born in New Market to George Franklin Staffney, Sr., and Lillian Henderson
Staffney, Mrs. Goins was one of four children; she had three siblings: George Staffney, Julia
Roper, and Dorothy Staffney; and
WHEREAS, Mary Goins attended Cottage Hill Elementary School and then graduated
from Nelson Merry High School, where she played guard on the school's basketball team and
sang in the choir; and
WHEREAS, her participation in high school sports was a manifestation of an inner
passion, and throughout her life, she followed baseball, basketball, and football, amassing a
profound depth of knowledge in each sport; and
WHEREAS, she cultivated a tender affection for the art of gardening, a love that her
father first instilled in her through their family garden in New Market, and while she was still
able, she maintained a vegetable garden and flowerbed that witnessed countless captivating
blooms over the years; and
WHEREAS, after graduating from high school in 1940, she moved to Knoxville, where
she found work at Miller's Department Store, and forty years later, she retired from Sears,

Roebuck, and Company, having been always beloved by her coworkers for her attention to
detail and solicitude for her customers; and
WHEREAS, with an indomitable industriousness, Mrs. Goins also served as an Avon
representative during her work career and in her retirement; and
WHEREAS, she was married to her beloved husband, Thomas Lace Goins, for sixty-four
years, a union blessed with three children, and she enjoyed a wealth of grandchildren and
great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, a woman of deep and abiding faith, Mrs. Goins was an eighty-year member
of Clinton Chapel AME Zion Church, where she was the oldest member of the congregation; in
addition to the daily bread of her religious television programming, she furthered her already
intimate relationship with the Lord through generous service to Clinton Chapel, including serving
as Director of Christian Education, a Sunday School teacher, and president of the Helen
Spurgeon Circle Missionary Society; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Goins enjoyed playing bridge with the Saturday Night Cotillion Bridge
Club, and she was also a consistent community servant, whether through helping with early
voting or raising funds for service projects such as the Heart Fund; her other interests included
watching her favorite game shows and listening to religious music and audio books; and
WHEREAS, a blessing to all who had the good fortune of knowing her, Mrs. Goins
attributed her longevity to reading and studying the Bible, attending Sunday School and church,
and following the principles outlined in Matthew 7:12 and James 1:5-6; and
WHEREAS, Mary Goins leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should pause to remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,

FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Mary Elizabeth Goins, reflecting fondly upon her
impeccable character as a public servant and human being and commending her stalwart
commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
