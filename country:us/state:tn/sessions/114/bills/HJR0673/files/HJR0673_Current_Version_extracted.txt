HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 673
By Barrett
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Police Chief Shanon
Dale Irwin of Centerville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the untimely passing of Police Chief Shanon Dale Irwin of Centerville; and
WHEREAS, Chief Irwin was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Davidson County on April 4, 1974, Shanon Dale Irwin learned the
values of hard work, integrity, and dedication to service that defined his life at an early age; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of Hickman County High School, Chief Irwin joined the United
States Marine Corps after high school and later joined the National Guard, serving with
distinction during his military career; and
WHEREAS, dedicated to serving and protecting his community, Chief Shanon Irwin
rendered sterling service as the Chief of Police for the Town of Centerville, where he earned the
respect and admiration of elected officials, the citizens of Centerville, and his fellow law
enforcement officers; and
WHEREAS, Chief Irwin was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; he enjoyed camping on the Tennessee River
with his family and loved the outdoors, good food, and Georgia football; and
WHEREAS, Chief Irwin enjoyed the loving companionship of his wife of thirty years,
Sydney Irwin, and was the proud father of Cole Irwin, Wyatt Irwin, and Hannah Irwin; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his mother, Deborah
(Ted) Graves; his father, Elmer (Angelina) Irwin; his sister, Shelly (Eric) Blyberg; mother- and
father-in-law, Randel and Brenda Totty; brother-in-law, Ty (Shanee) Totty; sister-in-law, Ginger
(Nick) Dearman; nieces and nephews, Alexis Blyberg, Trenton Blyberg, Ethan Blyberg, Mika

Totty, Selena Totty, Savannah Dearman, Addie Dearman, and Nate Dearman; aunts, Sandy
(Shannon) Buck and Glenda Corkin; uncle, Dale Gordon; and a host of extended family and
dear friends; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his grandmother, Bernice Gordon; and
WHEREAS, Chief Irwin leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Police Chief Shanon Dale Irwin, reflecting fondly
upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with
courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Chief Irwin.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
