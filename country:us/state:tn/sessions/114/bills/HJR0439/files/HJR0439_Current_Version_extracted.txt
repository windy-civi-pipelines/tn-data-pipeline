HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 439
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor and recognize Jayden Katherine
Franks, 2025 Top Ten Senior at Wayne County
High School.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to pay
tribute to those students who, through their natural talents and unflagging commitment to
academic excellence, have distinguished themselves in the classroom; and
WHEREAS, Jayden Katherine Franks is one such exceptional student, who was recently
named a Top Ten Senior of the 2025 graduating class at Wayne County High School; and
WHEREAS, through diligent study, steady composure during examinations, and
steadfast commitment of time and talent, Ms. Franks has achieved outstanding academic
success and joined the ranks of the best and brightest students in Tennessee and our country;
and
WHEREAS, in her remarkable high school career, Jayden Franks has earned an
exemplary grade point average of 98.643 while participating in numerous extracurricular
activities, including the soccer team, Beta Club, Math Club, and Interact; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Franks's display of intellectual aptitude and ambition surely bodes well
for her future success at the University of North Alabama, where she plans to pursue a degree
in psychology; and
WHEREAS, Jayden Franks's noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride
for her parents, Shannon and Beverly Franks, her school, and her community; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Franks's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of the respect,
admiration, and commendation of this General Assembly; now, therefore,
HJR0439
004824
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend and honor Jayden Katherine Franks of Wayne
County High School on being named a 2025 Top Ten Senior, salute her impressive academic
achievement, and extend to her our best wishes for a happy, successful future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
