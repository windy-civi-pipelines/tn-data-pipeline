SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 159
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor Thomas E. Mooty upon receiving the
Cocke County Citizenship Award.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to improving the
quality of life for their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Thomas E. Mooty is being honored with the Cocke County Citizenship
Award in recognition of his ongoing commitment to volunteerism, philanthropy, and community
involvement; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Mooty has served with distinction as the pastor of West End
Baptist Church in Newport for a combined total of approximately thirty-five years, and his record
of public service, both in the past and the present, is impressive; and
WHEREAS, graduating with honors from Cocke County High School and lettering in
band and football, Mr. Mooty further distinguished himself with honors at Tennessee Temple
College when he graduated in 1976; and
WHEREAS, in an early token of his driven spirit, Mr. Mooty established his own
lawncare business when he was fourteen years old, and he worked in various employments,
including the City Park Pool and the Stokley-Van Camp Power House, until he decided to enlist
in the U.S. Air Force, serving from 1961 to 1965 and exhibiting his willingness to lay down his
life and serve his country in the ultimate capacity; and
WHEREAS, upon returning from his tour of duty, Mr. Mooty briefly continued his work in
the utilities industry before hearing the call to ministry; after working part-time as pastor at West
End Baptist Church, he gave himself fully to the endeavor when he became senior pastor in
1978, a position he filled with grace and steadfast compassion for nine years; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Mooty moved to become the very senior pastor of the First Baptist
Church of Wartburg, and in his seventeen-year tenure, his abiding love and wisdom served well
the people of God gathered there; and
WHEREAS, returning to his first pasture in 2004, Mr. Mooty is once more the senior
pastor of West End Baptist Church, bringing his profound biblical knowledge and pastoral
insight to his congregation and community; and
WHEREAS, deftly using multimedia to the service of the kingdom, Mr. Mooty has
appeared on various radio and television programs over the years; these programs have been
instrumental in his evangelical efforts and have exposed a wider audience to his powerful
preaching and teaching; and
WHEREAS, even apart from his estimable role as a shepherd of souls, Mr. Mooty is a
fixture of his community; for thirteen total years, he was the press box announcer for the
Newport City Little League and Cocke County High School football and basketball, lending his
steady voice to the cheers and jeers of local sports, and he helped to install, repair, and operate
the stage lighting for performances at Cocke County High School; and
WHEREAS, his many accomplishments aside, he is fortunate to have shared a blissful
forty-five years with his beloved wife, Anita Hollingsworth, before her passing in 2016; their
fruitful marriage blessed the world with three children, Ed Mooty, Jolynn (Cal) Doty, and Lisa
(Larry) Ottinger; six grandchildren, Sean Mooty, Caitlin Mooty, Charlee Doty, Savanah Doty,
Gracie Ottinger, and Jack Ottinger; and one great-grandson, Crew Thomas Ottinger; and
WHEREAS, Thomas E. Mooty is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public
service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be
recognized on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
- 2 - 003528
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Thomas E. Mooty upon receiving the
Cocke County Citizenship Award and salute his unswerving commitment to living the examined
life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
