SENATE RESOLUTION 3
By Bailey
A RESOLUTION to commend Robert Glenn Officer on his election
as the Most Excellent Great Chief of the Grand
Council of Knight Masons of the United States.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this body should specially recognize those
estimable citizens who have distinguished themselves in their communities, their vocations, and
their organizations, putting their energies at the service of others; and
WHEREAS, Robert Glenn Officer of Sparta is one such exemplary person, who has
been elected by his peers to serve as Most Excellent Great Chief of the Grand Council of Knight
Masons of the United States of America in recognition of his laudable service; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Officer has been a member of the masonic fraternity for thirty-one
years, having been raised in 1993 to Master Mason in Sparta Lodge No. 99, Free and Accepted
Masons, where he still acts as chaplain; and
WHEREAS, recognized in many organizations for his outstanding qualities and facility
for leadership, Mr. Officer was exalted in Mt. Olivet Chapter No. 87, Royal Arch Masons and is a
Past High Priest, currently serving as treasurer; was greeted in Sparta Council No. 128, Cryptic
Masons and is a Past Illustrious Master, currently serving as treasurer; was knighted in
Cumberland Commandery No. 26 in Cookeville and is a Past Commander; and served the
Tennessee Grand Commandery as Grand Sentinel; and
WHEREAS, in the Tennessee Grand Chapter, Mr. Officer served in several offices
before being elected as the Most Excellent Grand High Priest in 2014, and he authored the
private ceremony for the incoming Grand High Priest; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Officer is a member of numerous other masonic bodies: the
Chattanooga Valley Scottish Rite, holding the title of 33° Inspector General Honorary; the Order

of Free Gardeners; Tennessee Forest No. 195, Tall Cedars of Lebanon; Cherokee York Rite
College; St. Gregory Conclave of the Red Cross of Constantine; Tennessee College Masonic
Societas Rosicruciana in Civitatibus Fœderatis; Allied Masonic Degrees; Masonic Order of
Athelstan; Masonic Order of Pilgrim Preceptor; St. Thomas of Acon; and the Worshipful Society
of Free Masons, Rough Masons, Wallers, Slaters, Paviors, Plasters and Bricklayers; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Officer is also a prominent member of the Knights of Pythias, where he
is a past Grand Chancellor of Tennessee, holds the title of Knight of the Golden Spur, and
currently serves as the Grand Treasurer for the Grand Lodge of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Officer is no stranger to national service, and he is the past Grand
Prelate of the Sovereign Order of Knight Preceptors; and
WHEREAS, utilizing his influence to effect positive change, he was the co-founder of the
Autism Assistance and Aid program and the Samuel Plemons Challenge for Autism; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his participation in fraternal organizations, Mr. Officer has
served his community as a school counselor for his alma mater White County High School and
as an adjunct faculty member at Motlow State Community College, where he teaches
psychology; and
WHEREAS, learned and experienced, Mr. Officer received his Bachelor of Science
degree in Psychology, his Master of Arts in Educational Psychology, and his Education
Specialist degree in Educational Psychology and Counselor Education from Tennessee
Technological University; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Officer has also employed his leadership skills to the betterment of his
neighbors as a member of the Sparta Board of Mayor and Aldermen since 1998, and he is
currently serving as Vice-Mayor; and
WHEREAS, he is the Chairman of the Sparta Higher Education Advisory Board and the
College Advisory Partnership, as well as other community boards; and

WHEREAS, thoughtful of this world and the next, Mr. Officer is a member of Sparta First
Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Officer has continued his family’s commitment to public service and
education, and his efforts at community enrichment are admirable; and
WHEREAS, Robert Glenn Officer is a model citizen and community servant; his
commitment to his family, community, and fraternity epitomizes the spirit of the Volunteer State
and thus, should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor Robert Glenn Officer
for his outstanding community leadership, congratulate him on being named Most Excellent
Great Chief of the Grand Council of Knight Masons of the United States of America, and extend
to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
