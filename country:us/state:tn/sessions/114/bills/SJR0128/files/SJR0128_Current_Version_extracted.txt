SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 128
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Diana Cecilia Harman
Brewer of Jefferson City.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Diana Cecilia Harman Brewer of Jefferson City; and
WHEREAS, Diana Brewer was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on February 2, 1943, in Yardley Hastings, Northampton, England, to
Cecilia R. Harman and Richard H. R. Harman, Ms. Brewer distinguished herself at a young age
with her athletic ability, particularly in basketball, track, long jump, and high jump; after
graduating from school, she went on to work in the administrative office of the United Counties
bus service; and
WHEREAS, she immigrated to America to care for her terminally ill aunt, and utilizing the
myriad skills she learned thereby, Ms. Brewer later served residents at Lifecare Center and
Chandler House Assisted Living, both of Jefferson City, for nineteen years, always manifesting
the abundant charity and kindness that was so characteristic of her person; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Brewer was an artist of prodigious quality, known particularly for her
lovely needlework, delicate drawings, and arresting paintings; and
WHEREAS, she was also deeply devoted to her family, and she always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Brewer will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by her daughters,
Odette (Bill) Shults and Richenda (Scott) Maples; her grandchildren, Nicholas (Rachel) Morgan-
Maples and Katy (Darren) Maples-Posey; her great-grandchildren, Caleb Morgan-Maples, Emily

Morgan-Maples, Hadleigh Morgan-Maples, and Aspen Posey; her sister-in-law, Joy Roberts;
her nephew, Andrew (Sam) Roberts; her niece, Kathryn Roberts; and many beloved cousins;
and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents, she was preceded in death by her brother, John
Roberts; and
WHEREAS, Diana Brewer leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Diana Cecilia Harman
Brewer, reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living
the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Ms. Brewer.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
