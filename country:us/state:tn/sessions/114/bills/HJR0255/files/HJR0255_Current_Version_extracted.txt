HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 255
By Powers
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Rhonda Longmire upon
her receipt of the 2025 Great Smoky Mountain
Council Good Scout Award.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Rhonda Longmire, who is being honored
with the 2025 Good Scout Award by the Great Smoky Mountain Council of the Boy Scouts of
America for her outstanding leadership in Campbell County and support and promotion of
Scouting; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Longmire will receive this prestigious award at the Campbell County
Good Scout Award Dinner on March 13, 2025, at the Ball Farm Event Center in LaFollette; and
WHEREAS, a 1988 graduate of Roane State Community College, Rhonda Longmire
received a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration and Accounting from the
University of Tennessee in 1992 and has been a Certified Public Accountant since 1995; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Longmire began her estimable career in banking in 1975 as a teller at
First National Bank of LaFollette, where she was later promoted to vice president and led the
bank's lending and marketing functions; she left First National Bank in 1996 to become senior
vice president and chief financial officer of First State Bank and later First Volunteer Bank, and
in 2005, she moved to First National Bank of Oneida and served as the president and chief
executive officer; and

WHEREAS, in 2013, she returned home to Community Trust Bank (formerly First
National Bank of LaFollette) as the market senior lender and in 2016 became president of the
LaFollette market; and
WHEREAS, Rhonda Longmire is a graduate of multiple banking schools and has a long
history of civic involvement; her volunteer activities include serving as a two-time chair of the
Campbell County Chamber of Commerce, two-time chair of the Roane State Foundation, and
member of the Foundation Board for over twenty years; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to accolades, she was chosen as a Roane State Community
College Outstanding Alumni in 2005; Mrs. Longmire is currently a board member of the Joint
Economic Development Board for Campbell County, a board member of the Campbell County
Industrial Development Board, and a member of the Campbell County Rotary Club; and
WHEREAS, her many accomplishments aside, Rhonda Longmire is most grateful for the
love and companionship she shares with her husband of fifty-two years, Jesse Longmire, and
their two children, Brooke and Brandon; she is also the loving grandmother of three grandsons,
Eli Dorton, Ethan Dorton, and William Dorton, and she greatly enjoys watching her grandsons
and their sports activities, especially basketball and football; and
WHEREAS, Rhonda and Jesse Longmire are longtime members of Forks Grove Baptist
Church, where they have served faithfully in many capacities over the years; and
WHEREAS, through her estimable service to Campbell County, Mrs. Longmire
epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Rhonda Longmire for
her dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Rhonda Longmire upon her receipt of the 2025

Great Smoky Mountain Council Good Scout Award, thank her for her meritorious service to
Campbell County and this State, and extend to her our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
